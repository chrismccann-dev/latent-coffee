## Language

### Brewing

**Extraction Strategy**:
The named extraction logic a brew is designed around — exactly one of six canonical values (`Suppression` / `Clarity-First` / `Balanced Intensity` / `Full Expression` / `Extraction Push` / `Hybrid`) chosen at the Coffee Brief Step 1d before any equipment or parameters are decided. **Intent-defined, not mechanics-defined**: the same mechanical recipe can name different strategies depending on what the coffee needs. Suppression and Clarity-First share coarse-grind / low-temp / low-agitation mechanics but differ in intent (holding an over-expressive coffee back vs preserving a delicate one's transparency); Full Expression and Extraction Push share fine-grind / high-temp mechanics but differ on the agitation lever (push the bed vs Melodrip-controlled turbulence). Five describe extraction *intensity* (one logic running throughout the brew); the sixth (Hybrid) describes extraction *structure* (phase boundaries where the brewer changes mode mid-brew). The primary act of recipe design — equipment and parameters fall out of it.
_Avoid_: "approach", "method", "brewing strategy" (overloaded), "recipe family", bare "strategy" when context could mean anything else.

**Two-Axis Framework**:
The structural backbone of Latent brewing — every brew names exactly one position on **Axis 1 (extraction strategy)** plus zero or more **Axis 2 modifiers**. A deliberate **learning-stage compression** of the WBC five-axis brewing taxonomy (Extraction Intensity / Time Distribution / Physical System / External Control / Output Selection) into a flatter 2-axis shape that can be represented cleanly in the app: Axis 1 corresponds to WBC's Extraction Intensity axis; Axis 2 folds External Control + Output Selection + parts of Time Distribution into a single heterogeneous "modifier" slot. The compression is intentional, not aspirational — Chris isn't actively pursuing many WBC subtypes (thermal mixing, tool-assisted moves, intentional blending), and the modifier system represents those moves without committing to first-class status. WBC's Physical System axis (Structural Systems family — blend-engineering, layered beds, dual-chamber extraction) is explicitly **out of scope** for Latent's single-origin home brewing context. Several cross-cutting WBC moves (water strength / agitation taper / filter behavior / pre-brew conditioning / time-distribution templates beyond Role-Based Pulse) live as **doc-layer reference patterns** in [docs/skills/wbc-brewing-archivist/cluster/wbc-reference.md](docs/skills/wbc-brewing-archivist/cluster/wbc-reference.md), deliberately NOT promoted to canonical modifiers. **Disposition**: the framework is expected to evolve — a modifier can be **promoted** to a strategy when usage volume + cross-coffee pattern justify it (v8.4 Hybrid promotion is the canonical example), and the 2-axis shape itself may expand to 3 or 5 axes in the future as Chris's brewing scope broadens.
_Avoid_: "the framework", "the 6+4 framework" (compact but loses the compression insight), "strategy + modifier system", "the Latent brewing taxonomy".

**Modifier** (Axis 2):
An optional, stackable layered technique applied to a brew on a different mechanical axis than the chosen extraction strategy. Default empty (most brews carry none — modifiers are deliberate additions, not defaults). Five canonical types (`lib/extraction-modifiers.ts`, write-side source of truth): `output_selection`, `thermal_staging` (renamed from `inverted_temperature_staging` in Sub-sprint 4c — alias-safe, covers kettle stance + active ramps), `aroma_capture`, `role_based_pulse`, `equipment` (persistent/timed gear — Melodrip / booster / Paragon ball — with free-text scope). Heterogeneous bucket — folds 4 of WBC's 5 control axes (Time Distribution / Physical System / External Control / Output Selection) into one slot rather than giving each its own axis. The modifier system plays two roles: (1) **representation** of WBC moves Chris isn't actively pursuing, so the brew vocabulary can name them without committing to first-class status, (2) **staging ground** for techniques that may later be **promoted** to strategies. Justification required at Step 1d, even when the answer is "none" — null modifier results are signal.
_Avoid_: "Axis 2 strategy" (modifiers are not strategies), "extraction modifier" (too narrow — modifiers operate on multiple non-extraction axes), "add-on", "technique".

**Strategy promotion**:
The deliberate elevation of an Axis 2 modifier (or unrepresented WBC subtype) into an Axis 1 extraction strategy when sustained usage and cross-coffee pattern justify dedicating it a first-class slot. Judgment-based but consistent criteria: (1) repeated active use across multiple coffees, (2) the modifier is increasingly load-bearing in recipe design rather than incidental, (3) a coherent strategy intent has emerged that's distinct from the existing strategies. Canonical example: v8.4 (2026-05-06) promoted the **Immersion modifier → Hybrid strategy**, triggered by Chris's heavy SWORKS Bottomless Dripper use at the office (an immersion-with-valve system) plus selective Hario Switch use at home reaching threshold. Promotion churns existing brew records — three lots previously archived as "Balanced Intensity + Immersion modifier" reclassified to "Hybrid (Sequential)" at promotion time — so the bar is deliberately high; the modifier system carries undeveloped techniques cheaply until usage justifies the schema cost. Distinct from the roasting-side variable → lever promotion (different domain, different mechanism — same word reused).
_Avoid_: "modifier upgrade", "axis promotion", bare "promotion" without qualifier (could mean variable → lever — that's roasting-side).

**Phase**:
A discrete segment of a brew separated from adjacent segments by a **phase boundary** — the moment when the brewer's mode, valve state, agitation, or temperature changes. Typical phases include the **bloom**, each numbered **pour**, the **drawdown**, and (for Hybrid brews) the **immersion** and **percolation** legs. The unit on which Phase-Mapped Hybrid, Temperature-Staged Hybrid, Intensity-Clarity Split, and Role-Based Pulse all operate. **Two parallel labels apply to every phase** (formal split, locked 2026-05-15): a **mechanical role** (what the brewer is doing — which WBC foundational axis is being engaged) plus a **cup-side target** (which sensory attribute the phase is meant to deliver). The two labels are orthogonal — recipe-design picks both — and were collapsed into "sensory target" phrasing in BREWING.md (`saturation / body / clarity / finish`) in a way that lost the design logic.
_Avoid_ (as a loose synonym for *phase*): "stage" (used loosely in BREWING.md; phase is the precise term), "pour" (a pour is one mechanical kind of event a phase can contain — bloom and drawdown are also phases; **Pour** (below) is the precise term for the *stored mechanical event*, distinct from this design-time umbrella), "step" (a code-only artifact in the `PourStep` TS type name — not lived vocabulary; the operator says "pour"), "segment". **A phase can be expansive** — grouping several mechanical pours under one design-intent label (Intensity-Clarity Split: bloom + first pour together = the "intensity phase"); it is not 1:1 with a single pour.

**Phase boundary**:
The moment within a brew when one phase ends and another begins, marked by a deliberate change in the brewer's mode (immersion ↔ percolation), valve state (closed / restricted / opened), agitation (push the bed ↔ Melodrip), temperature, or kettle position. **The defining structural feature of Hybrid strategy** — Hybrid is exactly the case where phase boundaries exist by design and change the brew's extraction logic mid-stream; the 5 intensity strategies have implicit phase boundaries (each pour ends and the next begins) but they don't change extraction logic, just continue it.
_Avoid_: "transition", "stage break", "mode change" (mode change is one *kind* of phase boundary; phase boundary is the umbrella).

**Pour**:
The mechanical liquid-addition event within a brew — how many there are, when each starts and stops, how much water goes in. The **granular execution unit**, distinct from the design-time **Phase** (which can *group* several pours — in an Intensity-Clarity Split the bloom + first pour together are the "intensity phase"). The **bloom** is recorded as the first pour-object (`type: 'bloom'`, always index 0) even though conceptually it is its own thing. Stored structured in `brews.pours[]` (migration 074, the data-model session 2026-05-30) as flat objects `{type: 'bloom'|'pour', at (m:ss, required), to_g? (cumulative grams), pour_s?, hold_s?, valve?, detail?}` — extensive MCP / claude.ai work records this coherently per session. **Only bloom + pours are stored as pour-objects** — drawdown / immersion / percolation are Phases but NOT stored pours (drawdown is derived for the recipe footer; immersion/percolation valve moves live as prose). **Mid-pour changes stay inside one pour**: a Hario Switch going open→closed partway through, or a valve dialed up as the bed drops below half, are captured in that pour's `valve` / `detail` fields — they do not split it into two pours. NULL `pours` = legacy row (the free-text `pour_structure` parser is the read-fallback).
_Naming_: the operator term is "**pour**" / "mechanical pour"; Chris does not use "step." The TS type `PourStep` carries "step" purely as a code artifact — "step" is **not** lived vocabulary and is avoided in design language (see Phase _Avoid_ above). The column name `brews.pours[]` matches the lived word.
_Avoid_: "step" (code-only, not spoken), "phase" (a pour is the mechanical event; a phase is the broader design-time grouping that can span multiple pours), "pour structure" (that's the legacy free-text column `pour_structure`, superseded by structured `pours[]`).

**Mechanical role** (per phase):
What the brewer is mechanically doing during a given phase — which WBC foundational axis is being engaged on that phase. Open-ended vocabulary tied to the 5 WBC axes: extraction-intensity moves (grind / ratio / temp shifts), time-distribution moves (pulse / hold / drain), physical-system moves (valve restriction / immersion / percolation mode), external-control moves (Melodrip agitation taper / Paragon chilling ball / kettle off-base). The **design-time label** picked during recipe construction. Distinct from the cup-side target (what the phase is supposed to *deliver* in the cup), though the two are paired during Phase-Mapped Hybrid design.
_Avoid_: "phase mechanic" (clunky), "what the brewer does" (too informal), the BREWING.md shorthand `saturation / body / clarity / finish` (mixed-vocabulary; retired in favor of this formal split).

**Cup-side target** (per phase):
What sensory attribute a given phase is meant to extract / deliver in the final cup. Used during recipe design (Phase-Mapped Hybrid, Role-Based Pulse) and during iteration (Step 3 retro — "the finish pour didn't deliver clean finish, adjust"). **Canonical attribute set is the fixed 5**: `aroma / attack / mid-palate / body / finish` — the same vocabulary used by Step 3 tasting and the Step 4 resolved-brew output, locked 2026-05-15 for design / iteration / archive consistency. The 5-attribute set is deliberately general — coffees vary heavily by profile (acidity-bent / sweetness-bent / funky / nutty-chocolatey / etc.) and a narrower vocabulary like `acidity / sweetness / clarity` would fail to describe many of them. **Phase → attribute mapping is soft, not 1:1**: typical pairings (bloom → aroma, last pour → finish) are design-time aspirations and "generally what you're targeting" rather than guarantees, and recipes often have fewer or more phases than 5 (single-pour, 2-pour, 3-pour, 4-pour all common; bloom and double-bloom variants are future-scope). The two-label formal split keeps cup-side targets strictly sensory; mechanical roles carry the "what you do" vocabulary.
_Avoid_: "sensory target" (BREWING.md's prior framing — fine as a category but ambiguous in practice because the doc's actual values mix sensory and mechanical), "phase outcome", "cup attribute" (too generic).

**Coffee Brief**:
The design-time framing of a brew, authored by **claude.ai** at the start of every brewing session before any recipe parameters are touched — Latent's input-side is MCP-only, and the Coffee Brief is Step 1 of that input workflow. Four sub-steps run in sequence: **(1a)** web search for the roaster's published brew guide, **(1b)** evaluate process + variety risk flags against the Process/Variety Signal table, **(1c)** write a 3-5 sentence brief synthesizing terroir / cultivar / process / roaster intent (cross-referencing prior Latent archive on related coffees), **(1d)** propose an extraction strategy + zero-or-more modifiers + named considerations (Cooling-Curve Design, WBC corpus check), with explicit justification + risk-if-wrong notes — then pause for Chris's "yep go for it" confirmation before producing the Step 2 recipe. **Operationally analogous to Claude Code's plan mode** — claude.ai plans, Chris confirms, then execution begins. Chris confirms ~99% of the time without changes, by design: the system is built to internalize judgment, not require manual override at runtime.
_Avoid_: "Step 1" (under-specified outside the document), bare "brief" (overloaded — could mean any prose summary), "coffee brief" lowercase (canonical with capitals as a workflow stage name), "design pass".

**Reference brew**:
The recipe Chris commits to for a coffee at the end of the brewing iteration loop — the terminal output of Step 3, recorded via the Step 4 resolved-brew artifact. Recognized (not designated via control experiment) when iteration deltas flatten and the cup matches the targeted profile. **Default term for purchased roasted beans** — for self-roasted reference roasts the same workflow output is called the **optimized brew** to emphasize that it pushes past the constrained xBloom reference cup baseline. Both names describe the same artifact shape and the same iteration-loop output; the distinction is the calibration-vs-consumption framing on the self-roasted side. Subjective by nature: coffee in cup form is taste-driven, no objective best, and the same coffee can land beautifully across multiple extraction strategies — "reference" reflects the calibration role this recipe plays for this coffee, not a claim that it's the only valid expression.
_Avoid_: "best brew", "final brew", "winning brew", "the recipe" (under-specified), "optimized brew" when context is specifically a purchased bean (use reference brew — optimized brew is reserved for the self-roasted handoff to emphasize the calibration-to-consumption pushdown).

**Signature method**:
A proper-name proprietary process — a producer- or facility-specific processing signature that's both **(1) branded under a proper-noun name** and **(2) mechanically opaque** (the producer markets it under the brand but doesn't fully disclose the underlying recipe). The two criteria together are what distinguish signatures from the structural process modifier axes (fermentation / drying / intervention / experimental): if the mechanical recipe is fully decomposable into canonical axes, the move lives in those axes — even when it's elaborate. The signature method axis exists precisely for the *black-box-with-a-brand* case. Persisted on `brews.signature_method` + `green_beans.signature_method` as a single canonical value; null when the process doesn't carry a proprietary signature. **Hint, not auto-fill**: when a signature is selected on a brew, the picker surfaces a hint like "Moonshadow is typically Washed at Sebastian Ramirez" but does NOT auto-fill `base_process` — rare variants (Moonshadow Washed vs Moonshadow Honey) are legitimate. The signature names *who and what they call it*; the structural fields name *the approximated mechanical recipe* (best-effort interpretation, not producer-disclosed).

**Canonical working list (Chris's, 2026-05-15): 14 signatures** — Moonshadow (Dark Room Dried + Extended Drying), TyOxidator (Aerobic Phase + Anaerobic Fermentation), Alchemy (Carbonic Maceration + Yeast Inoculated), TIM (Yeast Inoculated + Mossto), XO (Extended Anaerobic + Dynamic Cherry — CGLE Mandela XO is the reference example), Enzyflow (Enzyme Assisted + Aerobic Phase), Bio-innovation (Anaerobic + Biological Fermentation), Sous-vide (Enzyme Assisted + Thermal Control), Amazake (Koji + Yeast Inoculated), Anti-maceration (Anaerobic vacuum + Ozone + Zeolite Drying), Dynamic cherry (Anaerobic + Dynamic Cherry), Dry fermentation (Mossto + Cold Room + Natural), Splash (Anaerobic + Lactic Fermentation), Symbiotic (Mossto cross-varietal). **Wave Hybrid** (Finca Soledad / Pepe Jijón) is a 15th candidate flagged for addition. **The Latent app registry currently encodes only 3** (Moonshadow / TyOxidator / Hybrid Washed) — significant gap flagged for sprint. **Hybrid Washed** appears in the current Latent registry but NOT in Chris's working canonical list — likely deprecated; verify before any registry edit.

Promotion / addition discipline: low ceremony — Chris adds signatures manually as he encounters them. No `propose_canonical_addition` queue planned for this axis specifically because the rate of new-signature discovery is low.
_Avoid_: "proprietary process" (descriptive but under-specified — signature is canonical), "branded process" (close but the canonical noun is signature), "named process" (overloaded — all canonical processes have names; signature is the *proper-noun-branded-and-opaque* sub-case), "producer signature" (different — that's `ProducerEntry.referenceRole` Anchor/Signal/Experimental).

**Process-Dominant**:
A boolean flag on the resolved brew row (`process_dominant`) that records whether the cup's flavor was driven primarily by **processing** rather than by terroir, cultivar, or roaster house style. **An aggregation trust signal**, not a Step 1d signal-arbitration reflection: `true` = this brew's learnings are *process-specific* and should NOT be aggregated into the cultivar / terroir / roaster pages' compounding-knowledge surfaces; `false` = the brew's learnings apply broadly to those surfaces. Canonical example: Moonwake Lord Voldemort (yeast-inoculated watermelon-fermented Ombligon, Colombia) — the process is so heavy that lessons from it tell you nothing about Ombligon-the-variety, Colombian terroir, or even much about anaerobic-washed processes broadly; they tell you about *that specific yeast-inoculated watermelon-ferment signature* and nothing else.

**Tight in practice with Step 1d process-override, distinct conceptually**: when the process signal overrode at Step 1d (intervention-heavy process won the strategy-selection contest), the brew is *usually* also process-dominant at Step 4. But conceptually the two surfaces are different — Step 1d signal override is a *design-time judgment* about which signal wins for strategy selection, `process_dominant` is a *post-hoc aggregation hint* about whether to trust the brew's learnings to generalize beyond its process. Both happen to fire on the same heavy-process coffees in practice.

**Origin + current status**: predates the Latent app — emerged in the claude.ai-local-app precursor that became Latent, when Chris's compounding-knowledge sheet was small enough that one Lord-Voldemort-tier brew could skew cultivar / terroir aggregations. With today's larger corpus, the signal may be becoming less load-bearing; flagged for review.
_Avoid_: "process-driven" (informal — used in BREWING.md prose but `process_dominant` is the canonical field name), "process-forward" (refers to flavor presentation, not aggregation logic), "process flag" (under-specified), "process-specific" (close, but `process_dominant` is the canonical Boolean).

**Named Consideration**:
A Coffee Brief Step 1d gate item evaluated **outside the Axis 1 / Axis 2 canonical strategy + modifier taxonomy** — a categorical cross-check whose default answer is null and whose discipline is in being asked the question regardless. Originates at [BREWING.md § Step 1 — Coffee Brief](docs/skills/brewing-assistant/cluster/operational-guide.md) line 196 as a literal `**Named considerations (v8.4 - state explicitly even if the answer is the default):**` section heading inside the Step 1d output. Distinct from modifier check (which is also default-null but draws from a 4-value canonical Axis 2 registry); Named Considerations are orthogonal questions that fall outside the strategy/modifier axis structure, layered on top of it. **Two canonical instances today** (BREWING.md, v8.4 + v8.5 additions): **Cooling-Curve Design** (peak evaluation window declaration — persists as `cooling_curve_target` on the resolved brew) + **WBC corpus check** (competitor-archive cross-reference + cross-cutting calibration axis check — persists as `strategy_notes` when relevant). The "state explicitly even if the answer is the default" discipline matches the same property on modifier check, but the structural position differs: modifier check picks from a canonical taxonomy axis, Named Considerations are heterogeneous cross-checks that don't belong to any single axis. **Promotion path** mirrors the modifier → strategy promotion path: a Named Consideration earns first-class status when sustained Coffee Brief experience shows the question always-needs-asking-explicitly; demotion to "inline check" (or absorption into modifier / strategy) happens if it stops earning a dedicated gate slot.

**Authoring asymmetry**: the umbrella phrasing is **authoritative in BREWING.md** as a procedural section heading (claude.ai is instructed to "state explicitly"), but **does not appear in claude.ai's lived Coffee Brief output prose** — instances are written inline ("Cooling-Curve Target flagged: peak evaluation 45-50°C") without the umbrella label above them. The umbrella's function is the discipline of evaluation, not the output shape; the named instances carry the output.
_Avoid_: "Step 1d cross-check" (close, but informal and doesn't capture the deliberate-null-answer discipline), "Step 1d gate item" (under-specified — modifier check is also a gate item), "design consideration" (too generic), bare "consideration" (overloaded — too many things are "considerations" in BREWING.md prose), "named check" (informal).

**Cooling-Curve Target** (a.k.a. **Cooling-Curve Design** at Step 1d):
A WBC-derived Thermal Systems concept — *"design the brew, service, or wait time around the temperature window where the cup peaks"* — evaluated as a Named Consideration at Coffee Brief Step 1d and persisted on the resolved brew row as free-text `cooling_curve_target` (e.g. `"40-45°C peak"`, `"evaluate below 50°C"`). **Cooling-Curve Design** = the design-time question framing at Step 1d ("is the peak evaluation window IS the strategy for this coffee?"); **Cooling-Curve Target** = the declared answer recorded at Step 4. Two phrasings, one concept, paralleling the question → answer lifecycle. **Governs perception and service timing, not only brewing temperature** — applies at three moments: (a) brewing-time temp choice when peak-window is load-bearing, (b) evaluation-time discipline ("don't iterate before the cup cools to 45°C"), (c) service-time temp management ("rapid-cool with a PC fan to ~55°C before serving so guests taste the target end notes"). Only (a) and (b) intersect the Latent data model — service-time discipline is a Chris-side practice that doesn't currently get logged. Most brews omit/null because Chris's empirical default drinking window is 50-55°C; the field fires when a coffee's peak window diverges from that default (El Paraíso thermal-shock washed, Garrido Mokka/Mokkita naturals, anaerobic naturals, anoxic naturals, Picolot competition lots with S7X cooling signature).

Historical origin: the field name predates the WBC framing — emerged from Chris's early-Latent precision-tasting habit (handheld IR thermometer gun, recording per-coffee taste at 60°C / 55°C / 50°C / 45°C and declaring the optimal window). Years of precision-tasting collapsed into the 50-55°C default; the field today is the divergence signal, not the universal record.
_Avoid_: bare "cooling curve" (under-specified — could mean kettle decline, cup cooling arc, or this field), "evaluation window" (close, but Cooling-Curve Target is the declaration of that window, not the window itself), "peak temp" (informal — not canonical), "service temp" (third dimension of the broader concept but not the same as the brew-row field).

**WBC corpus check**:
A Coffee Brief Step 1d **Named Consideration** (v8.5 addition, BREWING.md line 200) — a deliberate cross-reference of the brew's profile against the **154-recipe WBC competitor archive** ([docs/skills/wbc-brewing-archivist/cluster/wbc-recipes.md](docs/skills/wbc-brewing-archivist/cluster/wbc-recipes.md)) and the **Cross-cutting control patterns** map ([docs/skills/wbc-brewing-archivist/cluster/wbc-reference.md § Cross-Cutting Control Patterns](docs/skills/wbc-brewing-archivist/cluster/wbc-reference.md)). **Composite instance**: BREWING.md bundles two structurally independent cross-checks under one bullet because they share the same workflow moment (pulling WBC reference materials when reaching for a non-default move): the **corpus pattern match** asks "does this coffee profile match a 2024–2025 finalist's recipe shape we haven't tried?" (substrate: the WBC corpus is the canonical source of "experiment Chris wouldn't think of"); the **cross-cutting axis check** asks "is a calibration axis other than strategy/modifier doing the load-bearing work?" (water strength / agitation taper / filter behavior / pre-brew conditioning may be the real lever rather than the strategy/modifier choice — see [Cross-cutting control patterns](docs/skills/wbc-brewing-archivist/cluster/wbc-reference.md) for the 5-area map). The two halves are independent first-class concepts; combining them into one BREWING.md bullet is a workflow-moment convenience, not a structural claim of equivalence.

Default: null on both halves — most brews don't match a corpus recipe directly and don't lean on a non-canonical calibration axis. Flag explicitly when a corpus pattern is structurally close (anaerobic natural with floral target → Vannelli Aroma Capture; clean washed Gesha pushing yield → Wölfl Extraction Push canonical; bloom-as-strategy → Ferket Selective Bloom Hybrid) or when a cross-cutting axis is the dominant move ("Clarity-First mechanically but the real move is dropping water 90ppm → 50ppm"). Persists as free-text in `strategy_notes` when relevant.

**Lived vocabulary**: claude.ai's authoring uses "WBC corpus check" as the stable umbrella for the composite; the cross-cutting half is sometimes inlined under specific calibration-axis nouns ("water strength check" / "agitation taper check") rather than the umbrella when the axis is known up front. Future grilling may split the composite into two distinct Named Considerations if the bundling proves load-bearing-confusing in practice.
_Avoid_: "WBC check" (under-specified — corpus + cross-cutting are two surfaces), "competitor archive lookup" (informal — WBC corpus is the canonical noun), bare "corpus check" (overloaded — Latent has multiple corpora — green-bean canonical-registries authoring also uses "corpus" framing), "WBC reference lookup" (close, but the lookup is bidirectional with archive recipes AND cross-cutting patterns; the noun is "check" for the cross-reference verb, not "lookup").

**Variety signal**:
A strategy bias derived from the coffee's cultivar genetics — what extraction strategy the cultivar's expressive ceiling and structural tendencies naturally fit. Transparency-driven varieties (Gesha, Sydra / Typica Mejorado, Sudan Rume, Pink Bourbon, Rosado, Laurina) → Clarity-First default; dense high-elevation varieties (Pacamara, Mokka, Mokkita) → Balanced Intensity default; broad-tolerance varieties (Bourbon, Catuai, Catimor) → context-dependent. **The default signal source** at Coffee Brief Step 1c — variety speaks first unless process or roaster override.
_Avoid_: "cultivar signal" (sometimes used informally — variety is canonical), "varietal signal", "variety bias".

**Process signal**:
A strategy bias derived from the coffee's processing — what extraction strategy the fermentation / drying interventions point toward. Read at Coffee Brief Step 1b via the Process/Variety Signal table. **Strength varies with intervention depth**: mild processes (washed, controlled DRD natural, climate-controlled NC natural) → low-strength signal, often defers to variety; intervention-heavy processes (anoxic natural, heavy co-ferment, anaerobic washed Huila/Cauca, yeast-inoculated lots) → high-strength signal, can override variety. Anaerobic naturals are a special process bucket → Suppression default. The **canonical heaviest-process-wins example: Moonwake Lord Voldemort** (Deiro Garcia Finca yeast-inoculated Ombligon, Colombia) — the process is so intervention-heavy it overrides cultivar, terroir, and even the roaster's own brew guide; the coffee becomes "its own beast."
_Avoid_: "fermentation signal" (too narrow — drying matters too), "process flag" (could mean an individual modifier), "process tag".

**Roaster signal** (a.k.a. **House style**):
A strategy bias derived from the roaster's house style and / or published brew guide — what extraction strategy this roaster targets when they serve this coffee. Encoded in [docs/brewing/roasters.md](docs/brewing/roasters.md) per roaster card; ~70 canonical roasters with strategy tags spanning Clarity-First / Balanced / Extraction-Forward / System / Varies / Self-Roasted. **House style** is the same concept framed as a roaster's *persistent extraction direction* across lots — used when the override is driven by the roaster's general bias rather than a specific brew guide. **Strong house styles point at specific strategy zones, not generic strong-vs-weak preferences**: Sey = Extraction Push-leaning ("light, very high extraction, very low agitation"); DAK = Full Expression ("you need to push it to get out what you want"); Picolot Garrido raised-bed naturals = Full Expression with fast/fast/slow valve discipline. Weak / varies-style roasters defer to variety + process; strong house-style roasters routinely override both.
_Avoid_: "roaster bias", bare "brew guide" (under-specified — could mean the document itself), "roaster intent" (close, but less precise — house style is the canonical synonym).

**Signal override**:
The arbitration outcome when two or more signals (variety / process / roaster) point at different strategies and one wins. **Judgment-based weighting at Coffee Brief Step 1c, not strict precedence**: each signal carries weight that depends on its specificity and reliability for the coffee at hand. Three reliable override patterns: **(1) Process > Variety** for intervention-heavy processes (anoxic natural overrides Pink Bourbon's transparency ceiling; Moonwake Lord Voldemort overrides everything); **(2) Roaster > Variety + Process** for strong-house-style roasters (DAK = Full Expression on clean Ethiopian anaerobic washed, overriding the Balanced Intensity default that variety + process would otherwise call); **(3) Variety > Process** for transparency-driven varieties with mild processes (Sydra TyOxidator confirmed Clarity-First despite the experimental process flag). When signals agree, no override is needed and the default applies cleanly. **Default = variety-driven** when no override condition fires.
_Avoid_: "signal conflict resolution", "override logic", "signal arbitration" (the umbrella concept — fine as a doc-section header but "signal override" is the operative term), bare "override" without a qualifier.

**Strategy zone**:
The region of recipe space defined by a given extraction strategy choice — the "country" in Chris's geography mental model where strategy = country, Hybrid sub-form / modifier choice = state, and iteration loop = city. Five intensity strategies + one structure strategy define six non-overlapping zones; brews live inside one zone at a time. **Iteration tightens within a zone, it does not transit between zones** — the iteration loop can dial from one city to another inside the same country, but it cannot move you from North America to Asia by changing one variable per turn. Wrong-zone strategy choices are the most expensive mistake the brewing workflow can make, because no iteration budget recovers them; the Coffee Brief's Step 1d confirmation gate exists specifically to catch this before iteration starts.
_Avoid_: "extraction zone" (close, but conflates the strategy's region with the chemistry of extraction itself), "neighborhood" (BREWING.md uses this loosely; "zone" is the precise term), "strategy region", "strategy territory".

**Wrong-zone trap**:
The failure mode in which a Coffee Brief Step 1d strategy choice lands in the wrong **Strategy zone** for the coffee, trapping the subsequent **Iteration loop** in suboptimal recipe space regardless of how many turns it runs. Defined against the property of Strategy zones being non-overlapping: iteration tightens within a zone but cannot transit between zones, so wrong-zone strategy choices are **unrecoverable by within-zone parameter adjustment** (grind / temp / agitation / ratio / pour structure). The **diagnostic signature** mid-iteration is structural wrongness rather than incremental wrongness — consistently sour, flat, hollow, or one-dimensional across multiple tweaks (per BREWING.md Step 3 iteration-loop instruction). Recovery requires re-naming the strategy (a **strategy pivot**), not adjusting parameters.

**Canonical example (N=1 by design)**: Jeferson Motta anaerobic washed Gesha. Pre-framework, iterated as Clarity-First to no avail (sour / flat / grassy); the candied strawberry / hibiscus profile only surfaced once the strategy was re-named to Full Expression. The Motta event is **historical, not recurring** — it produced the framework, and the framework's job is to keep N=1. Brews subsequently tagged "follows same pattern as Jeferson Motta" (Moonwake El Eden Tamarind Washed; the entire heavy-anaerobic-washed-Colombian-Gesha category encoded in the Process/Variety Signal Table as "Anaerobic Washed heavy → Full Expression, confirmed") are **applications of the Motta lesson, not independent wrong-zone events** — they were correctly diagnosed Full Expression at Step 1d the first time the framework saw them.

**Prevention chain** (forward-looking): Strategy-zone completeness ensures the Coffee Brief author can see every zone the coffee might want; Coffee Brief Step 1d is the confirmation gate that catches wrong-zone calls before iteration starts; the gate's inputs are prior-archive lookup + WBC corpus check + roaster brew guide cross-check + the three signals (variety / process / roaster) feeding strategy arbitration. **Detection chain** (post-hoc, when prevention fails): **Extraction Confirmed** is the reconciliation field on the resolved brew that records the strategy actually validated by iteration, signaling that the upstream-prevention layer missed something. **Causal role in registry design**: the Motta event triggered the roaster registry's promotion from a plain attribute on brews to a page-bearing rich registry with strategy tags — the trap is load-bearing for why the registry has its current shape, not only for what gets validated against it.

**Glossary-internal vocabulary**: the term is CONTEXT.md-internal by design — operational docs (BREWING.md, prompts, taxonomy markdown) describe the resolved-and-encoded form ("confirmed Full Expression," "see Jeferson Motta entry," Process/Variety Signal Table rows) without naming the trap-class. The glossary's job is to name what the operational docs operate on procedurally; "wrong-zone trap" is one of the cleanest examples of that role in the Brewing cluster.
_Avoid_: "wrong-strategy trap" (under-specified — strategy is the noun being mis-applied, but the trap is zonal not just categorical; the geography metaphor is load-bearing), "wrong-country trap" (Chris's informal geography phrasing — fine in prose but "wrong-zone" is the canonical noun that aligns with the Strategy zone headword), "Motta trap" (collapses the category into its sole canonical example — useful as eponym in prose but not as glossary lemma since the trap concept has to outlive the historical event), "iteration sink" (informal — captures the unrecoverability but loses the strategy-wrongness diagnosis), "extraction strategy mis-call" (descriptive but flat — doesn't capture the unrecoverability or the diagnostic signature).

**Extraction Confirmed**:
A reconciliation field on the resolved brew that records the **strategy actually validated by the iteration loop, only when it diverged from the strategy proposed at Coffee Brief Step 1d**. If the planned strategy matched the tasted result, the field is empty — the brew's `extraction_strategy` column already records what was confirmed, so no extra prose is needed. **Legacy field with declining relevance**: originated pre-Latent on Chris's spreadsheet as a wrong-zone gut-check after the Jeferson Motta wrong-zone trap (Clarity-First iteration on a coffee that needed Full Expression — no number of within-zone tweaks would have recovered it). Its original purpose has been **subsumed by the Coffee Brief upstream** — wrong-zone strategies now get caught at Step 1d via prior-archive lookup + WBC corpus + roaster brew guide cross-check, so the iteration loop rarely transits zones anymore. When the field does populate today, it's a signal worth taking seriously: the upstream prevention layer missed something, and the archive should carry the lesson forward.
_Avoid_: "extraction strategy" (that's the design-time field), "actual strategy" (under-specified), "confirmed strategy" (ambiguous — could mean confirmed-as-planned vs confirmed-after-divergence).

**Modifiers Confirmed**:
A reconciliation field on the resolved brew that records, in one closing line, which modifiers were validated and whether each resolved what it was meant to resolve. **State `None` explicitly when no modifiers were used** — null modifier results are signal, because they confirm the modifier check at Step 1d ran and the brew didn't need any. **Active experimental field**, distinct from the legacy Extraction Confirmed: modifiers are "add-ons / slight variations of the core recipe," WBC competitor recipes use them heavily, and Chris is actively expanding modifier experimentation rather than relying on the pre-iteration design pass to settle them. Examples: "Output Selection (late cut) confirmed — drying tail resolved" (validated as intended); "Aroma Capture confirmed bloom + Pour 1, cooling-arc artifact noted" (validated with new sub-finding); "None — Suppression alone resolved the bitter tail; ITS not needed" (null result, still recorded).
_Avoid_: "modifier outcome", "modifier validation", "applied modifiers" (under-specified — could mean planned, applied, or confirmed), "modifier null" (jargon for the `None` case).

**Resolved brew**:
The concrete Step 4 structured write-up of a reference brew (or optimized brew) — the key/value list (Coffee Identity / Origin / Process / Recipe / Roast Level / Tasting / Flavor Notes / Learnings) that gets validated against canonical registries and synced into the Latent app via the `push_brew` MCP Tool. **The artifact form of the reference brew** — one reference brew = one resolved brew row in the `brews` table. The artifact-vs-recipe distinction matters because the resolved brew is what gets archived (sync row, validated against canonicals, surfaced on `/brews/[id]`); the reference brew is the abstract recipe-concept the resolved brew represents. Latent records only the resolved brew today — intermediate iteration brews and tasting deltas live in the claude.ai thread and are lost when it rolls off (flagged ambiguity; Chris is open to reconsidering).
_Avoid_: bare "the brew" (ambiguous — physical brew vs recipe vs artifact), "Step 4 output" (under-specified), "sync row" (implementation-flavored), "brew record".

**Iteration loop** (brewing):
The dial-in cycle between Brew 1 and the locked reference brew — Step 3 of the brewing workflow. One iteration = claude.ai produces a recipe → Chris executes it physically → Chris records verbal/written tasting notes back into the claude.ai thread → claude.ai proposes adjustments → next recipe. Loop terminates at **diminishing returns** ("close enough to the end cup I'm looking for") or at a strategy pivot (if 2-3 brews suggest the original strategy is structurally wrong). Typical iteration count: **2-4 brews, ~3 average**, occasionally one-shot success (in which case Chris does one confirmatory test before locking), occasionally many turns on a difficult coffee. Bounded by physical constraints — most roasted-bean purchases are 125-500g, narrowing the iteration budget compared to roasting's ~10 V-sets per lot. **Distinct from the roasting-side V-set loop**: brewing iterations are sequential (single recipe per turn, not parallel batch slots), focused on the brewing recipe only (the bean is fixed input from the roaster), and cup-side feedback drives recipe adjustment rather than physical roast variables.

**Three named termination paths** (cluster anchor for the iteration-termination taxonomy): **Diminishing returns** (success-trajectory completion), **Strategy pivot** (mid-loop course correction when within-zone tweaks aren't moving toward target), **Wrong-zone trap** (post-hoc description of the failure mode when neither the Coffee Brief Step 1d gate nor the mid-loop pivot caught a wrong-zone strategy call). The first two are active loop terminators; the third is the failure-mode noun the first two prevent.
_Avoid_: "dialing-in", "trial-and-error", bare "iteration" (ambiguous with the roasting V-set iteration — qualify when context could mean either), "iteration cycle".

**Iteration budget**:
The consumable resource bound on the brewing **Iteration loop** — how many dial-in turns can be spent on a single coffee before exhaustion forces a stop. **Three constraints define the budget; whichever fires first is binding**:

- **Physical floor** — grams of roasted beans on hand. Typical roasted purchase is 125-500g; at 15-25g per brew this allows ~8-30 physical attempts per bag. Hard ceiling, set at purchase time.
- **Cognitive ceiling** — the turn-count at which iteration suggestions stop producing differentiated signal. Operationalized in BREWING.md Step 3: 2-3 iterations of structural wrongness (sour / flat / hollow despite tweaks) escalates to the strategy-pivot question rather than continuing within-zone adjustment. Soft ceiling around 6-8 turns total.
- **Pragmatic cap** — Chris's diminishing-returns judgment call ("close enough to the end cup I'm looking for"). Most often binding; fires at ~3 brews average per the Iteration loop typical-count.

The effective budget is the floor-of-three. In practice the pragmatic cap fires first (~3 brews), well below the physical floor (8-30) and at the low edge of the cognitive ceiling — which is why the brewing workflow has its bias toward one-shot success and tight iteration. The budget is **the resource Wrong-zone trap wastes**: the trap is unrecoverable by within-zone adjustment, so the pragmatic cap fires at the same ~3-brew mark whether the strategy was right or wrong, consuming budget without convergence.

**Asymmetric with the roasting-side V-set budget**: roasting has a parallel "~10-V-set budget per green lot" anchored to green-bean lot size (not roasted-bag size); the brewing budget is narrower (single-digit brews vs ~10 V-sets, with 3 batch slots per V-set on the roasting side giving an effective ~30-attempt ceiling). The asymmetry drives the brewing workflow's archive-driven disposition (per-coffee single-shot stance) vs the roasting workflow's iterative disposition (per-lot multi-round exploration). Same iteration-loop shape, different budget scale, different operational posture. The roasting-side sibling concept ("V-set budget") is named once in Relationships but remains unheadworded — the 2026-05-17 roasting cross-party audit ([latent-coffee#167](https://github.com/chrismccann-dev/latent-coffee/pull/167)) covered 21 entries but didn't promote V-set budget; future-scope when the cross-side asymmetry becomes load-bearing in workflow authoring.

**Glossary-internal vocabulary**: BREWING.md operationalizes the budget's effects (one-shot bias, 2-3-iteration-then-pivot discipline) without naming the budget itself. Same shape as Wrong-zone trap — the glossary names the meta-concept that operational docs operate on procedurally.
_Avoid_: "brew budget" / "bean budget" / "attempt budget" (none appear in any doc; Iteration budget is the only grep-anchored form), "dial-in budget" (close — dial-in is the colloquial verb but doesn't capture that the cognitive + pragmatic constraints also fire; the budget is loop-scoped not just dialing-scoped), "iteration count cap" (descriptive but flat — collapses the three-constraint structure into a single number), bare "budget" (overloaded — could mean cost / time / cognitive load on any axis).

**Diminishing returns**:
The subjective judgment signal that **terminates an iteration loop when the cup is close enough to the target that further iteration isn't worth the budget cost**. Chris's verbalization: "close enough to the end cup I'm looking for" (per the Iteration loop entry). Defined by the conjunction of three judgment conditions, **not** a measurable threshold:

- **Δ-shrinkage** — each iteration's cup-change is smaller than the previous, signaling trajectory narrowing toward a target.
- **Gap-to-target small** — the residual distance from current cup to intended target is below Chris's "worth-iterating-further" threshold.
- **External ballpark match** — for the roasting side specifically, producer-notes alignment serves as a sanity check the trajectory is closing on the right target (per the Reference roast entry).

**Not convergence-without-direction**: a cup can stabilize at a wrong target (consistent sour, consistent flat) without firing diminishing returns — that pattern is **Wrong-zone trap** or structural wrongness within zone (which BREWING.md Step 3 catches with the strategy-pivot escalation). Diminishing returns specifically names the **success-trajectory terminator**.

**Cross-side symmetry — same concept, both loops** (per CONTEXT.md Relationships: "the canonical iteration-stop signal on both sides — same word, same shape"):

- **Brewing side** — terminates the Iteration loop and triggers reference-brew commit. Single grain level (one loop per coffee). Typically fires at ~3 brews per the Iteration loop entry — **the content of the pragmatic-cap sub-constraint in the Iteration budget**.
- **Roasting side** — fires at two grain levels. **Within-V-set**, designates which batch slot reads "the one to repeat" pre-control-experiment. **At lot-close**, combines with producer-notes-ballpark + expectations-match to designate the lot's Reference roast. Locked via one final control experiment (replicate prior leading slot with two slight adjustments) before commit.

**Two framings of one decision moment**: the **Iteration budget** side names the resource consumed at termination ("pragmatic cap fires, releases the budget"); the **diminishing returns** side names the signal content of the judgment ("Δ-shrinkage hit the threshold, cup is close enough"). Both framings answer different questions about the same decision — resource consumption vs signal quality — and both are real. The budget framing belongs in the budget entry; the signal framing belongs here.

**Glossary-internal vocabulary**: operational docs (BREWING.md, ROASTING.md, prompts) don't name the stop-signal explicitly; iteration prompts just instruct "until reference brew locks" or "until lot close-out." The glossary names the implicit judgment that fires those moments. Same shape as Wrong-zone trap and Iteration budget — three consecutive glossary-internal meta-concepts the operational docs operate on procedurally.
_Avoid_: "close enough" (Chris's verbalization, lives inside this definition — not a competing noun), "good-enough call" (informal, doesn't appear in any doc), "convergence" (process-form / verb — describes the trajectory leading to diminishing returns, not the stop-signal itself; appears in CONTEXT.md example dialogue as "iteration loop converges to diminishing returns"), "iteration-stop signal" (descriptive paraphrase appearing inside this entry's own Relationships gloss — not a competing canonical noun), "pragmatic cap" (the Iteration budget sub-constraint label — names the budget-side role this signal plays, not the signal itself), bare "stopping" / "termination" (overloaded — wrong-zone traps and strategy pivots also terminate loops).

**Strategy pivot**:
The mid-iteration decision to **re-name the extraction strategy** when 2-3 brews of structural wrongness (consistently sour / flat / hollow / one-dimensional despite parameter tweaks) signal that the original Step 1d strategy call was wrong-zone. Operationalized in BREWING.md Step 3: "If 2-3 iterations in and the cup still feels off structurally, Claude should flag this explicitly and ask whether to pivot extraction strategy rather than continue tweaking parameters within the current one."

**The mid-iteration prevention surface paired with Wrong-zone trap**. Two active prevention layers for wrong-zone strategy choices:

1. **Coffee Brief Step 1d gate** (upstream) — catches wrong-zone calls before iteration starts (via prior-archive lookup + WBC corpus check + signal arbitration).
2. **Strategy pivot** (mid-iteration) — catches wrong-zone calls after iteration starts but before Iteration budget exhausts.

Wrong-zone trap is the **failure-mode description** for what fires when both prevention layers miss (canonical example: Jeferson Motta). Strategy pivot is active prevention; Wrong-zone trap is passive description of the prevention-failure outcome. The three are sequential states of the same trajectory, not sibling terminators on equal footing.

Distinct from the other two iteration-termination signals along three dimensions:

- **Timing** — fires mid-loop (turn 2-3), unlike **Diminishing returns** (success-trajectory completion at ~3 brews) or **Wrong-zone trap** (post-hoc description of the full-failure case).
- **Direction** — discrete zone-jump (re-names the strategy), not parameter tightening within-zone.
- **Budget effect** — partially resets the **Iteration budget** structure: cognitive ceiling resets (the new strategy's pattern-recognition restarts at brew 1), physical floor and pragmatic cap continue (bean weight doesn't refill; Chris's "close enough" threshold is unchanged). After a pivot at the 2-3 brew mark on a typical 125-250g bag, ~5-13 brews of physical floor typically remain — a tight new-strategy iteration window, but real recovery on remaining budget.

**Pivot frequency in practice: rare**. The Coffee Brief upstream gate catches most wrong-zone calls before iteration starts, so Strategy pivot fires only when (a) the gate had thin signal (rare cultivar / novel process / silent roaster brew guide) AND (b) iteration produces structural-wrongness shape rather than incremental-wrongness shape. Same logic as **Extraction Confirmed** (the post-hoc reconciliation field) being expected to fire rarely — when Strategy pivot fires, that signal is worth carrying forward because it means the upstream-prevention layer missed something specific.

**Coexists with operational-doc presence** — unlike the three preceding iteration-cluster entries (Wrong-zone trap, Iteration budget, Diminishing returns, all of which had zero BREWING.md mentions). Strategy pivot has BREWING.md presence in two adjacent Step 3 bullets ("pivot extraction strategy" + the prior "strategy shift" phrasing — both refer to this same move; "strategy pivot" wins as canonical because it preserves the discrete zone-jump property that "shift" implies a continuum of, and the strategy axis is non-overlapping per the Strategy zone entry). BREWING.md's framing is operational (when claude.ai should fire the pivot recommendation); CONTEXT.md's framing is analytical (what the pivot is and how it relates to the iteration-termination trio).
_Avoid_: "strategy shift" (legacy BREWING.md phrasing — superseded by "strategy pivot" for cross-doc coherence; "shift" implies gradual continuum, which the strategy axis doesn't have), "zone jump" (informal — captures the discreteness but loses the deliberate-decision framing; pivot is an active call, jump is a passive outcome), "extraction pivot" (under-specified — the pivot is about strategy, not extraction-as-process), "re-pivot" (incorrect — there's no prior pivot to re-do; this is the first pivot), "strategy switch" (mechanical-sounding; pivot preserves the deliberate-judgment framing), bare "pivot" (overloaded — could mean any kind of mid-process course-correction in another context).

**Brewer rotation discipline**:
The standing operational practice that **brewer choice at Step 2 (Recipe Output) is driven by strategy + coffee profile, not by recent usage habit**. Chris owns 11 brewers spanning 4 geometries (cone / flat / wave / immersion-hybrid) per BREWING.md § Brewer Rotation; most days one or two get used. The discipline corrects for this by requiring claude.ai to propose the brewer that best fits the strategy and coffee profile at recipe authoring, even if it means reaching for an under-used brewer — Orea v4 for a flat-bottom Clarity-First test, Hario Switch for a Hybrid (Sequential / Selective Bloom) test, Chemex Funnex for small-dose Clarity-First, Sibarist Brewing System when dripper / paper interface needs to be eliminated as a variable, SWORKS Bottomless Dripper as the canonical office Suppression / Full Expression vehicle.

**The default-brewer trap is the same shape as the default-strategy trap** (BREWING.md § Brewer Rotation, verbatim). Sibling concept to **Wrong-zone trap** on a different mechanical axis:

- **Wrong-zone trap** — strategy choice lands in the wrong Strategy zone; iteration cannot recover by within-zone parameter adjustment.
- **Default-brewer trap** — brewer choice defaults to recent-usage habit rather than strategy-fit; iteration tightens parameters on the wrong vehicle, producing structural-wrongness shape that *looks* like zone-wrongness but is actually brewer-wrongness. Recovery requires brewer-jump, not strategy-pivot.

The two traps share **trap shape** (default-pattern overriding deliberate-choice) but live on different axes (extraction strategy vs vehicle selection). **Diagnostic challenge**: default-brewer-trap failure modes can mimic wrong-zone-trap failure modes, since a wrong vehicle can produce the same structural-wrongness signal (sour / flat / hollow / one-dimensional) that fires the **Strategy pivot** escalation. The discipline's job is to make the brewer call deliberate at Step 2, so that when iteration produces structural-wrongness, the diagnostic surface starts with "is this strategy-wrong?" rather than getting tangled in "is this brewer-wrong vs strategy-wrong?"

**Three operationalizations in BREWING.md**:

1. **Recipe Output (Step 2)** — claude.ai proposes the strategy-fit brewer, not the recent-usage brewer.
2. **WBC anchor cross-refs** (v8.5) — brewer choice has canonical-recipe templates from the WBC corpus: Hario Switch ↔ Ferket 2025 Selective Bloom Hybrid; Orea v4 + Sibarist FAST + Melodrip ↔ Wölfl 2024 Extraction Push; SWORKS ↔ Carlos Medina 2023 Suppression analog. Pull `docs://brewing/wbc-reference.md` + `docs://brewing/wbc-recipes.md` for the lookup.
3. **End-of-coffee review** — explicit retrospective question: "were you relying on the same brewer repeatedly? If so, plan to rotate next coffee." The discipline has a feedback loop, not just a Step-2 reflex.

**Why the discipline is load-bearing**: brewer geometry is a **non-iterative variable inside a single coffee's iteration loop**. Strategy, grind, temp, ratio, pour structure all dial mid-loop; brewer doesn't — once you pour the first bloom, the geometry is locked for that brew, and changing it across brews is a structural variable change, not a within-zone tweak. So the brewer call is more like the Step 1d strategy call than the within-loop parameter dials — both are loop-scoped commitments, both reward deliberate-choice at the top of the loop, both produce trap-shaped failure modes when defaulted.

**Layered vocabulary**: the discipline is CONTEXT.md-internal vocabulary on the meta-concept side (default-brewer trap = analytical layer) and BREWING.md-operational on the practice side (rotation framework, end-of-coffee review = procedural layer). Same shape as **Strategy pivot** — both layers speak, different roles. **Default-brewer trap could earn its own headword in a future audit** (mirror of how Wrong-zone trap got first-class treatment), but for now lives inside this entry as the failure-mode noun the discipline prevents.
_Avoid_: "brewer choice discipline" (under-specified — the discipline is specifically about rotation away from default-usage, not just deliberate choice in general), "brewer selection" (mechanical, loses the default-correction framing), "equipment rotation" (too broad — Chris also rotates filters, but filter rotation is parameter-axis-coupled to brewer choice and lives inside the brewer call), bare "rotation discipline" without "brewer" qualifier (overloaded — could mean any equipment).

**Hybrid sub-form**:
A strict 5-value canonical enum that's **required and only required** when extraction strategy is Hybrid; null on every other strategy. Names which kind of structural compositing the brew is doing across its phase boundaries. Unique architectural anomaly in the taxonomy — Hybrid is the only strategy with a mandatory second-level pick, because **Hybrid itself is a pointer, not content**: the strategy says "the brew's extraction curve changes mode mid-stream" but carries no recipe shape until the sub-form names what kind of change. The five sub-forms:
- `sequential` — if-this-then-that switch between two modes (immersion → percolation), pure on/off, no gradient. Canonical Hario Switch pattern (closed bloom + main, opened drawdown) and SWORKS slow/slow/open.
- `phase_mapped` — each pour is tied to a cup-side attribute it primarily extracts: bloom → aromatics, Pour 1 → acidity, Pour 2 → sweetness, last pour → finish. The brewer's valve / agitation / flow per phase is tuned to emphasize the targeted attribute (e.g. SWORKS Dial 5 restriction on Pour 1 to develop acidity, opened later for sweetness).
- `selective_bloom` — bloom liquid separated from the main brew, evaluated independently, then either recombined (whole or in proportion) or discarded. Ferket 2025 pattern. ⚠ Boundary against Output Selection is intentionally drawn: this stays in Hybrid because it's about *role assignment* (the bloom fraction participates differently in the cup design), while Output Selection is about *fraction discarding* (kept some, threw some away).
- `intensity_clarity_split` — phase-mapped with a specific composition: immersion (or restricted) phase builds body / intensity early, percolation (or opened) phase recovers clarity late. Phase order matters. ⚠ Probably collapsible into `phase_mapped` as a sub-case — flagged.
- `temperature_staged` — combines valve/phase work with kettle temperature modulation across phases. Simplest case: kettle-off natural decline (~2-3°C drop) across the brew. Distinct from the Inverted Temperature Staging modifier because the temperature change is *bound* to the phase boundary rather than running independently.

The sub-form picker exists because **Hybrid alone among the 6 strategies sits on the structural-composition axis** rather than the extraction-intensity axis. The other 5 strategies are self-contained ("Suppression" tells you what the brew is doing"); Hybrid only becomes meaningful once you name the kind of compositing.
_Avoid_: "Hybrid variant", "sub-strategy", "Hybrid type", bare "sub-form" (which sub-form?).
