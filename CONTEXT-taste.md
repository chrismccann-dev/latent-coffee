## The apex (the taste target)

The single cup the whole system resolves toward. Verbatim, Chris-approved (Session 2):

> **The Latent taste target.** The cup is **distinctive and multilayered, and it transforms** - it moves hot-to-warm-to-cool and attack-to-body-to-aftertaste, revealing new layers as it goes. The anti-target is **one-dimensional, whether that one dimension is clean or funky**: a loud single-note fruit-bomb misses exactly as much as a flat clean cup. **Clarity is the carrier, not the goal** - clean extraction and clean processing are the price of entry, and the complexity rides on top without ever going muddy. It is **complexity made legible**.
>
> This is **opinionated and noncommercial**: the cup Chris most enjoys, deliberately *not* the clean/tea-like/high-transparency profile the broader community gravitates toward. The competition play is to brew the cup he loves and **craft the narrative argument for why it is the truest expression of coffee** - not to chase an assumed judge or crowd preference.
>
> **What produces it (primary to free):** *Process* is the deepest lever - slow, controlled, engineered naturals (dark-room/cold-room drying, dry-fermentation, oxidative, yeast-inoculated); slow-and-controlled is what lays complexity in legible layers instead of one-dimensional funk. Process-agnostic in principle, this family is the high-probability vehicle. *Terroir + producer* is a co-occurring bundle - high-altitude Panama Chiriquí and a master-producer tier (Garrido, Lamastus, Altieri, Janson) as the **starting roster, not a closed set**. *Variety* is free - exotic-distinctive genetics as a class (Gesha, Mokkita, Pacamara, Typica Mejorado), no single cultivar as the gate. *Roast* is lighter-to-medium-light - developed enough to carry layered body, never enough to bleed roast into the cup.
>
> **What it scopes:** the search space collapses from "all coffee" to high-altitude slow-engineered naturals from top-tier producers, Panama-centered but not Panama-limited. The target is what sourcing -> roasting -> brewing all resolve toward.

## The canonical apex term

The shorthand the three philosophies travel on. Verbatim, Chris-approved (Session 3):

> **Layered-evolving** *(adj., the Latent apex cup):* multidimensional and *in motion* - distinct layers that change across the temperature arc (hot->warm->cool) and across the structural arc (attack->body->aftertaste), revealing new character as the cup moves. The antonym is **one-dimensional** (flat across both arcs), whether that flatness is clean or loud/funky. Adopted as the shorthand for Session 2's longer "distinctive, multilayered, and it transforms" so it travels cleanly through all three philosophies.

_Avoid_: "expressive" / "loud" / "funky" as synonyms for the target - they name the *anti-target* pole (one-dimensional loudness), not the apex. "Complex" alone is also insufficient: the apex requires complexity that is both *legible* (clarity is the carrier) and *in motion* across both arcs.

## The keystone (the cross-lever through-line)

The single rule that unifies all three lever-philosophies. Verbatim, Chris-approved (Session 3):

> **Reveal the latent, don't inject the absent.** The multilayered, temperature-evolving complexity is *already latent in the bean*. Sourcing finds beans that carry it; roasting and brewing pick the steps to *reveal what is already there*, never to add what isn't. The product name **Latent** encodes this thesis - Chris flagged the homage in-grill. "Injecting the absent" is the cross-lever anti-pattern: inject-engineering (aggressive co-ferment / yeast on the process side), roast-bleed (roast-developed chocolate/nutty/roasty character the bean didn't have), and over-extraction artefacts are all the same sin wearing three hats. Each is *admitted* when it lands legible, but each is the high-variance path toward the apex's anti-target.

### 1. Sourcing philosophy

**Operational home:** [docs/skills/wbc-roasting-archivist/cluster/sourcing/strategy.md § 0](docs/skills/wbc-roasting-archivist/cluster/sourcing/strategy.md). Verbatim, Chris-approved (Session 3):

> Source for the *latent* cup - beans that already carry layered-evolving complexity, so roasting and brewing only have to reveal it. The load-bearing filter is the **process signature**: producers who run a systematized, experimental, engineering-minded processing method (slow-controlled drying, dark/cold-room, oxidative, dry-ferment, yeast) whose output is *legible layered complexity* - the same method-kinship Chris brings to roasting and brewing (the draw is the producer's *embodiment of an engineering mindset*, not name-loyalty). Judge on the **output, not the mechanism**: a co-ferment that lands layered-and-legible is welcome; a slow-dry that lands flat fails. **Reveal-engineering is the trusted, highest-probability *and* easiest vehicle** (the complexity is already in the bean); **inject-engineering is admitted but high-variance** - its easy mode is the anti-target (loud single-note funk), so it earns a place only when it lands legible. The anti-target governs: *one-dimensional, whether clean or funky.*
>
> **Filter stack:** process-signature is the hard gate; exotic-distinctive variety is a *distinctiveness floor* (working theory, not proven rule); high altitude is a *strong prior* (every favorite sits 1,350-2,500m; hypothesized slower-maturation->complexity, untested); geography is **density, not a gate** - Panama Chiriqui is where the signature currently clusters in Chris's experience, but the signature is portable (Ecuador already proves it), so non-Panama engineered naturals are actively sought.
>
> **The producer comes forward, but later.** No current producer relationships, no producer index yet, no lot-scale buying - the tight producer cluster in the favorites was an artifact. "Bring the producer forward" is a *narrative + access* lever that becomes first-order near competition time; today the roster is **open and process-defined, not name-locked.**
>
> **Acquisition (small-sourcer reality):** take-what-you-can-get at 1-2kg; can't interrogate protocol yet (that unlocks with relationship). Source by **cross-referencing weak signal**: (1) producer reputation as proxy for the engineered process, (2) process detail on the label when present, (3) tasting-note complexity tells - varied-not-monotone notes, a complexity vocabulary ("layered" / "complexity" / "multidimensional" / "evolves"), and temperature-variance notes (Forward Coffee writes hot/warm/cool notes per lot; big swing = strong signal, and Forward is the exception not the norm). **Near-term execution: the Oct 2026 Panama push** (World of Coffee Panama, Best of Panama, Forward Coffee, the sourcing-site contact) - request "more like the HiU Los Lajones," not the classic-clean Geshas - *plus* a deliberate non-Panama pull to run the portability test.

### 2. Roasting philosophy

**Operational home:** [docs/skills/coordinator/catalog.md § Roasting domain principles](docs/skills/coordinator/catalog.md). Verbatim, Chris-approved (Session 3):

> The roast adds nothing - it only reveals and preserves the **layered-evolving** complexity already latent in the bean. **Roast-developed character** (chocolate/nutty/roasty/baked) is character the bean didn't have; the roast *injected* it - the cardinal anti-pattern, the roast-side twin of inject-engineering. But roasting has **two hard walls, not one**: a genuinely underdeveloped cup (grassy/hay/vegetative) is as unrecoverable as a roast-bled one, and there's a long, hard-to-hit gradient between them. Cup-side markers: the **underdev signal** and **overdev signal**; bounds: the (soft) **FC floor** and (hard) **drop ceiling**.
>
> **The move - develop fully for expression, relocate suppression to the brew.** Rather than under-roast to dodge roast-bleed (which risks the underdev wall and kills the body that carries the layers), roast **far enough to express every latent layer** - likely *slightly darker* than the old ultra-light instinct, in the high-end-of-light-into-light-medium register (the Glitch / Bean-&-Bean-for-PicoLot school) - and handle the residual roast character **downstream in the brew**. Roast for expression; brew for clarity. Roasting and brewing are therefore **coupled - a two-stage express-then-clarify system, not two independent philosophies.**
>
> **The ceiling is defined by the apex, not by an Agtron number.** Over-development isn't a color; it's the cup going *flat / loud / one-dimensional* and losing its temperature arc. A "developed enough to pop" cup that no longer evolves has failed the apex exactly as much as a roast-bled one. The target is a **narrow band** the V-sets actively hunt.
>
> **Glitch is a mechanism, not a target.** Borrow develop-more-so-you-can-brew-controlled; keep the target layered-evolving, *not* Glitch's loud fruit-pop (which sits on the anti-target pole). Loud-one-note doesn't give a WBC routine its hot/warm/cool arc or its narratable, judge-trackable story; layered shows better than loud at the table.
>
> **One philosophy, purchased and self-roasted - but purchased is paradigm-locked.** Self-roasted: *execute* develop-fully -> narrow band -> brew-suppress. Apex-aligned purchased: *select* roasters in the register, then treat like self-roasted. Off-register purchased: can't be suppressed (wrong-zone trap) -> the brewing training ground. The brew zone is roast-determined.

### 3. Brewing philosophy

**Operational home:** [docs/skills/coordinator/catalog.md § Brewing domain principles](docs/skills/coordinator/catalog.md) + [docs/skills/brewing-assistant/cluster/operational-guide.md](docs/skills/brewing-assistant/cluster/operational-guide.md). Verbatim, Chris-approved (Session 3):

> Brew to *reveal* the layered-evolving complexity latent in the bean - the same reveal-not-inject through-line. The brew is the **clarify stage of the express-then-clarify couple**: when Chris controls or selects the roast (developed-fully for expression), the brew pulls that expression forward and tamps the injected roast byproduct back. So for **apex coffees** (self-roasted or apex-selected purchased - which by the roasting philosophy are developed-fully-for-expression) the **default strategy leans clarify-side - Suppression / Clarity-First / Hybrid** - with **Full Expression / Extraction Push the exception**. For **non-apex coffees** (off-register purchased) the brew is the **training ground**: continue current practice *plus* deliberately reach for more WBC techniques while the stakes are low (these roasts often *force* Full Expression - the **wrong-zone trap**: brew zone is roast-determined - and double as a low-stakes repertoire-building bench). Strategy stays **intent-defined and keyed to the lot+roast**, chosen at Coffee Brief Step 1d before equipment; the clarify-side lean is an *apex default*, not a global override of the strategy-neutral framework. *(Hedge RESOLVED in-session: Chris confirmed the apex clarify-side default + the non-apex training-ground framing.)*
>
> **Whole-arc is the governing evaluation frame.** The apex is an arc claim, so every brew is designed and judged across the *whole* temperature arc - no throwaway station; the transitions *are* part of the product. Disciplined protocol every time: **aroma -> hot ~59-60 degC -> warm ~54-55 degC -> cool <=50 degC and below**, each station recorded; **never iterate off a single temperature** - judge the shape of the evolution. Single-peak **Cooling-Curve-Target** is a *named exception within* the whole-arc default (fires when a coffee's peak window diverges from the 50-55 degC drinking default).
>
> **The clarify-stage toolkit:** intensity/clarity split, thermal staging (two-kettle), **valve modulation** (S-Works Bottomless Dripper - continuous per-phase contact-time control), and **output selection** - the levers that pull expression forward while suppressing roast byproduct.
>
> **Water chemistry is the named #1 frontier lever** - the majority of the brew by mass, the comp winner's edge, Chris's biggest gap, the top item on the research roadmap. **Gated promotion:** until the water research project runs, water stays a cross-cutting doc-layer flag (surfaced via the WBC corpus check's cross-cutting half); on completion it becomes first-class - own taxonomy category + a dedicated "Water Design" Step 1d gate (peer to brewer/filter/temp) + ongoing use. Not forced before then: asking a question with no means to answer is empty discipline.
>
> **Latent is the training ground, not a journal.** Apex-aligned coffees (self-roasted or selected purchased) are brewed narrowly toward layered-evolving; off-register purchased is the repertoire-building playground (mellow drip, high/low temp, double bloom, two-kettle staging), banking technique against the WBC corpus. Daily light/floral/tea-like cups are fine but outside what Latent optimizes for.

## Empirical anchors

Post-arc evidence, added 2026-06-17. The session statements above are verbatim and unedited; this section records measured data that *confirms and quantifies* them without re-authoring them.

### Roast-level anchor (Agtron)

Chris read whole-bean Agtron (Lighttells) on the favorite PicoLot lots he had >=40g of - a direct measurement of the roast register the apex sits in:

| Coffee | Producer | Process | Agtron (WB) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Altieri CHOMBI Gesha | MIMA / Altieri | Natural dry-ferment | 80.7 |
| Emerald Garrido Mokka | Garrido / Mama Cata | Natural | 77.2 (78.4 2nd read) |
| Kotowa Silvia Marina Geisha | Kotowa | Natural | 76.1 |
| Elida Vuelta Washed DRD Gesha | Lamastus | Washed DRD | 75.8 |
| Garrido Mokkita ("mulled wine") | Garrido | Cold-room natural | 71.1 |

**Range WB ~71-81, centered ~76-77, every lot device-classed "Light."** This *quantifies* the existing canon - the apex's "lighter-to-medium-light" and the roasting philosophy's "high-end-of-light-into-light-medium register... slightly darker than the old ultra-light instinct" - and rules out the Nordic/ultra-light-bleached end (nothing above ~81). It is not a new claim; it is the measured location of the register the philosophies already name.

**Internal gradient (descriptive, not a setpoint):** the lightest favorite (Altieri CHOMBI, 80.7) is the most *transparent*; the darkest (Mokkita, 71.1) is the most *layered*. So within the band, lighter leans transparency and more-developed leans layered body - a dial for placing the "narrow band the V-sets hunt" (Roasting philosophy). Per that philosophy, **the ceiling is still defined by the apex, not by an Agtron number** - this anchor describes *where the favorites land*, it does not prescribe a target color.

**Caveats:** (1) whole-bean Lighttells readings - per [brewing-assistant operational-guide](docs/skills/brewing-assistant/cluster/operational-guide.md), *ground* Agtron at dose-out is the authoritative brew-time extraction prior, not WB, and the WB-to-ground delta can be wide; these numbers describe roast color, not a brew prior. (2) Cross-device calibration (Lighttells vs the cupping Agtron device, `whb_agtron`) is needed before ~76-77 is treated as a literal self-roast setpoint. (3) These are PicoLot's roasts (the apex reference), not Chris's self-roasts.
