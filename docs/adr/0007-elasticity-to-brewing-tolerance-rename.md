# 0007 — Elasticity column rename and the brewing-tolerance × acceptable-roast-window concept split

The Roast Character attribute previously called **elasticity** is split into two parallel concepts: **acceptable roast window** (latitude on the *roast* side — the range of primary-lever values that keep the cup in the desired zone) and **brewing tolerance** (latitude on the *brew* side — how well the cup holds up when brewing variables are pushed toward extremes). The `roast_learnings.elasticity` column is renamed to `brewing_tolerance` (migration 060); the existing `roast_learnings.roast_window_width` column carries the roast-side concept and is relabeled "Acceptable Roast Window" on the ResolvedView CharacterCard. Alternatives considered: leave elasticity as a single conflated concept (preserved the physics metaphor but had ambiguous polarity — high elasticity = forgiving? sensitive? — and conflated roast deviation latitude with brew deviation latitude), or add `brewing_tolerance` alongside `elasticity` and drop elasticity later (avoided rename churn but multiplied storage for the same axis and delayed the workflow-language fix indefinitely). The rename + split wins because (i) elasticity's ambiguous polarity was actively misleading in `push_roast_learnings` Tool descriptions and close-lot.md prompts — both repeatedly clarified "elasticity = brewing tolerance" inline; (ii) the two latitudes operate on different lifecycle phases and don't reduce to one number (the Sudan Rume Washed #133 reference has a narrow acceptable roast window but wide brewing tolerance — peak inlet within ±1°C, but the cup holds across Suppression / Full Expression / Extraction Push); (iii) Latent's house posture is **controls-both-sides** (Chris roasts and brews; the roasted bean has to survive being pushed or pulled from either direction, and brewing intensity is a deliberate per-cup decision rather than a cafe-repeatable target — see Latent's renamed motto "Roast for brewing tolerance, brew for intensity") so the *brew*-side latitude IS the load-bearing axis worth naming, while the *roast*-side latitude is the operational constraint within which the bean has to land. Reversal cost is high (data carries through the column rename, but the substrate-wide grep + 12-surface change + downstream prompts + Tool descriptions are the unwind cost; renaming back would require the inverse churn plus rebuilding the disambiguation prose this ADR encodes). Locked 2026-05-19 via Sprint 10.
