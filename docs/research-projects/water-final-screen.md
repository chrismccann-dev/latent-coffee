### Analysis

- **HF5 (the track's headline):** blind forced ranking = 4:1 blend > straight > NaCl, with blend-over-straight explicitly tie-adjacent. - **HF2:** 2 ppm NaCl produced zero savory inject on the Gesha in BOTH flights it appeared in, and won flight 2 semi-blind - but ranked last blind in flight 3 the same day. Verdict: a workable low dose EXISTS on this coffee (contradicting a blanket "no workable dose" row), but the preference effect did not survive blind re-test. - **HF3/HF4:** the sulfate (CaSO4) axis is confirmed live on the Gesha - structure that holds sweetness into the finish, the exact deficit the anchor shows - but at 17% share (5:1) it still read slightly overdone unblinded, while 20% (4:1) WON blind in flight 3. MgSO4 as sulfate carrier deposits savoriness, not sweetness, on this coffee; the sweetness-payload hypothesis is dead here and the Phase 2b flight-5 read did not replicate (P6T4-AI-2).

Descriptor→salt mapping is unreliable even for the trained operator; blind protocol is load-bearing for ordering claims.
