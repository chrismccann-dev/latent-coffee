## Hypotheses (pre-state predictions before scoring, per Lesson #16)

- **HF1 (silica generalizes?):** +1 drop/L (~12 ppm) on the peak base lifts the whole cup + finish on the Gesha as it did on the PB. *If it holds on 2/2 coffees, the § 3 silica row de-provisionalizes to "verified 2-coffee amplifier"; if it fails, it stays a PB-only quirk.*
- **HF2 (NaCl dose-tolerance on a fruity natural):** the PB's ≥5 ppm savory inject came with a latent tomato note to amplify. The Gesha (mango/violet/strawberry) has no obvious savory anchor — does a LOW dose (~2 ppm) find a workable roundness, or does NaCl inject on this coffee too? *2-step ladder, stop early on inject.*
- **HF3 (5:1 vs 4:1 CaSO₄ ratio):** 5:1 keeps most of the silky/structured character at lower sulfate share. *Directional read only — one cup.*
- **HF4 (MgCl₂:MgSO₄ sweetness blend):** a 4:1 MgCl₂:MgSO₄ blend (same 80/20 GH split as the CaSO₄ variant, sulfate carried by Mg) deposits sweetness-in-body and ties or beats the CaSO₄ blend. *The flight-5 exploratory mix says live.*
- **HF5 (blind ordering adjudication):** blind re-run of straight MgCl₂ vs the best blend of the day resolves § 6 row 3's primary/variant ordering. *No prediction — that's the point of blind.*
