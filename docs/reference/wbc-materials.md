**Authoring source** — YouTube transcripts of WBC routine presentations. Every competitor talks through their recipe + style + water + intent during the live routine; transcripts back-populated a spreadsheet that became the corpus. **Two-pass construction**:
- **First pass (2026-05-03, Sprint 2.7)** — 1st / 2nd / 3rd winners 2022-2025, ~18 recipes. Drove the canonical strategy enum's first WBC-triggered expansion: pre-WBC was `Clarity-First` / `Balanced Intensity` / `Full Expression`; first pass added `Suppression` + `Extraction Push`, reaching 5 intensity strategies + the Two-Axis Framework.
- **Second pass (2026-05-06, v8.4)** — all participants (not just top 3) 2022-2025, 102 recipes. Recipe-level signal is bottoms-up decomposed into 5 axes + 8 families + ~38 subtypes — the same methodology used for cultivar / terroir / process / producer canonical taxonomies, applied to a different domain.

**Data-density asymmetry across the 4 docs** — brewing is the most complete because that's what competitor routines are primarily about; sourcing-side and roasting-side data are **byproducts** of the brewing data collection (competitors mention their coffee + producer + roast in passing, but in less detail than they talk about the brewing recipe). The asymmetry is preserved in the doc structure: `wbc-reference.md` + `wbc-recipes.md` carry the full bottoms-up decomposition; `wbc-roasting.md` is an open-ideas hypotheses doc (~100 entries, mostly 2-5 lines each, no equivalent 38-subtype taxonomy); `wbc-sourcing.md` is portfolio framing with Tier 1/2/3 priorities and Latent inventory snapshot. The data-density asymmetry is *load-bearing* for how each doc gets used at workflow time — brewing docs as cheat-sheet, roasting / sourcing docs as hypothesis-and-priority anchors.

**Strategy-zone completeness**:
The principle that Latent's brewing strategy taxonomy should be **legible against the complete known world of strategy zones**, even when most of that world is explicitly not adopted. The principle's purpose is **proactive Motta-prevention** — the WBC reference materials carry a full map of every strategy zone serious competitive brewing employs, so that wrong-zone traps become impossible *to enter* unaware. Chris may decide not to operate in a zone, but he will never not-know-it-exists. Extends the strategy-geography metaphor (strategy = country, sub-form / modifier = state, iteration = city) one level up: the WBC corpus maps the **complete continent of strategy zones**, including the ones Latent currently doesn't visit.

**Awareness vs adoption** — the load-bearing distinction the principle produces. **Awareness surface** = everything mapped in the WBC reference materials (5 foundational control axes + 8 strategy families + 38 subtypes + 102 recipes). **Adoption surface** = Latent's canonical brewing taxonomy (6 strategies + 4 modifiers + 5 Hybrid sub-forms). The gap between them — recorded in `wbc-reference.md § Consciously not pursuing` with explicit per-item reasons — is the **deliberately-not-pursued** set; awareness is universal, adoption is selective. The two halves together cover the full mapped surface.

**Coverage strategy parallels cultivar / terroir** (Chris, 2026-05-16) — the WBC mapping is **comprehensive** (in the canonical-registries Coverage-strategy sense), not tier-scoped, not owned-comprehensive, not self-only. The 102-recipe corpus across 4 competition years × all participants (not just winners) produces enough data density that new WBC routines are expected to map cleanly onto the existing 8 families / 38 subtypes or to be **combinations** of them, rather than introducing truly new families. **Saturation projection** — same asymptotic 95-96% target as the canonical-registries cluster's Pick-not-author principle; new-family-discovery rate decays as competition years accumulate; the mapping converges as the corpus grows.

**Distinct from canonical taxonomies in two ways**: (1) the awareness surface is *external*, not Latent-specific (it maps competitors' work, not Chris's); (2) the canonical adoption decisions (which zones to promote into Latent's strategy enum vs keep awareness-only) happen via **Strategy promotion** (already canonical — v8.4 Hybrid promotion is the precedent), not via the canonical-registries arbiter procedure. Same compounding-knowledge motivation, different operational mechanism.

**Substrate gap is the per-entity analog at the canonical-taxonomies layer** — both name "data exists, but the canonical surface doesn't yet receive it." Substrate gap closes via taxonomy expansion or deliberate scope-out at the entity level (e.g. meso terroir demoted); strategy-zone completeness closes via Strategy promotion or Consciously-not-pursuing at the family/axis level. Different scales, same diagnostic pattern.
_Avoid_: "WBC completeness" (close — but the principle is about *strategy-zone* completeness specifically, not "completeness of the WBC corpus"), "continent mapping" (informal — fine in prose, but the geography metaphor isn't the canonical noun), "wrong-zone-trap prevention" (anchors on the failure mode rather than the principle), "strategy world map" (Chris's spoken phrasing — informal; "strategy-zone completeness" is the canonical noun), "WBC saturation" (overloaded — saturation is a related concept, see canonical-registries Pick-not-author).

**Consciously not pursuing**:
The dedicated `wbc-reference.md` section that records **awareness-locked-but-not-adopted decisions** at the strategy-family / axis / subtype level — the operational form of the Strategy-zone-completeness principle's awareness-vs-adoption gap. Each entry pairs the WBC technique name with the **explicit reason it's not pursued**: hardware overhead, blend stagecraft (no single-origin transfer), out-of-context (heritage brewer modifications), redundant with existing practice (Triangle variable thinking), or coverage decision (Time Distribution as a foundational axis, Filter-Mediated Flow as a foundational axis). The reason is load-bearing — the section exists so the next "should we do this?" question has a recorded answer, and so a future reader knows the omission is deliberate rather than ignorant.

**Doc-section as ADR-substitute** — each "Consciously not pursuing" entry plays the role an ADR file would play for a non-adoption decision, but lives inline in `wbc-reference.md` rather than at `docs/adr/`. The decision *is* hard-to-reverse (re-mapping the taxonomy + back-applying to existing brews is costly), surprising-without-context (a future reader will ask "why doesn't Latent have Time Distribution as a foundational axis when the WBC corpus uses it heavily?"), and the result of a real trade-off — meeting the canonical 3-criteria ADR bar. Kept inline because the section is a natural grouping; promoting individual decisions to `/adr` would scatter related context.

**Promotion-from-not-pursuing is a documented pattern** — entries can move out when Chris's usage justifies it. The v8.5 Role-Based Pulse promotion is the canonical precedent: Time Distribution stayed a foundational-axis non-add (still listed in v8.5's section), but the **Role-Based Pulse sub-case** was promoted from "consciously not pursuing" to a 4th canonical Axis 2 modifier. Same shape as Strategy promotion's modifier → strategy elevation, applied to the awareness → adoption boundary.
_Avoid_: "WBC non-additions" (close — but Chris's phrasing in the doc is "Consciously not pursuing"), "out-of-scope list" (under-specifies the *deliberate* + *with-reasons* framing), "WBC exclusions" (technical but loses the explicit-awareness-but-deliberate-choice nuance), "deferred features" (close — but deferred implies "maybe later"; consciously not pursuing is explicitly "decided no, with a recorded reason — promotion is possible but requires fresh evidence").

**Full-map workflow accessibility** (Chris-locked 2026-05-16) — the operational mandate paired with Consciously-not-pursuing: claude.ai's workflow-authoring process (Coffee Brief construction in particular) must have **MCP read access to the FULL WBC awareness surface**, including the Consciously-not-pursuing section, NOT a pre-filtered view of just Latent's adopted surface. Chris's framing: "I want to make sure the brain and the process for constructing a Coffee Brief is well aware of the whole map and all the zones... it should have access to Google Maps, then go down to where it needs to go, but we shouldn't not give it access to the full map. It should always be aware that it exists." Why this matters: if claude.ai forgets a zone exists (e.g. Time Distribution as a foundational axis), the next Coffee Brief might wrong-zone-trap on a coffee that wants that zone — exactly the Motta failure mode the WBC corpus was authored to prevent. Operationalized today by `wbc-reference.md` being a `docs://` Resource + `read_doc` Tool target with all sections (including Consciously-not-pursuing) intact; future re-organizations of the doc must preserve the full-map shape.

**Foundational control axes**:
The 5-axis analytical decomposition WBC competition routines collectively employ for brewing decisions — Chris's bottoms-up derivation from the 102-recipe corpus during WBC reference materials authoring. The **awareness-surface canonical** for brewing-strategy thinking; distinct from Latent's adopted 2-axis framework (which compresses these 5 into 1 first-class strategy axis + 1 heterogeneous modifier bucket — see Two-Axis Framework for the compression rationale, Strategy-zone completeness for the awareness-vs-adoption framing).

The five axes:

- **Extraction Intensity** — *how much is extracted*. Main levers: grind, ratio, temperature, water chemistry. **Fully absorbed** as Latent's Axis 1 — the 5 intensity strategies (Suppression / Clarity-First / Balanced Intensity / Full Expression / Extraction Push) cover this axis directly. Hybrid is a structural exception (sits on the structural-composition axis but operates with Extraction-Intensity choices inside each phase).
- **Time Distribution** — *when extraction happens*. Main levers: pours, pulse timing, immersion duration. **Partially absorbed**: percolation-only sub-case via the `role_based_pulse` modifier (v8.5 promotion); immersion sub-case via the Phase-Mapped Hybrid sub-form. Remainder (fixed pulse / drain-based pulse / double bloom / short-long-short) lives at doc-layer (Cross-Cutting Control Patterns in `wbc-reference.md`) without first-class promotion — the v8.4 deliberate-non-add reasoning. The **largest under-served axis** — 34 of 102 recipes use Time Distribution as the primary axis.
- **Physical System** — *where / how extraction occurs in the bed*. Main levers: brewer geometry, bed layering, particle-size distribution, grind separation. **Out of scope for Latent** — the Structural Systems family is almost entirely blend stagecraft (24+ recipes are layered beds / dual grinds / blend ratios), doesn't transfer to single-origin home brewing. Consciously not pursuing locks the decision per-pattern with reasons.
- **External Control** — *stabilizing or shaping the process externally*. Main levers: flow tools, thermal handling, agitation devices. **Partially absorbed** as two modifier types: `aroma_capture` (chilling-ball / Paragon for volatile preservation) + `inverted_temperature_staging` (low → high or high → low temp across phases). The `cooling_curve_target` named consideration at Coffee Brief Step 1d touches this axis at a different operational layer (evaluation / service timing, not extraction).
- **Output Selection** — *which parts of extraction are kept*. Main levers: discarding early / late fractions, yield cutoff, post-brew dilution. **Fully absorbed** as the `output_selection` modifier (4 forms post-v8.5: `early_cut` / `late_cut` / `both` / `dilution`). The Selective Bloom Hybrid sub-form is intentionally split off — role assignment, not fraction discarding (see Hybrid sub-form entry).

**Absorption status taxonomy** — three statuses describing each WBC axis's relationship to Latent's adopted surface, the operational form of the awareness-vs-adoption split:

- **Fully absorbed** — Latent's canonical surface covers the WBC axis directly + completely. Today: Extraction Intensity (Axis 1) + Output Selection (output_selection modifier).
- **Partially absorbed** — Latent canonical covers a sub-set of the WBC axis; remainder lives at doc-layer (Cross-Cutting Control Patterns) for awareness without first-class promotion. Today: Time Distribution + External Control.
- **Out of scope** — WBC axis mapped in awareness (Strategy-zone completeness) but adoption deliberately not pursued. Today: Physical System.

**The translation layer IS the architecture, not a hack** (Chris-locked 2026-05-16). Latent's 4 modifier types map structurally 1:1 onto WBC axes 2-5 (Output Selection ↔ `output_selection`; External Control ↔ `aroma_capture` + `inverted_temperature_staging`; Time Distribution ↔ `role_based_pulse` + Phase-Mapped Hybrid; Physical System ↔ out-of-scope). Latent represents the same information content as 1 strategy axis + 1 typed bag, rather than 5 separate first-class axes. The 5-axis adoption was deliberately rejected because (a) 3 of the 5 axes (Physical System / Time Distribution / External Control) would be empty or single-valued on most brews — exactly the **category bloat** failure mode (separate entry); (b) **Strategy promotion** already exists as the operational mechanism for moving sub-axes from awareness to adoption when usage justifies (v8.4 Hybrid, v8.5 Role-Based Pulse); (c) Strategy-zone completeness already gives the awareness floor without forcing schema expansion.

**Promotion authority is Chris-driven, not system-driven** (paired with Strategy promotion's existing rules) — promoting a WBC axis or family from awareness to adoption is the human operator's judgment call, NOT claude.ai's authoring decision and NOT Claude Code's arbiter decision. The mapping itself (which WBC axis lands where in Latent) is system vocabulary; the **promotion timing** is Chris's call. Concretely: claude.ai may surface "this brew's framing routinely reaches for Time Distribution moves the modifier system absorbs awkwardly" as a doc-proposal observation, but the decision to promote (e.g. add `time_distribution` as a first-class axis) is Chris's per-sprint scoping call after enough cross-coffee pattern accumulates.

_Avoid_: "WBC 5-axis taxonomy" (close — fine in prose; "Foundational control axes" is the canonical noun from `wbc-reference.md`), "WBC axes" (under-specified — could mean 5 foundational or 8 family), "control axes" (bare — overloaded against generic control-system meaning), "brewing axes" (under-specified — Latent's 2 axes are also brewing axes), "axes" without qualifier (always pin "foundational" or "Latent's two" so the layer is unambiguous).

**Absorption status**:
The per-axis classification of each WBC Foundational control axis's relationship to Latent's adopted brewing taxonomy — three values (**fully absorbed** / **partially absorbed** / **out of scope**) that together name the awareness-vs-adoption gap operationally. Distinct from the canonical-registries cluster's **Aggregation-eligibility** (page-bearing / page-deferred / validation-only) which classifies *axes' relationship to knowledge aggregation*; same per-axis-status shape, different decision. Distinct from the canonical-registries cluster's **Coverage strategy** (comprehensive / tier-scoped / owned-comprehensive / self-only) which classifies *how far a registry tries to reach into a domain*; Coverage strategy describes registry extent, Absorption status describes Latent-canonical-surface coverage of an external taxonomy. **The pattern generalizes** — any future imported taxonomy (e.g. the SCA tasting-protocol vocabulary, an alternative roasting school's terminology) would also use Absorption status as its translation surface; today's only instance is the WBC Foundational control axes mapping.
_Avoid_: "axis status" (under-specified — many things have status; absorption-into-Latent is the specific framing), "mapping status" (close — but mapping is what produces absorption status, not the status itself), "coverage status" (overloaded against Coverage strategy), "adoption level" (close — but adoption is the binary boundary; the per-axis status carries 3 values + the partially-absorbed middle is the load-bearing one).

**Sourcing priority**:
The WBC-corpus-derived 3-value classification of green-bean lots for **buying decisions** — a "soft guiding post" (Chris-locked 2026-05-16) for sourcing, NOT a canonical-validation surface. Lives in [wbc-sourcing.md § 7](../../docs/skills/wbc-roasting-archivist/cluster/sourcing/strategy.md). Three values, each with a heuristic green-bean shape:

- **T1 — Best Fit** — cleanest-fit for the dominant WBC competition palate. Examples: washed Panama Gesha, white honey Gesha (Colombia or Panama), clean natural Panama Gesha, Bolivia washed Gesha / Takesi-style, washed or clean honey Chiroso, clean Sidra, Sudan Rume washed / controlled natural.
- **T2 — High Learning Value** — high-information lots that teach how cultivar / process / roast interact. Examples: Mikava CM Gesha, CGLE Mandela / CGLE 17, Inmaculada Sudan Rume / Eugenioides, Janson natural Gesha, Finca Deborah washed / hybrid Gesha, Las Flores Java / Chiroso.
- **T3 — Buy Selectively** — high-intensity competition coffees that can become stylized or process-forward. Examples: thermal-shock Wilton Benitez-style coffees, heavy anaerobic natural Gesha, Eugenioides standalone, Liberica blends, process-heavy Ombligon.

**Naming-clash disambiguation against Producer Reference role tier** (Chris-locked 2026-05-16) — both schemes use Tier 1/2/3 numbering, but they classify different things:
- **Producer Reference role tier** (Anchor / Signal / Experimental) — names the producer's **calibration purpose** in Chris's research-and-aggregation system. Determines how much registry content gets authored per producer + the producer's role in cross-coffee aggregation. Author-side / taxonomy-build decision.
- **Sourcing priority** (T1 Best Fit / T2 Learning / T3 Selective) — names the green-bean lot's **buying priority** against the WBC-palate target. Author-time decision is irrelevant; this is a recurring buy-decision input. WBC-research byproduct.

The two schemes are **orthogonal** — a T1 Anchor producer (e.g. CGLE) can produce T2-Learning sourcing-priority greens (Sudan Rume Natural learning lot) AND T3-Selective sourcing-priority greens (heavy anaerobic experimental) AND historically T1-Best-Fit greens (clean washed lots) — same producer, three different sourcing priorities depending on the green. Reference role classifies the *producer*; Sourcing priority classifies the *lot*. The historical naming overlap was incidental — Chris's intent was never to lockstep them; the WBC sourcing tier was renamed Sourcing priority during the 2026-05-16 canonical-registries / WBC-cluster grilling to make the disambiguation explicit.

**Latent-adjusted sourcing priority** — the re-ranked reading after applying Chris's *actual* palate (VERY-expressive + Suppression-leaning) against the WBC-palate-keyed raw tiers. Under the adjustment:
- **T1 (raw)** is **important but not urgent** — clean clarity-Gesha is over-indexed in specialty coffee right now and not Chris's current focus, BUT see Skill-maintenance lane (separate entry) for why the lane still earns periodic buying.
- **T2 (raw)** is **aligned with current focus** — roast-learning hybrids (Sudan Rume / Mandela / CGLE 17 / Java) are already core to Latent's recent inventory.

The 3 values are not a canonical registry; they're an analytical framework with worked examples.

**Skill-maintenance lane**:
An adopted strategy zone or coffee style where Chris's *personal preference* pulls away from regular practice, but **periodic practice is maintained explicitly to prevent the zone from becoming a blind spot**. Sits between regular-adoption (in the awareness-vs-adoption frame from Strategy-zone completeness) and Consciously-not-pursuing — Chris knows this lane exists, hasn't deliberately scoped it out, and is choosing to *practice it periodically* even though it isn't where his current focus naturally pulls.

**Canonical instance** — the **clean clarity-Gesha roasting lane**. Chris's framing (2026-05-16): "the clean clarity-Gesha type coffees, I do have a little bit of a blind spot towards. And even though maybe I may lean towards more funkier process suppression-type stuff, I really do need to do a whole green-bean lot iteration exercise around normal clean Geisha type stuff because that is so represented on the WBC scene. It can't be a blind spot for me even though that might not necessarily lean towards my personal direct preference." The WBC corpus is *heavy* on washed Panama Gesha and clean clarity coffees — they dominate the dataset; maintaining roasting skill on this lane keeps championship-mode readiness viable. The wbc-roasting.md framing names the cadence: "A reference clean-Gesha lot every 2-3 buying cycles keeps the lane practiced."

**Distinct from Consciously not pursuing** — Consciously not pursuing is "decided NO with a recorded reason"; Skill-maintenance is "decided YES periodically, with the natural pull elsewhere." Different boundary on the same Strategy-zone-completeness map.

**Distinct from regular adoption** — regular adoption follows organic interest; skill-maintenance is deliberate counter-pull practice. Without the explicit framing, skill-maintenance lanes naturally drift toward becoming blind spots over time because new lots / brews / experiments default to where preference points.

**Generalizes beyond clean-Gesha** — any zone the WBC corpus surfaces as load-bearing for championship-mode skill but where Chris's preference naturally pulls away is a candidate. Today's only locked instance is clean clarity-Gesha; future candidates could include specific brewing strategies (e.g. a Clarity-First brewing skill-maintenance if Suppression dominates Chris's recent brews) or specific roast intents (e.g. fast counterflow on a particular cultivar lane).

**Operational mechanism is sourcing-time** — skill-maintenance lanes earn their place via the buying cycle. `wbc-sourcing.md § Best next sourcing moves` carries item 6: "Clean washed Panama Gesha — schedule for a future cycle to keep the clean clarity-Gesha roast lane practiced; not the urgent buy." That's the operational form — periodic buying at a relaxed cadence, not aggressive sourcing.
_Avoid_: "blind spot" (the *failure mode* the lane prevents, not the lane itself), "practice lane" (under-specified — could mean any practice cadence), "maintenance lane" (close — but skill-maintenance pins the *why*; the maintenance is specifically about skill retention against natural-preference drift), "secondary lane" (close — but secondary implies a hierarchy; skill-maintenance is parallel to primary focus, not below), "discipline lane" (close — but discipline implies effort against resistance; skill-maintenance is more periodic-rotation than ongoing-discipline).

**Portfolio lanes**:
The 5-lane portfolio-construction frame Chris uses for **green-bean inventory balance** — distinct from Sourcing priority (which classifies per-lot buying priority). The 5 lanes name the **role-shape** a balanced inventory should cover, regardless of which individual lots fill each lane. Lives in [wbc-sourcing.md § 10](../../docs/skills/wbc-roasting-archivist/cluster/sourcing/strategy.md). Formalized 5-lane authored shape locked Sprint T2 (2026-05-18) after living as a 4-lane authored shape + implicit-5th-via-gap-analysis between 2026-05-09 and 2026-05-18.

- **Reference clarity** — clean clarity-Gesha-style. Examples: washed Panama Gesha, Bolivia Gesha, clean Ethiopia 74158.
- **Fruit-tea expression** — clean natural / honey-process with fruit-forward + tea-like character. Examples: natural Panama Gesha, white honey Gesha, clean Sidra.
- **Process learning** — high-process-intervention coffees for cultivar / process / roast interaction learning. Examples: CM Gesha, mossto washed, cold-fermented natural.
- **Roast-learning hybrids** — Gesha-alternative hybrids whose roast behavior teaches across cultivar lineages. Examples: Mandela, Sudan Rume, Java, Chiroso, CGLE 17.
- **Value / roast-practice** — sub-$30/kg cheap experiment substrate for 3-batch dev ladders + rest-curve experiments + green-curve calibration without burning expensive lots on basic-skill iterations. Examples: washed Bourbon / Caturra; value-tier Brazil / Guatemala / Honduras washed. Eats fast at 100g/batch — even a 5-pound value lot supports ~22 practice roasts, which the iteration cadence consumes quickly enough to matter (see Sourcing constraints).

**Portfolio gap analysis** — the operational practice of mapping current inventory against the 5-lane frame to surface missing lanes. Runs at each inventory-change moment (new green purchase, lot close-out, archive). Today's worked example is `wbc-sourcing.md § Latent inventory mapped to tiers` (2026-05-09 snapshot) which calls out:
- Reference clarity: **off-pattern only** (Bukure Red Bourbon; no active washed Panama Gesha)
- Fruit-tea expression: **reasonable coverage** (GESHA CLOUDS)
- Process learning: **well-covered** (El Paraíso + Higuito + Wush Wush + Sudan Rume Natural)
- Roast-learning hybrids: **partial coverage** (Sudan Rume only; no Mandela / CGLE 17 / Sidra / Chiroso / Java active)
- Value / roast-practice: **real gap** (no active sub-$30/kg practice coffee)

**Distinct from Process lanes A / B / C** (in `wbc-sourcing.md § 4`) — Process lanes A/B/C classify *processing intent* (Lane A clean / Lane B learning / Lane C high-risk). The two frames overlap (a Process-Lane-A coffee usually fills the Reference-clarity Portfolio lane), but aren't identical — Process-Lane-C high-risk coffees usually fill the Process-learning Portfolio lane; Fruit-tea-expression and Roast-learning-hybrids Portfolio lanes have no clean Process-lane mapping. Portfolio lanes is the portfolio-construction frame; Process lanes A/B/C is the process-intent classification orthogonal to portfolio role.

**Distinct from Skill-maintenance lane** — Skill-maintenance lane is a *kind* of adopted zone (clean clarity-Gesha being the canonical example); Portfolio lanes is the inventory-shape frame. They intersect — the Reference-clarity Portfolio lane is largely the Skill-maintenance lane in operational practice — but Portfolio lanes is the higher-level umbrella covering all 5 role-shapes.
_Avoid_: "lanes" without qualifier (now overloaded across Skill-maintenance lane + Process lanes A/B/C + Portfolio lanes — always pin which), "inventory lanes" (close — but Portfolio lanes is the canonical noun matching wbc-sourcing.md § 10), "buy lanes" (under-specified — could mean any sourcing decomposition), "portfolio buckets" (informal — fine in prose), "portfolio slots" (close — slot suggests fixed shape; lane suggests parallel paths through inventory shape).

**Sourcing constraints**:
The physical and structural realities that bound Chris's green-bean buying — the **substrate the Sourcing priority + Portfolio lanes + (future) Risk-tier sourcing frames all operate against**. Three layers:

- **Roest L200 100g-per-batch capacity** — every roast is 100g; a 1-pound (454g) lot supports ~4-5 roasts; a 5-pound (~2.3kg) lot supports ~22-23 roasts. The volume + access patterns of importer / exporter relationships keyed to commercial buyers (typically 50-200kg orders, multi-month contracts) don't apply; access patterns must be hobbyist / specialty / direct-relationship-keyed, not commercial.

**Risk-tier sourcing**:

**Calibration pair**:
A sourcing practice Chris uses to **anchor his interpretation of a green-bean lot** against an existing roasted expression of the same lot — the **pairing** of (green bean lot, roasted bean from a known roaster or the producer themselves) acquired together for calibration. Specifically Chris-articulated 2026-05-16, flagged for inclusion as a standing sourcing-prompt practice (paired follow-up item).

**Worked example** — CGLE Sudan Rume Natural: (1) Chris cupped the coffee at an event with the head roaster from Special Guests Coffee (face-to-face calibration data point); (2) Chris bought the roasted version of that specific lot from Special Guests (a structured roaster expression of the green); (3) Chris bought the raw green lot from CGLE direct (his own roasting input). The three together produce a **multi-anchor calibration baseline**:
- **Producer's tasting notes** (already canonical via `green_beans.producer_tasting_notes`) — external ballpark anchor.
- **Known roaster's interpretation** — one validated expression of what the green CAN be at competition-roast quality (when sourced from a Reference-role-Tier-1-Anchor or known-style roaster).
- **Chris's brewing of the roaster's roasted bean** — Chris's own consumption-condition cup of the roaster's expression; calibrates Chris's brewing layer against the roaster's roasting layer.
- **Chris's own roast** — the target of the V-set work, to be compared against the multi-anchor baseline.

**Massively accelerates dialing in his own roast of the green** — collapses the design search space substantially. Without a calibration pair, V1 design is anchored only on producer notes + carry-forward learnings from similar cultivar / terroir / process lots (which may not match this specific producer's processing nuances). With a calibration pair, Chris knows what the lot is *capable of* in a competition-roast hands, and his V-set work is directly comparable against a known target.

**Not always available** — depends on finding a roaster who's bought + roasted the specific green lot Chris is also buying. The intersection of "I'm buying this green" + "a roaster I trust roasted this green" + "the roaster's roasted bean is still purchasable" is narrow. When available, deliberately sought; when not, V1 design falls back on producer notes + carry-forward learnings only.

**Pairs with face-to-face cupping at events** — the highest-fidelity calibration data point. Cupping with the head roaster at an event adds palate-calibration metadata that can't be reconstructed from the bag-only purchase later. The Special Guests Coffee + CGLE Sudan Rume Natural example combined all three: event cupping with the head roaster + bag purchase of the roasted version + green-bean lot from CGLE directly.

Today it happens opportunistically when available; the operational mandate is to *actively search for the pair* when buying competition-grade lots, since the calibration uplift is high enough to justify the additional search cost.

**Same-green blending**:
The umbrella WBC-derived experiment shape for **single-lot blending experiments** — three variants that all operate on one green lot without requiring multi-origin sourcing. The deliberate Latent-scope alternative to multi-origin blend stagecraft (which lives in Consciously not pursuing). Lives in [wbc-roasting.md § Blending experiments](../../docs/skills/wbc-roasting-archivist/cluster/wbc-roasting.md). Chris's framing: "the most direct WBrC lesson for Roest work."

Three variants:

- **Same-green roast layering** (development split) — 3 batches at differential development / drop levels from one green lot. Canonical Roest L200 protocol: Batch A floral clarity (fast counterflow, shortest clean dev, ~9-10% dev, earliest acceptable color drop); Batch B fruit + sweetness (same profile but slightly more late Maillard / slightly longer dev, ~11-12% dev or +1-2°C drop); Batch C structure (slightly more development or higher drop, still light, ~12-14% dev or +2-4°C drop). The day-7 evaluation produces a **blend matrix** of 6 cups: A alone / B alone / C alone / 70%B+30%A / 70%B+30%C / 50%A+50%C. Same pourover recipe + same rest day + ground Agtron tracking per component. WBC precedents: George Peng 2025 1st (3 dev levels, single Mount Totumas Gesha), Raul Rodas 2025 (Stronghold + Link split on Finca El Injerto Gesha), Barry McGeehin 2025 (50/50 Stronghold + Link split on Esmeralda Ellipse).
- **Same-green Agtron-paired blending** (color-targeted split) — two roasts at deliberately different Agtron readings (e.g. 80 + 90 or 70 + 90) from one green lot, blended to test whether color-paired layering outperforms a single mid-color roast. Daiki Hatakeyama 2025 pattern (Agtron 80 sweet aroma + Agtron 90 sweet taste, 1:1).
- **Same-green rest split** (rest-window blend) — two rest windows on the same roast (e.g. 14 days + 3 days) blended to test rest-window layering. Barry McGeehin 2025 pattern (14 days Stronghold + 3 days Link, same coffee).

**Green-quantity gating**: 2000g+ remaining = full 3-batch ladder + blend matrix; 1000-2000g = ladder but meaningful fraction of green; 500-1000g = tight, consider 2-batch (A vs C) split; <500g = single reference roast only, defer blending to a future lot. Operationalized via `list_roest_inventory` MCP Tool — check inventory before scoping the ladder.

**Structural overlap with V-set framing**: same-green blending is a V-set at the design level (1-4 batches from one green, each with specific intent), but the EVALUATION step diverges — V-sets evaluate batch slots against each other to designate a leading slot; same-green blending evaluates against the blend matrix to identify the best combination (which may be a blend rather than a single batch). The experiment frame's `primary_question` would name the blend hypothesis ("does layering A + B outperform either alone?"); levels_tested = the dev / drop / Maillard gradient across A / B / C. Chris's 2026-05-16 framing: "all the V-set experiment stuff that I do in that same framework... layer on perfectly together."

**Multi-origin blending is out of scope** — multi-coffee blend stagecraft (Tom Hutchins 2024 Ombligon + Gesha; Wataru Iidaka 2024 layered three-coffee Panama bed; Yamil Quino 2024 washed + anaerobic + mossto Bolivian trio) lives in Consciously not pursuing. Same-green is Latent's adapted form.

**Rest curve protocol**:
The structured rest-window experiment shape that **sequences 4 cupping events across post-roast time on one reference roast** to map the rest behavior of a specific lot. Lives in [wbc-roasting.md § Rest as a structured variable](../../docs/skills/wbc-roasting-archivist/cluster/wbc-roasting.md). The roasting-side answer to Latent's current "freeze at peak" practice having no structured peak-detection method — WBC competitors treat rest as a strategic variable (Tom Hutchins 30 hours, Aga Rojewska 2-3 days, Lakis Psomas 10-14 days, Andrea Batacchi 20+ days, Thomas Philips flash-frozen at peak), Latent doesn't yet.

**Protocol**:
- Carve out 80g from the reference roast immediately after Day 7 evaluation: 4 × 20g portions.
- Cup each portion at a sequenced rest day: **Day 4-5** (aroma, volatility, sharpness, CO2 interference, top-note brightness) — **Day 7** (canonical pourover gate, existing reference cup) — **Day 10-12** (sweetness integration, body development) — **Day 18-21** (stability, body, florals vs fade).
- Same brewer + grinder + grind setting + recipe per cupping — rest is the only variable.
- Per-cupping records: dry / wet aroma, attack, mid-palate, finish, body, clarity, integration. Cupping notes carry the rest-day-vs-Day-7 delta ("vs Day 7: tighter finish, less aromatic lift").

**Schema-friendly**: lives on the existing `cuppings` table (no schema change). Each rest-day cupping is one `cupping` row with the same `roast_id` and `cupping_date` advancing across the curve; `rest_days` derives from `cupping_date - roast_date`. The rest curve emerges as 4 cupping rows under one roast.

**Freeze decision rule** (locked 2026-05-16 via wbc-roasting.md): **freeze when two consecutive tastings show the cup is no longer improving**. Replaces the implicit "Day 7 then freeze" cadence with an evidence-based call. Records the freeze decision + driving cup notes in `roast_learnings`.

**Today's status**: Active in Experiment status (queued for next reference roast — likely CGLE-SRUME-NATURAL-2026 or GESHA CLOUDS). Not yet executed; would be the first formal rest curve in `docs/roasting/archive.md`.

**Distinct from rest behavior** (currently in `roast_learnings`) — rest behavior is the *retrospective observation* about a lot's rest pattern (peaks early / late / plateaus / declines), often free-text. Rest curve protocol is the *structured experiment* that produces that observation. The protocol is the input; rest behavior is the output.
_Avoid_: "rest experiment" (under-specified — could mean many rest-related experiments), "rest curve" without "protocol" (overloaded — rest curve is also used informally to mean any post-roast time progression; the canonical noun for the structured experiment is "Rest curve protocol"), "4-portion cupping" (descriptive but loses the structured-experiment framing), "rest schedule" (close — but schedule is the cadence; protocol is the experiment shape).
