## Header

- **Doc reworked:** `docs/skills/brewing-historian/cluster/patterns/cross-coffee-insights.md` — **80.0 KB → 54.1 KB** (clears the 80 KB Historian single-doc cap). The brewing-historian cluster dropped **157.2 KB → 143.6 KB** (condensation outweighed capsule growth). The 7 N≥3 by-cultivar capsules grew from stubs to full capsules (e.g. gesha 1.5 KB → 10.0 KB).
- **Trigger:** single-doc 80 KB cap on the Historian cluster ([doc-tripwires.md](../../architecture/doc-tripwires.md); ADR-0014 logged it "76/80, approaching" on 2026-05-23, tripped at 80.0/80 by 2026-06-03).
- **Shape used:** **NOT the `split` the kickoff scoped.** Chris's audio + a refactored prototype reframed the work from "split one doc into axis-siblings" into a **re-architecture of what the CCIL is for**: the CCIL holds *only* cross-anchor learnings (patterns crossing ≥2 of roast / process / cultivar / strategy / roaster / equipment / water); single-axis detail lives in its own capsule with a router pointer here. So the dominant shape is **`extract` + `re-home`** (single-axis detail flows OUT to capsules), not split-into-parallel-files. Staged 007a (this) / 007b (next).
- **Governing principle (Chris):** *uniform n-threshold graduation* — the same lifecycle governs every axis and the open-question queue. An item lives in the CCIL until it accrues enough evidence (n≥3, the threshold the capsules were originally seeded at), then graduates into its single-axis capsule and the CCIL drops to a router. The CCIL is "the home for all the crossings and the things that haven't been settled yet."
- **Judgment calls:**
  1. **Orphan cultivars stay in the CCIL (don't create capsules now).** Mokka (n=1), Mokkita (n=2), Catimor (n=2), Catuai (n=1), Rosado (n=1), Pink Bourbon (n≤2) are all sub-threshold — exactly why Wave 2 PR 2 never stubbed them. They sit in a `## Sub-Threshold Cultivar Candidates` section and graduate at n≥3. Chris deferred this call to me; the uniform principle made it unambiguous.
  2. **by-roast-level axis NOT stood up (kept in the CCIL).** The roast-overrides-process pattern is n=2 and both confirmations are Untold (same roaster); the doc itself flags "needs one non-Untold." Sub-threshold → it lives as **Active Cross-Coffee Pattern #1** (it is genuinely cross-anchor, so it belongs here on the merits regardless of count), with `by-roast-level/` listed **Proposed** in the Ownership Boundary and the non-Untold confirmation tracked in Open Questions as the graduation trigger. This slightly revised an earlier multiple-choice answer ("evidence-backed files only"); Chris confirmed "can sit within the ccil for now."
  3. **Office Brewing Notes + By-Process + un-routed cooling → a `## Pending Relocation (007b)` holding block, full fidelity.** These belong in *other* homes (equipment cluster; by-coffee-family capsules) whose migration is cross-cluster / out-of-scope for 007a. Parking them in a clearly-flagged holding block keeps the main doc reading as the new architecture while orphaning nothing — 007b cuts each block to its destination. (Chris: Office notes "live within the equipment side not the insights side"; cooling "more presentation than recipe-derivation.")
  4. **Prototype is lossy; I am not.** The handed-over prototype condensed ~50 KB of lot-level detail (recipes, temps, SWORKS dial numbers, brew IDs) into ~12 KB of abstracted rules. I adopted its *shape* (Purpose / Promotion Rule / Ownership Boundary / Read Order / Router / Active Patterns / Modifier Layer / Open Questions / Workflow) but relocated the full detail to the capsules rather than dropping it — per Chris's own line, "these are not deletions from the knowledge base, they are routing fixes."
- **Heuristic learned:** **For a compounding Historian doc at its single-doc cap, the honest shape is `re-home to capsules`, not `split into parallel siblings`.** The cap is a symptom of single-axis detail accumulating in the cross-anchor index; the fix is to push that detail to its axis capsule and keep a router. **The forced safety-ordering is capsules-first, index-last:** the by-cultivar stubs *pointed into* the CCIL rollup, so thinning the CCIL before filling them would orphan the detail. Fill the capsule at full fidelity → leave a router → the index shrinks as a consequence. **"Preserve the append target":** after the re-home, the End-of-Coffee Workflow's routing list was rewritten to name the new homes (narrowest-correct-home table), or the next Historian refresh would re-bloat the index.

## Shape-coverage note

This is the **first case where the doc's *purpose* was re-scoped, not just its size reduced** — the prune surfaced that the CCIL had been acting as a catch-all rollup rather than a true cross-anchor layer. Distinct from case 004's ownership-axis `split` (two docs + intact validator) and the CONTEXT zone split's load-surface `split`. New shape vocabulary candidate for the systematization decision: **`re-scope` / `re-home`** — reduce a doc by tightening its job definition and pushing the now-out-of-scope content to its canonical home, leaving routers. Also the first prune driven by a single-doc cap on a *compounding* (designed-to-grow) Historian doc.
