## 3. The gate + the instrument (Decisions 2-3)

### Making the PicoLot bar checkable

- **(b) Structured self-score is the standing instrument:** adopt the real WBrC score-sheet methodology as a permanent structured self-score on *everything brewed and roasted from now on* — the WBrC seven coffee components (Aroma / Flavor / Aftertaste / Acidity / Sweetness / Mouthfeel / Overall, 0-9) + descriptor-accuracy practice + **one Latent-native layered-evolving apex axis** the official sheet lacks. Kills two birds: the corpus finally gets queryable quality scores ("best coffee I've ever had" currently lives only in Chris's head — zero structured scores exist on 102 brews), and every brew becomes lived score-sheet practice.
- **(a) Blind PicoLot side-by-side runs opportunistically**, not as a standing ritual: when a genuine contender and a worthy PicoLot roast coexist, brew head-to-head, whole-arc stations recorded for both; gate cleared when the Latent cup ties-or-wins across the arc. Logistics caveats Chris named: PicoLot supply is ephemeral and small-quantity; only *some* PicoLot roasts carry the layered quality; the external benchmark is never static.
- **The freezer backlog is reframed as calibration material, not brewing inventory** — benchmark bags, compulsory-drill pool (§ 5), replication stock.

### The apex-vs-judges bet

**The apex is a hard commitment, not a hypothesis.** Chris optimizes for the cup he loves and crafts the story around it; a competitive disadvantage, if real, is accepted (for-love-not-commerce). The Q2 score data runs anyway but its role is **informational, not steering** — it measures the size of the gap being carried, it gets no vote on the target.

**The 2026 rules materially shrink the assumed divergence.** The official methodology has judges evaluate over time as the cup cools, and "Overall" explicitly includes *how the cup holds its character as it cools* — layered-evolving is close to a direct play for the Overall component. The whole-arc discipline isn't a private aesthetic to argue judges into; it is the evaluation protocol. CONTEXT-taste.md's framing ("deliberately not the profile the community gravitates toward") predates this rules read; the divergence is smaller than the substrate implies, though the clean/tea-like stereotype persists in Chris's (non-competition-calibrated) peer circle.

## 5. The performance layer (Decision 5)

The system is world-class at exactly one of the three things that win (cup quality); descriptors and consistency-under-clock are trained by rehearsal, which has no home in the current cadence. Three rituals, by cost:

1. **Descriptor-accuracy practice — starts now, nearly free.** At brief time, write the descriptors the cup is expected to show *before* tasting; score the match after on the sheet. This is the Coffee Brief "expected half" already on the roadmap, now with a competition-grade reason to exist. **Kim as a second palate**: does she taste what Chris says? (Acknowledged: feels ridiculous in a home kitchen; committed anyway.)
3. **Compulsory drills — freezer backlog as the unknown-coffee pool.** A bag not brewed in months, 7 minutes, three uniform cups, no archive lookup. **WBC-recipe replication folds in here** (Decision: no fourth ritual) — same session, run a champion recipe from the 154-corpus against the same coffee. Plus the everyday habit: **borrow strong champion components into regular brews of new coffees as technique reps**, even when it isn't "the best recipe" for that coffee — extends the existing off-register-purchased training-ground doctrine ([CONTEXT-taste.md § Brewing philosophy](CONTEXT-taste.md)).

## 9. Homework brief B — judging / shadowing pathway (Chris, parallel)

The formal ladder ([JSP](https://wcc.coffee/judge-skills), [certification](https://wcc.coffee/judge-certification), [SCA announcement](https://sca.coffee/sca-news/2024/10/16/learn-the-skills-to-become-a-wcc-certified-judge)):

1. **WCC Judge Skills Program (JSP)** — local workshop run by certified WCC Reps: judging criteria, scoring methods, evaluation systems, current rules, CVA logic; yields a WCC+SCA digital certificate. **From 2027 it is the prerequisite for WCC Judge Certification.** Open to competitors and enthusiasts — no experience gate. *Action: find a US/West-Coast JSP session (schools like [Timberline](https://timberline.coffee/products/wcc-judge-skills-program) run them) and take it in the 2027 observe year — one day, low cost, and it doubles as competitor intel on exactly how judges are calibrated.*
2. **WCC Judge Certification** — two-day written + practical test, valid 3 years, discipline-specific (Brewers Cup is one track). **Eligibility gate**: judging experience at one *world* championship OR **two years judging the same competition at national level**, within the last 5 years. So the realistic ramp is national-level first.
3. **National-level judging ramp** — volunteer at US CoffeeChamps qualifiers (same Feb registration window as competitors), then shadow/apprentice judge before sensory-judging. This is the 2027 "seriously judging" path if the competitor entry waits for the taste gate.
4. **Cup of Excellence invitation — accept**, with eyes open: CoE is a different organization and instrument (CVA-style cupping, not WBrC brew service), so it does *not* count toward WCC judge-cert eligibility — but it is the highest-density calibrated-palate exposure available and trains the instrument that every path here shares.
5. **The conflict to verify before committing either way**: judging and competing in the same discipline/season generally conflict (judge conflict-of-interest rules). If 2028 becomes a *competing* year, the judging track pauses; confirm the exact US CoffeeChamps policy so the 2027 choices don't accidentally foreclose the 2028 entry.
