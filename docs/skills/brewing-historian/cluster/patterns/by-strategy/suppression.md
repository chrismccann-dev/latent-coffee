# Coffees That Confirmed Suppression

*Coffee Research · Latent · Brewing Historian cluster · by-strategy*

**Strategy definition:** see [BREWING.md § Axis 1 - Extraction Strategy](docs/skills/coordinator/catalog.md#brewing-domain-principles) for the canonical Suppression definition (mechanics: lower temperature 88-92°C primary lever, coarser grind 6.7-6.5, low agitation, low-moderate ppm water, often pulse-pour with kettle held off-base or actively cooled; intent: deliberately limit extraction on coffees with more expressive material than is desirable to fully extract).

Migrated from [BREWING.md § Cross-Coffee Insight Layer > Coffees That Confirmed Suppression](BREWING.md) in Wave 2 PR 2 (2026-05-26) per [ADR-0011](docs/adr/0011-composable-sub-skills-architecture.md).

---

These coffee types consistently required *holding extraction back* - temperature primacy as the resolving lever, with the underlying logic being "this coffee has more than is desirable to fully extract." Mechanically resemble Clarity-First but with the explicit intent of suppressing fermentation harshness rather than preserving delicate transparency.

- Colombian natural Gesha with anaerobic fermentation (e.g. Hydrangea Finca Inmaculada Gesha Natural, Valle del Cauca) - confirmed Suppression. EG-1 6.4, 93°C, Kalita Wave 155 + xBloom Premium Paper. Bitter finish is temperature-driven, not grind-driven - dropping temp from 95°C to 93°C resolved it. Coarsening grind was the wrong lever. Cup peaks cool; evaluate below 50°C. Reclassified from "Balanced Intensity" in v8.0 - the original entry had the right mechanics but the wrong named intent. The brew is not pushing extraction up from Clarity-First; it is holding extraction back from where Balanced would otherwise place it.

- Ethiopian landrace anaerobic natural (e.g. Hydrangea Basha Bekele Kokose, 74158, Sidama Bensa, 2,250 masl) - confirmed Suppression at light end. EG-1 6.5 / 92°C, Orea Glass + Sibarist Fast Flat S, 15g / 240g (1:16). Same temperature-primacy pattern as Finca Inmaculada - extends the rule across origins (Colombia -> Ethiopia), confirming the pattern is process-driven rather than terroir-driven. At 93°C, bitter/drying tail disconnected attack from body; dropping to 92°C resolved it cleanly while preserving fruit. Grind ceiling at 92°C is 6.5; 6.4 over-extracted preferentially (roast character, oversteeped tea, loss of fruit clarity) without adding body. Body is variety-intrinsic tea-like; do not chase weight via grind. Evaluation window wider than Finca Inmaculada - integration present from serving temp, not just post-50°C. Reclassified from "Balanced Intensity at light end" in v8.0.

- Panama Gesha NASD (Natural Anaerobic Slow Dry) from Altieri Family / Mima Estate (e.g. Picolot Comp Edition Altieri Gesha CHOMBI Natural Dry Fermentation, Alto Quiel, 1800 masl) - confirmed Suppression at the office. EG-1 6.5 / 92°C / SWORKS Bottomless Dripper Dial 0 closed bloom (20s) -> Dial 6 Half-Open through main pours / xBloom Premium Paper / 15g / 240g (1:16). Three findings: (1) **Anaerobic-natural pattern extends to a third origin (Panama) and to a third process variant** ("Natural Dry Fermentation" = sealed-bag low-moisture ferment phase between two raised-bed drying stages = Altieri's published NASD signature). Pattern is now confirmed across Colombia (Inmaculada), Ethiopia (Basha Bekele), and Panama (Altieri); process-driven, not terroir-driven. (2) **Kettle-on-base at 92°C cupped clean with no bitter or drying tail** - kettle-off natural decline is not a hard requirement for Suppression at the lower end of the temp band. This lot is less temperature-sensitive than Inmaculada (which required 93°C -> 92°C drop) and comparable to Basha Bekele. (3) **SWORKS Dial 6 (Half-Open) through main pours is the canonical valve position for anaerobic-natural Suppression at the office** - distinct from Dial 5 Restricted slow/slow/open used for yeast-anaerobic Hybrid Sequential lots (Janson 1010). Dial 6 holds extraction back without building immersion-like contact time; Dial 5 would push toward Hybrid Sequential territory. Establishes a separable office Suppression template from the yeast-anaerobic Hybrid template on the same brewer. Cooling mode is transformation, not integration: brown-tea body warm replaced by cherry on the body cool, body heavier/silkier on cooling rather than thinner. Variety transparency holds through Suppression on transparency-driven Gesha at 1800m. Reference recipe for Panama Gesha NASD on Picolot S7X at the office.

- Cold-room dehydration natural with Ethiopian heirloom green (e.g. Picolot Simba Comp Edition Ethiopia Heirloom Cold Room Natural, Gissell & Lily Garrido / Garrido family Panama facility, S7X) - confirmed Suppression at the office. EG-1 6.5 / 93°C kettle-on / SWORKS Bottomless Dripper Dial 0 closed bloom (20s) -> Dial 6 Half-Open through main pours, no open finish / xBloom Premium Paper / 15g / 240g (1:16) / 3:30-4:00. Three findings: (1) **Cold-room dehydration sits in the Suppression strategy lane** alongside ASD anaerobic naturals - the drying-modifier intervention concentrates body and tannic structure in a way that wants extraction held back rather than pushed, even without anaerobic fermentation flagged on the bag. Extends the Suppression pattern beyond fermentation-driven naturals to drying-modifier-driven naturals. (2) **Cold-room dehydration has narrower valve tolerance than Altieri NASD** - Dial 5/5/6 at 92°C over-extracted on Brew 2 (grapefruit + roast attack + phenolic bitter aftertaste at warm window) while still leaving the under-extracted finish from Brew 1 (Dial 6/6/6 at 92°C, grassy/raw tail) unresolved. Selectivity ceiling not yield ceiling - more contact time pulled bitter compounds ahead of finish-clarity compounds. Establishes that 'Suppression lane' is not monolithic on the valve side: Altieri NASD tolerates Dial 6 cleanly but cold-room naturals cannot extend past it. (3) **Temperature is the higher-leverage finishing lever on this process** - +1°C at fixed Dial 6/6/6 (Brew 3 at 93°C vs Brew 1 at 92°C) cleared the grassy/raw finish that Dial 5/5/6 (Brew 2) could not, brought out aroma for the first time across all three brews, and landed body in syrupy-expressive territory. Small drying tannic edge on finish reads as lot-level character of cold-room concentration, not extraction defect. Cooling discipline: 50-45°C peak; cooled past 40°C surfaces tannic edge more sharply (evaluation window narrower than other Picolot lots).

**Suppression default rule.** Anaerobic naturals AND cold-room dehydration naturals where bitter/drying tail does not resolve at Clarity-First temperatures and resolves with a temperature drop (or with a +1°C push from base 92°C to 93°C, on drying-modifier-driven lots specifically) - that is Suppression, not Balanced Intensity. Four data points now: Colombia (Inmaculada), Ethiopia (Basha Bekele), Panama (Altieri NASD), Ethiopia/Panama-processed (Picolot Simba Cold Room). Pattern is process-driven, not terroir-driven, AND now demonstrates that Suppression covers fermentation-driven AND drying-modifier-driven processes. **Valve-tolerance sub-rule for office SWORKS Suppression:** Anaerobic-natural lots (Altieri NASD) tolerate Dial 6 cleanly and resolve at 92°C kettle-on. Cold-room dehydration lots (Picolot Simba) want Dial 6 maximum and benefit from +1°C to 93°C kettle-on. Do not push past Dial 6 on cold-room lots - pulls bitter ahead of clarity (selectivity ceiling, not yield ceiling). If a fifth anaerobic or drying-modifier natural arrives that does not resolve at Suppression with these valve+temp ranges, the experimental fallback is **Balanced Intensity + Thermal Staging** (88°C -> 94°C across two phases) - not Balanced at higher temp, which has historically over-extracted on this process.
