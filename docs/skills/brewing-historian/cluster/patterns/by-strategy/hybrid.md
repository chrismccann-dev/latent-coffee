# Coffees That Confirmed Hybrid

*Coffee Research · Latent · Brewing Historian cluster · by-strategy*

**Strategy definition:** see [BREWING.md § Axis 1 - Extraction Strategy](../../../../../../BREWING.md#axis-1---extraction-strategy) for the canonical Hybrid definition (used when the brew is structured around phase boundaries with different jobs assigned to each phase, rather than a single extraction logic running throughout). Hybrid is the 6th first-class strategy (promoted v8.4, 2026-05-06) and ships with 5 sub-forms: Sequential / Phase-Mapped / Selective Bloom / Intensity-Clarity Split / Temperature-Staged. Hardware: Hario Switch at home (canonical) or SWORKS Bottomless Dripper at office when valve transitions are doing strategic work.

Migrated from [BREWING.md § Cross-Coffee Insight Layer > Coffees That Confirmed Hybrid](../../../../../../BREWING.md#coffees-that-confirmed-hybrid) in Wave 2 PR 2 (2026-05-26) per [ADR-0011](../../../../../adr/0011-composable-sub-skills-architecture.md).

---

Hybrid was promoted to the 6th first-class strategy in v8.4 (2026-05-06). Three brews previously archived as "Balanced Intensity + Immersion modifier" reclassified to **Hybrid (Sequential)** at promotion - all three are SWORKS slow/slow/open recipes (closed bloom + restricted main pours + Dial 7 open finish "to rinse rather than steep") that fit the Sequential sub-form's "immersion phase then percolation phase, each doing one job" definition. The 4 other sub-forms (Phase-Mapped, Selective Bloom, Intensity-Clarity Split, Temperature-Staged) are empty at promotion - candidate experiments below.

Confirmed Hybrid (Sequential):

- *Picolot Comp Edition Janson Green-Tip Gesha Anaerobic 1010*. SWORKS Bottomless Dripper. Dial 0 closed bloom (20s), Dial 5 restricted Pours 1-2 (aromatic-extraction window), Dial 7 open final pour (rinse, not steep). Slow/slow/open inversion of Picolot's usual fast/fast/slow house structure - the inversion is what makes this a Sequential Hybrid rather than a standard Balanced Intensity. The Dial 7 finish is doing a different job from the Dial 5 main pours: cutting tannic/over-extracted finish without compromising body that the immersion-like restricted main pours built. Reference recipe for heavy yeast-anaerobic naturals on the SWORKS at the office.

- *Sebastian Ramirez White Honey Gesha (Moonwake)*. SWORKS slow/slow/open same as Janson 1010 - restricted main pours through immersion-like contact window + opened finish to drain. Reference confirmed across two yeast-inoculated Geshas (this lot + Janson 1010), suggesting the Sequential Hybrid template is the right fit for SWORKS yeast-inoculated lots when contact-time control matters more than intensity tuning.

- *Finca La Reserva Gesha Anaerobic Honey (Colibri)*. Same SWORKS slow/slow/open template - Dial 0 closed bloom, Dial 5 restricted Pour 1, crack to Dial 6 (Half-Open) once bed drops in Pour 2. Aromatic floral extraction (jasmine, lavender) preserved by the immersion-like contact phase; sweetness integration via the controlled drawdown finish. Three data points now suggest this is the canonical Sequential Hybrid template for the SWORKS: anaerobic-natural / yeast-anaerobic-honey / anaerobic-honey lots all converge on the same valve sequence.

Candidate (untested) - other sub-forms:

- **Hybrid (Phase-Mapped) re-framing of the Tamarind reference.** Take the existing *Moonwake El Eden Tamarind Washed* Full Expression recipe (Dial 5 through main pours, crack to Dial 6 midway through Pour 3) and re-document it as Phase-Mapped: Pour 1 = saturation, Pour 2 = body building (near-immersion), Pour 3 transition = clarity finish. Same execution; sharper framing. Hypothesis: explicit role-mapping at brief time surfaces failure modes (premature transition → thin body) earlier than implicit valve-state framing did. Compare iteration efficiency on the next heavy co-ferment lot.

- **Hybrid (Selective Bloom) on aromatic Clarity-First lot.** Eline Ferket 2025 pattern. Bloom liquid separated from main brew, evaluated independently, recombined or discarded. Highest-leverage candidate: Esmeralda climate-controlled natural or clean washed Gesha where bloom likely carries different aromatic character from main extraction.

- **Hybrid (Intensity-Clarity Split) — FIRST CONFIRMED 2026-05-22 on Untold Coffee Lab Brazil Fazenda Um Wush Wush Natural (WWNAT).** Original goal stated: structured extraction without muddiness on a heavy co-ferment lot; the originally-anticipated trigger was the next heavy anaerobic washed Colombian Gesha (Jeferson Motta family). **Actual first trigger was a roast-character problem, not a co-ferment problem** — a medium-roast clean controlled natural (Wush Wush variety) where Balanced Intensity amplified the developed-roast solubles into a punishing oversteeped-black-tea wall. The Intensity-Clarity Split structure (closed immersion → fast open percolation drain) relocated the cup entirely: immersion reaches the buried prune/cacao sweetness; the fast open drawdown drains the heavy roasty tail rather than steeping it in. Locked recipe: Hario Switch Glass + Hario V60 Meteor Filter 02, 15g / 240g (1:16), EG-1 6.3, 90°C kettle off base, Phase 1 closed immersion 45g bloom→25s→fill to 240g by ~0:55→steep closed to 1:20, Phase 2 open percolation 1:20→drain undisturbed. **Counterintuitive lever:** coarsen the bed (6.0→6.3) so the open phase drains as a true fast rinse — on Hybrid the open phase is a rinse, not an extraction (this generalizes from the SWORKS "open to rinse not steep" framing). **Sub-form purpose broadened by this first data point:** from "co-ferment muddiness suppression" to "roast-tail suppression + buried-sweetness reach" — the same architecture suppresses any over-expressive heavy register (process-derived OR roast-derived) while the immersion reaches what's buried beneath it. Future candidate triggers expand accordingly: the original heavy anaerobic washed Colombian Gesha (Jeferson Motta family) is still untested; other medium-roast specialty naturals are now also candidate triggers (the 2-3 confirmation set for the operational-guide medium-roast hook → CCIL promotion threshold per `docs/grilling-queue.md` item 15).

- **Hybrid (Temperature-Staged) on a coffee that reads thin at 95°C and bitter at 96°C.** Phase boundary coincides with temperature change. Distinct from a standalone Inverted Temperature Staging modifier because the temperature change is bound to the phase boundary.

> The Picolot Emerald PL#015 Mokka Natural reference recipe is **NOT classified as Hybrid** despite its Dial 0 / Dial 7 / Dial 7 / Dial 5 fast/fast/slow valve work. The bloom-immersion phase and the slow restricted finish are recipe details (already documented in Pour Structure / What I Learned), not phase boundaries that change extraction logic mid-brew - the brew is fundamentally Full Expression at fast-flow extraction parameters with a brief restricted finish to round out the tail. Strategy classification is about intent (Full Expression = push extraction on a clean coffee) rather than about whether any single valve transition exists.


- **Hybrid (Intensity-Clarity Split) - SECOND CONFIRMED 2026-05-26 on Special Guests El Placer Wush Wush Black Honey (Colombia, Huila).** Different trigger from the first data point, same architecture. The first (Untold Wush Wush Natural, 2026-05-22) was a medium-roast roast-tail problem. This one is a **very light roast** (WB Agtron 81.7, no roast character to fight) **heavy anaerobic honey** - the "Black Honey" label undersells a documented 96h CO2 anaerobic + dry-ferment + submerged process, so it behaves as a heavy ferment. Brew 1 ran flat Balanced Intensity (Sibarist Brewing System + CAFEC Abaca+ Cup 1, EG-1 6.4, 94°C, 15g/240g): tangerine attack but a dark-brown ginger-spice heavy-tea body that over-concentrated and flattened the tangerine as the cup cooled. The problem shape was two opposing goals - pull harder early for the buried sweet-tart tangerine, but drain fast so the ginger-spice tail does not concentrate. Intensity-Clarity Split resolved it in one pivot. Locked recipe: Hario Switch + CAFEC Abaca+ Cup 1 Cone, 15g / 240g (1:16), EG-1 6.4, 94°C flat. Phase 1 closed immersion: 45g bloom (valve closed) 40s, at 0:50 pour to 150g gentle, steep closed. Open valve at 1:40. Phase 2 open percolation: pour to 240g over ~20s, drain fast. Landed all three target notes (tangerine attack, lemongrass carrying throughout, restrained ginger finish) and held them integrated on cooling. **Two notes worth carrying forward:** (1) opening at 1:40 rather than the prescribed 1:50 was correct - the 10s-shorter steep kept the back half clean; on this sub-form the open-phase timing is the primary tail-control lever. (2) Unlike the first data point, grind was held at the Balanced Intensity 6.4 (no coarsening to 6.3) and the fast drain still worked on the Switch + Abaca+ pairing - the coarsen-for-fast-rinse move from the Untold lot was not required here, suggesting the brewer/filter flow profile can substitute for the grind move. **Generalized purpose now spans three problem shapes:** co-ferment muddiness (original hypothesis, still untested), roast-tail suppression (first confirmed), and process-derived front/back cooling-curve imbalance (this lot). The common thread is one over-expressive register that wants draining while a buried register wants reaching.

**Confirmed Hybrid (Sequential) - second roast-forward data point - 2026-05-26 - Newbery Street Jaroon Khun Lao Double Honey (Khun Lao cultivar = Catimor × SL28, Northern Thai Highlands / Chiang Mai / Khun Lao, 1,200 masl, Black Honey).** Hario Switch Glass + Sibarist FAST V60 cone, 16g/240g (1:15 poured, ~1:12.5 in cup after cut), EG-1 6.5, 92°C kettle off base. Phase 1 closed immersion: 50g bloom → fill to 160g → steep closed to 1:20. Phase 2 open percolation: open valve at 1:20, drain through fast cone, STOP at ~200g in cup (late_cut drops the roast-heavy tail). brew_id 1f7d5ad1-6655-4741-84ed-c775ca902a7e.

This is a sibling result to the Untold WWNAT Intensity-Clarity Split entry above - same diagnostic family (medium-roast clean lot where developed-roast solubles amplified into an oversteeped-black-tea wall), same architectural fix (closed immersion → fast open drain on the Switch). It resolved as **Sequential** rather than Intensity-Clarity Split because the immersion front's job was to build sweetness off the percolation clock and the open phase's job was to drain-and-cut, rather than the immersion building body for a clarity-recovery percolation. Two new things this lot adds that the WWNAT entry does not:

1. **The 'both under-developed AND over-developed at once' signal is a Hybrid trigger, not a tweak trigger.** This coffee read hollow on the attack AND heavy/roasty in the body *simultaneously* - across a 6-brew arc (Balanced Intensity → Suppression → back toward Balanced), no even-extraction lever could fix both ends, because more extraction feeds the roast-body and less starves the fruit. The simultaneous under-AND-over signal is the diagnostic that the cup cannot be reached by any single-mode extraction and needs phase separation. When you see it, pivot to Hybrid rather than spending more iterations on grind/temp/ratio. (Contrast the WWNAT trigger, which was a one-directional oversteeped-tea wall.)

2. **On the Hario Switch, Output Selection (late_cut) and the Hybrid open-drain phase are the SAME physical action** - turning the valve off IS the cut. This makes the Switch the cleanest tail-cut hardware in the kit: no swapping servers mid-drain, no guessing by eye, just close the valve at the target cup weight. Cut tighter, not later, for roast-heavy tails on a fast paper - 200g clamped the smoky-bitter finish where 210g (one brew prior) let the first of the tail through. Shortening the closed-immersion steep (1:30 → 1:20) additionally reduced smoke at the source: less of the heavy fraction goes into solution before the drain. (See also the equipment-side note on the Hario Switch Glass row in brewing-equipment-expert/operational-reference.md, and the Newbery Street water-gap entry in cross-coffee-insights.md § Office Brewing Notes.)

Roaster context: Newbery Street's Pack Katisomsakul (3rd US Brewers Cup 2024 + 2025) develops Thai coffees longer for sweetness; the published recipe used ZeroWater TDS 000 to suppress body - non-replicable zero-mineral water that was doing load-bearing suppression work (confirms the v8.5 WBC Water Strength cross-cutting pattern). Home Third Wave mix was materially better than office tap for this roast-forward lot.
