# Coffees That Confirmed Hybrid

*Coffee Research · Latent · Brewing Historian cluster · by-strategy*

**Strategy definition:** see [BREWING.md § Axis 1 - Extraction Strategy](docs/skills/coordinator/catalog.md#brewing-domain-principles) for the canonical Hybrid definition (used when the brew is structured around phase boundaries with different jobs assigned to each phase, rather than a single extraction logic running throughout). Hybrid is the 6th first-class strategy (promoted v8.4, 2026-05-06) and ships with 5 sub-forms: Sequential / Phase-Mapped / Selective Bloom / Intensity-Clarity Split / Temperature-Staged. Hardware: Hario Switch at home (canonical) or SWORKS Bottomless Dripper at office when valve transitions are doing strategic work.

Migrated from [BREWING.md § Cross-Coffee Insight Layer > Coffees That Confirmed Hybrid](docs/skills/brewing-historian/cluster/patterns/cross-coffee-insights.md) in Wave 2 PR 2 (2026-05-26) per [ADR-0011](docs/adr/0011-composable-sub-skills-architecture.md).

Hybrid was promoted to the 6th first-class strategy in v8.4 (2026-05-06). Three brews previously archived as "Balanced Intensity + Immersion modifier" reclassified to **Hybrid (Sequential)** at promotion - all three are SWORKS slow/slow/open recipes (closed bloom + restricted main pours + Dial 7 open finish "to rinse rather than steep") that fit the Sequential sub-form's "immersion phase then percolation phase, each doing one job" definition. The 4 other sub-forms (Phase-Mapped, Selective Bloom, Intensity-Clarity Split, Temperature-Staged) are empty at promotion - candidate experiments below.

Confirmed Hybrid (Sequential):

- *Picolot Comp Edition Janson Green-Tip Gesha Anaerobic 1010*. SWORKS Bottomless Dripper. Dial 0 closed bloom (20s), Dial 5 restricted Pours 1-2 (aromatic-extraction window), Dial 7 open final pour (rinse, not steep). Slow/slow/open inversion of Picolot's usual fast/fast/slow house structure - the inversion is what makes this a Sequential Hybrid rather than a standard Balanced Intensity. The Dial 7 finish is doing a different job from the Dial 5 main pours: cutting tannic/over-extracted finish without compromising body that the immersion-like restricted main pours built. Reference recipe for heavy yeast-anaerobic naturals on the SWORKS at the office.

- *Sebastian Ramirez White Honey Gesha (Moonwake)*. SWORKS slow/slow/open same as Janson 1010 - restricted main pours through immersion-like contact window + opened finish to drain. Reference confirmed across two yeast-inoculated Geshas (this lot + Janson 1010), suggesting the Sequential Hybrid template is the right fit for SWORKS yeast-inoculated lots when contact-time control matters more than intensity tuning.

- *Finca La Reserva Gesha Anaerobic Honey (Colibri)*. Same SWORKS slow/slow/open template - Dial 0 closed bloom, Dial 5 restricted Pour 1, crack to Dial 6 (Half-Open) once bed drops in Pour 2. Aromatic floral extraction (jasmine, lavender) preserved by the immersion-like contact phase; sweetness integration via the controlled drawdown finish. Three data points now suggest this is the canonical Sequential Hybrid template for the SWORKS: anaerobic-natural / yeast-anaerobic-honey / anaerobic-honey lots all converge on the same valve sequence.

Candidate (untested) - other sub-forms:

- **Hybrid (Phase-Mapped) re-framing of the Tamarind reference.** Take the existing *Moonwake El Eden Tamarind Washed* Full Expression recipe (Dial 5 through main pours, crack to Dial 6 midway through Pour 3) and re-document it as Phase-Mapped: Pour 1 = saturation, Pour 2 = body building (near-immersion), Pour 3 transition = clarity finish. Same execution; sharper framing. Hypothesis: explicit role-mapping at brief time surfaces failure modes (premature transition → thin body) earlier than implicit valve-state framing did. Compare iteration efficiency on the next heavy co-ferment lot.

- **Hybrid (Selective Bloom) on aromatic Clarity-First lot.** Eline Ferket 2025 pattern. Bloom liquid separated from main brew, evaluated independently, recombined or discarded. Highest-leverage candidate: Esmeralda climate-controlled natural or clean washed Gesha where bloom likely carries different aromatic character from main extraction.

- **Hybrid (Intensity-Clarity Split) — FIRST CONFIRMED 2026-05-22 on Untold Coffee Lab Brazil Fazenda Um Wush Wush Natural (WWNAT).** Original goal stated: structured extraction without muddiness on a heavy co-ferment lot; the originally-anticipated trigger was the next heavy anaerobic washed Colombian Gesha (Jeferson Motta family). **Actual first trigger was a roast-character problem, not a co-ferment problem** — a medium-roast clean controlled natural (Wush Wush variety) where Balanced Intensity amplified the developed-roast solubles into a punishing oversteeped-black-tea wall. The Intensity-Clarity Split structure (closed immersion → fast open percolation drain) relocated the cup entirely: immersion reaches the buried prune/cacao sweetness; the fast open drawdown drains the heavy roasty tail rather than steeping it in. Locked recipe: Hario Switch Glass + Hario V60 Meteor Filter 02, 15g / 240g (1:16), EG-1 6.3, 90°C kettle off base, Phase 1 closed immersion 45g bloom→25s→fill to 240g by ~0:55→steep closed to 1:20, Phase 2 open percolation 1:20→drain undisturbed. **Counterintuitive lever:** coarsen the bed (6.0→6.3) so the open phase drains as a true fast rinse — on Hybrid the open phase is a rinse, not an extraction (this generalizes from the SWORKS "open to rinse not steep" framing). **Sub-form purpose broadened by this first data point:** from "co-ferment muddiness suppression" to "roast-tail suppression + buried-sweetness reach" — the same architecture suppresses any over-expressive heavy register (process-derived OR roast-derived) while the immersion reaches what's buried beneath it. Future candidate triggers expand accordingly: the original heavy anaerobic washed Colombian Gesha (Jeferson Motta family) is still untested; other medium-roast specialty naturals are now also candidate triggers (the 2-3 confirmation set for the operational-guide medium-roast hook → CCIL promotion threshold per `docs/grilling-queue.md` item 15).

- **Hybrid (Temperature-Staged) - FIRST CONFIRMED 2026-06-05 on Component Coffee Colombia Terra Pink Bourbon Anaerobic Natural (Huila community lot, 8 producers; WB Agtron 80.8 light; 14hr anoxic anaerobic natural).** brew_id dc886312-1b9b-4765-9c23-0e52bf63ffeb. The originally-anticipated trigger (thin at 95°C / bitter at 96°C, a bittersweet-cliff-at-temperature) is NOT what triggered it; the actual trigger was a within-Phase-2 front/back gradient that emerged AFTER an Intensity-Clarity Split pivot had already fixed the attack. Arc: started Suppression (anaerobic-natural process signal); B1 surfaced the simultaneous under-front-AND-over-back diagnostic → pivoted to Hybrid (Intensity-Clarity Split) on the Hario Switch. The ICS open-drain on CONE FAST rinsed rather than extracted (light roast, no roast tail to flush - unlike the Untold/Newbery dark-roast cases where the fast rinse is correct), thinning the back half, so Phase 2 was converted to a SECOND closed immersion (drain P1 down, re-close, steep, drain) - steep time, not paper flow, as the body lever. Front-end then stayed slightly under through 92-93°C; 95°C fixed the attack (temperature is the front-end reach lever on anaerobic naturals) but pushed the back half into a drying tail - the two phases wanted DIFFERENT temperatures. Temperature-Staged (95°C Phase 1 / 90°C Phase 2, temp change bound to the phase boundary) resolved both: hot front for reach, cooler back for a clean tail without starving front-of-body. Locked recipe: Hario Switch Glass + Sibarist CONE FAST, 15g / 240g (1:16), EG-1 6.5. Phase 1 closed immersion at 95°C: 45g bloom 40s → pour to 150g → steep closed to 1:25 → tight drain (open 1:25, re-close by 1:40). Phase 2 closed immersion at 90°C: pour to 240g at 1:40, steep 25s closed, open ~2:20 drain to finish. Total 2:40-3:05. Final cup: full raspberry/fruit-punch attack, round integrated body, clean finish, holds as a cool raspberry fruit punch with guava-tart as a feature (45-50°C peak). **Carry-forward:** (1) Temperature-Staged's real-world trigger is a two-phases-want-different-temps signal that can emerge LAYERED on top of an ICS build, not only the clean thin-hot/bitter-hotter cliff. (2) Diagnostic for tart-vs-underextraction: tart that FLATTENS on cooling = under-extraction; tart that HOLDS/sweetens on cooling = the lot's fruit character (here guava). (3) Pink Bourbon variety ceiling (transparency-driven, Balanced normally the ceiling) was overridden by the anaerobic-natural process - process-dominant; this extends the Scenery Rosado lesson (Pink Bourbon + anoxic natural also needing more than the variety suggested) one step further to needing full phase separation.

- **Hybrid (Temperature-Staged) candidate still open:** the clean bittersweet-cliff trigger (thin at 95°C, bitter at 96°C, phase boundary coincides with a single temperature change on an otherwise even cup) remains untested as a standalone shape - the Component lot reached Temperature-Staged via an ICS pivot rather than directly, so the direct single-pivot path to Temperature-Staged is not yet confirmed.

> The Picolot Emerald PL#015 Mokka Natural reference recipe is **NOT classified as Hybrid** despite its Dial 0 / Dial 7 / Dial 7 / Dial 5 fast/fast/slow valve work. The bloom-immersion phase and the slow restricted finish are recipe details (already documented in Pour Structure / What I Learned), not phase boundaries that change extraction logic mid-brew - the brew is fundamentally Full Expression at fast-flow extraction parameters with a brief restricted finish to round out the tail. Strategy classification is about intent (Full Expression = push extraction on a clean coffee) rather than about whether any single valve transition exists.


- **Hybrid (Intensity-Clarity Split) - SECOND CONFIRMED 2026-05-26 on Special Guests El Placer Wush Wush Black Honey (Colombia, Huila).** Different trigger from the first data point, same architecture. The first (Untold Wush Wush Natural, 2026-05-22) was a medium-roast roast-tail problem. This one is a **very light roast** (WB Agtron 81.7, no roast character to fight) **heavy anaerobic honey** - the "Black Honey" label undersells a documented 96h CO2 anaerobic + dry-ferment + submerged process, so it behaves as a heavy ferment. Brew 1 ran flat Balanced Intensity (Sibarist Brewing System + CAFEC Abaca+ Cup 4, EG-1 6.4, 94°C, 15g/240g): tangerine attack but a dark-brown ginger-spice heavy-tea body that over-concentrated and flattened the tangerine as the cup cooled. The problem shape was two opposing goals - pull harder early for the buried sweet-tart tangerine, but drain fast so the ginger-spice tail does not concentrate. Intensity-Clarity Split resolved it in one pivot. Locked recipe: Hario Switch + CAFEC Abaca+ Cup 4 Cone, 15g / 240g (1:16), EG-1 6.4, 94°C flat. Phase 1 closed immersion: 45g bloom (valve closed) 40s, at 0:50 pour to 150g gentle, steep closed. Open valve at 1:40. Phase 2 open percolation: pour to 240g over ~20s, drain fast. Landed all three target notes (tangerine attack, lemongrass carrying throughout, restrained ginger finish) and held them integrated on cooling. **Two notes worth carrying forward:** (1) opening at 1:40 rather than the prescribed 1:50 was correct - the 10s-shorter steep kept the back half clean; on this sub-form the open-phase timing is the primary tail-control lever. (2) Unlike the first data point, grind was held at the Balanced Intensity 6.4 (no coarsening to 6.3) and the fast drain still worked on the Switch + Abaca+ pairing - the coarsen-for-fast-rinse move from the Untold lot was not required here, suggesting the brewer/filter flow profile can substitute for the grind move. **Generalized purpose now spans three problem shapes:** co-ferment muddiness (original hypothesis, still untested), roast-tail suppression (first confirmed), and process-derived front/back cooling-curve imbalance (this lot). The common thread is one over-expressive register that wants draining while a buried register wants reaching.

**Confirmed Hybrid (Sequential) - second roast-forward data point - 2026-05-26 - Newbery Street Jaroon Khun Lao Double Honey (Khun Lao cultivar = Catimor × SL28, Northern Thai Highlands / Chiang Mai / Khun Lao, 1,200 masl, Black Honey).** Hario Switch Glass + Sibarist FAST V60 cone, 16g/240g (1:15 poured, ~1:12.5 in cup after cut), EG-1 6.5, 92°C kettle off base. Phase 1 closed immersion: 50g bloom → fill to 160g → steep closed to 1:20. Phase 2 open percolation: open valve at 1:20, drain through fast cone, STOP at ~200g in cup (late_cut drops the roast-heavy tail). brew_id 1f7d5ad1-6655-4741-84ed-c775ca902a7e.

This is a sibling result to the Untold WWNAT Intensity-Clarity Split entry above - same diagnostic family (medium-roast clean lot where developed-roast solubles amplified into an oversteeped-black-tea wall), same architectural fix (closed immersion → fast open drain on the Switch). It resolved as **Sequential** rather than Intensity-Clarity Split because the immersion front's job was to build sweetness off the percolation clock and the open phase's job was to drain-and-cut, rather than the immersion building body for a clarity-recovery percolation. Two new things this lot adds that the WWNAT entry does not:

1. **The 'both under-developed AND over-developed at once' signal is a Hybrid trigger, not a tweak trigger.** This coffee read hollow on the attack AND heavy/roasty in the body *simultaneously* - across a 6-brew arc (Balanced Intensity → Suppression → back toward Balanced), no even-extraction lever could fix both ends, because more extraction feeds the roast-body and less starves the fruit. The simultaneous under-AND-over signal is the diagnostic that the cup cannot be reached by any single-mode extraction and needs phase separation. When you see it, pivot to Hybrid rather than spending more iterations on grind/temp/ratio. (Contrast the WWNAT trigger, which was a one-directional oversteeped-tea wall.)

2. **On the Hario Switch, Output Selection (late_cut) and the Hybrid open-drain phase are the SAME physical action** - turning the valve off IS the cut. This makes the Switch the cleanest tail-cut hardware in the kit: no swapping servers mid-drain, no guessing by eye, just close the valve at the target cup weight. Cut tighter, not later, for roast-heavy tails on a fast paper - 200g clamped the smoky-bitter finish where 210g (one brew prior) let the first of the tail through. Shortening the closed-immersion steep (1:30 → 1:20) additionally reduced smoke at the source: less of the heavy fraction goes into solution before the drain. (This entry is the canonical home for the Switch tail-cut note - the brewing-equipment-expert operational-reference.md Hario Switch row points here as of pruning case 010. See also the Newbery Street water-gap entry in cross-coffee-insights.md § Office Brewing Notes.)

Roaster context: Newbery Street's Pack Katisomsakul (3rd US Brewers Cup 2024 + 2025) develops Thai coffees longer for sweetness; the published recipe used ZeroWater TDS 000 to suppress body - non-replicable zero-mineral water that was doing load-bearing suppression work (confirms the v8.5 WBC Water Strength cross-cutting pattern). Home Third Wave mix was materially better than office tap for this roast-forward lot.

**Confirmed Hybrid (Intensity-Clarity Split) - THIRD data point - 2026-05-31 - Untold Coffee Lab Panama Janson Pacamara Natural (Hacienda 491, Los Alpes farm, Volcán Barú Highlands; Pacamara; Dark Room Dried natural).** Hario Switch Glass + Sibarist CONE FAST, 15g / 250g poured (~205g in cup after cut, ~1:13.7), EG-1 6.8, 88°C kettle off base. Phase 1 closed immersion: 45g bloom (brief open at 0:25 to seat bed, re-close at 0:50) → pour closed to 140g → steep closed to 1:20. Phase 2 open percolation: open valve at 1:20, pour to 250g over ~15s through the fast cone, CLOSE valve (late_cut) at ~205g to clamp the smoky/ashy tail. brew_id 24b39678-903d-4ebb-a5e9-e8fcb51d3c44.

This is the SECOND roast-tail trigger from Untold Coffee Lab and a direct sibling to the first Intensity-Clarity Split data point (Untold Brazil Wush Wush Natural WWNAT, 2026-05-22) - same diagnostic family (a medium/dark-roast clean lot where developed-roast solubles amplify into a punishing oversteeped-black-tea wall), same architectural fix (closed immersion reaches buried sweetness → fast open drain rinses the heavy roasty tail). What this lot adds:

1. **The dark-roast override is the dominant signal and can be caught at the Agtron read, before the cup.** Whole-bean Agtron 47.9 (medium-dark, visibly oily - the darkest roast in the archive) overrode BOTH the cold-room-dehydration drying flag (Dark Room Dried) AND the Pacamara-density variety signal. When a coffee reads materially darker than the light/light-medium default, roast level becomes the governing extraction lever above process and variety. Brew 1 (Orea + FLAT 2 B3, Clarity-First mechanics / suppression intent, 88°C, EG-1 6.7) produced guava/molasses on the attack but a bitter/dark/smoky/ashy body - the oversteeped-tea wall. That simultaneous 'fruit-on-attack BUT ashy-on-body' signal is the Hybrid trigger; pivot rather than iterate single-mode.

2. **The open-phase rinse needs a GENUINELY fast paper, and 'fast' on the spec sheet is not the same as fast in the cup.** Brew 2 used the Hario V60 Meteor 02 - labeled 'Fast' flow but measured ~65s (baseline), so the open phase kept extracting roast on the way down and the cup stayed bitter. Brew 3 swapped to Sibarist CONE FAST (measured 45s, genuinely the fastest cone tested) and the open phase finally drained as a true rinse. Pair the open-phase paper choice to the MEASURED drawdown, not the marketing label.

3. **Coarsen for the rinse + cut tight.** Two of the three carrying levers reinforce existing rules: coarsen the bed (6.5→6.8) so the open phase rinses rather than extracts (generalizes the 'open phase is a rinse, not an extraction' framing); and the late_cut clamps the tail tighter, not later (~205g of 250g; the separately-captured tail was confirmed overwhelmingly ashy in isolation, validating that the heavy fraction concentrates in the final drain). On the Switch the late_cut and the open-drain are the same physical action.

**Honest ceiling note carried forward:** at Agtron 47.9 the roast-derived ashiness is inherent and not fully eliminable. The Intensity-Clarity Split RELOCATES the heavy register beneath the fruit rather than removing it - 'best dark roast cup of the arc' is a relocation win, not an elimination. This is now THREE Intensity-Clarity Split confirmations (Untold WWNAT roast-tail / Special Guests El Placer process-imbalance / this Untold Pacamara roast-tail) and TWO of the three are medium/dark-roast roast-tail problems from Untold specifically - the roast-tail → Intensity-Clarity Split pattern is at the 2-3 data point threshold for the operational-guide medium-roast hook → CCIL promotion.

**Hybrid (Intensity-Clarity Split) - THIRD CONFIRMED 2026-06-03 on Latent self-roasted Mount Elgon Ladies' Lot Fully Washed (Uganda, Batch 199 - one-shot, Outcome B salvage brew).** First ICS application to an UNDERDEVELOPED cup rather than an over-expressive one. The first two data points (Untold WWNAT roast-tail; SG El Placer process front/back imbalance) both had a loud register that wanted DRAINING. This lot had no heavy tail to drain - it was structurally light (30s dev, 10% weight loss, -6.2 WB->ground delta = surface-ahead-of-core) with the producer triplet (brown sugar / orange / chocolate) present but buried under an emergent almond/nutty register and a hollow back half. So the two halves did different jobs than on the over-expressive lots: the closed immersion phase BUILT the body the cup lacked and reached the buried producer notes; the open clarity phase's job was to finish WITHOUT surfacing the grassy underdevelopment compounds a single-mode hard percolation push would pull - not to drain a heavy tail. Locked recipe: Hario Switch + CAFEC Abaca+ Cup 4 cone, 15g/240g (1:16), EG-1 6.2, 96C kettle off base. Phase 1 closed immersion: 45g bloom 40s -> fill to 160g -> steep closed to 1:45. Phase 2 open percolation: open valve at 1:45, pour to 240g, drain fully - NO cut. brew_id f5ea7303-eb62-4b7d-9da9-3fe6b34adfe1.

Two findings worth carrying forward:

1. **On an underdeveloped / hollow tail, do NOT cut - the back-half body lives in the LATE-draining fraction.** This is the inverse of the Khun Lao roast-heavy guidance (cut tighter, 200g). An accidental cut-and-recombine proved it: the 6.2 bed choked, the operator clamped the main cup at ~3:00 and caught the slow tail separately - the fast main ran thin, the slow tail carried the entire back half, and recombined was the best cup of the session. The full drawdown (even a slow ~5-min choked one) did NOT over-extract. When the tail is hollow rather than heavy, the open phase is a body-recovery drain, not a rinse-and-cut - the Switch's cut-as-valve-close is exactly the wrong instinct here.

2. **ICS is a viable recovery move for underdeveloped washed cups, not only an over-expression-suppression move.** Pushing the immersion hard (96C / 6.2 / open 1:45, up from a thin Brew 1 at 94C / 6.3 / open 1:30) lifted the producer notes clear of the almond with zero over-extraction. Sub-form purpose now spans four problem shapes: co-ferment muddiness (still untested), roast-tail suppression, process front/back imbalance, and now buried-producer-note recovery on an underdeveloped roast. The common thread still holds (one register buried, reach it via immersion) but the open half is repurposed from tail-dump to clean finish. Residual thin second-half body was the roast ceiling (not brew-fixable) - locked as the Outcome B optimized brew. Second archive data point (after Rancho Tio Emilio's Clarity-First recovery) that an underdeveloped washed one-shot CAN be partially recovered through extraction-strategy choice - here via a hard ICS push rather than a gentle Clarity-First pivot.

**Confirmed Hybrid (Intensity-Clarity Split) - FOURTH data point - 2026-06-05 - Latent (self-roasted) Bukure Natural Lot 21, Red Bourbon (Rwanda, Northern Province, Nova Washing Station; RWA-NOVA-NAT21-RB-2026; Batch 194 reference roast).** April Switch (NET-NEW, valve-equipped flat April - flag for brewers registry) + April Paper Filter (standard flat), 15g / 240g poured (~185g in cup after cut, ~1:12.3), EG-1 6.7, 91 C kettle off base. Phase 1 closed immersion: 45g bloom (valve closed) 40s -> pour closed to 150g at 0:45 -> steep closed to 1:30. Phase 2 open percolation: open valve at 1:30, pour to 240g, drain as fast rinse, CLOSE valve (late cut) at ~185g to clamp the dark-tea/roasty tail. brew_id c6b699ae-d6db-4f34-8ebb-c69727572d12.

This is the optimized daily-drinker brew for the lot's reference roast (Batch 194), and the FIRST Intensity-Clarity Split confirmed on a flat-bottom switch vehicle - the three prior data points (Untold WWNAT / SG El Placer / Untold Pacamara) were all Hario Switch cone. Same diagnostic family as the WWNAT/Pacamara siblings (a clean lot where a heavy register - here a dark-tea overhang - buries the sweet producer notes), same architectural fix (closed immersion reaches the buried honey/caramel/berry; fast open drain rinses the heavy fraction). The session started in Balanced Intensity (the SPG starting direction); two brews of Balanced Intensity + late cut left the cup subdued-front / body-centric, and the pivot to Intensity-Clarity Split relocated the cup in one move (aroma to honey/caramel/berry, sweet register forward). Three things this lot adds:

1. **Flat-bottom switch vehicle drains slower than the V60-cone Switch - compensate, don't fight it.** The flat April bed + April paper rinses slower than the Hario Switch's V60 cone, so the open phase risks steeping the dark tea on the way down. Carrying levers: coarsen (6.5 -> 6.7) so the open phase rinses rather than extracts; kettle off base so the slower drain runs cooler; clamp the late cut tight. Generalizes the 'coarsen for the rinse' rule from the Untold lots to flat-bottom switch hardware. (April FAST paper is the held-in-reserve faster-drain lever if 6.7 + off-base is not enough next time.)

2. **The late-cut clamp point is the primary back-half lever on a flat switch, and tighter beats later.** 195g let the dark-tea tail through; 185g cleaned it. The separated discard glass read much darker + roastier in isolation, confirming the heavy fraction concentrates in the final drain (same finding as Newbery 200g and Pacamara 205g). On the April Switch, as on the Hario Switch, the valve-close IS the cut.

3. **When the heavy register is one of the producer's OWN stated notes, output selection strips the over-extracted OVERHANG, it does not eliminate the note.** Black tea is the FIRST published producer note on this lot. The resolution reframe was recognizing the dark-tea body as inherent (not a defect to extract around) and using the cut to remove only the roasty/over-extracted overhang on top of it - the discard glass confirmed the roasty fraction separates out while the clean black-tea body stays. Honeycomb surfaced for the first time across the entire V1+V2+brew project as the cup cooled (4/5 producer notes now: black tea + caramel + honey + honeycomb; berry/cranberry up front; lingonberry still absent). Evaluate this lot warm-to-cool - sweetness and honeycomb build on cooling.

First East African Red Bourbon natural in the Hybrid archive. Now FOUR Intensity-Clarity Split confirmations; the dark-register-burying-sweetness -> Intensity-Clarity Split pattern is well past the 2-3 data-point threshold.

**Confirmed Hybrid (Intensity-Clarity Split) - FIFTH data point, SECOND on April Switch - 2026-06-06 - Terraform Coffee Roasters Loja Ecuador Clara Luz Sidra Natural (Servio Lenin González Jiménez, Finca Clara Luz; Southern Andean Highlands / Loja / Quilanga; Sidra; Natural; WB Agtron 62.4 Light-Medium; 2025/09 harvest).** April Hybrid Brewer (April Switch) + April Paper Filter, 15g / 250g (1:16.7), EG-1 6.4, 94°C kettle on base. Phase 1 closed immersion: 50g bloom via Melodrip 45s -> drain (0:53, ~10s) -> re-close 1:05 -> pour closed to 150g via Melodrip 18s -> steep closed to 1:43. Phase 2 open percolation: open valve 1:43, pour to 250g via Melodrip 15s, drain fully (no cut). Total 2:45-3:10. brew_id 37affc20-67ce-438d-a255-ad801afe1644.

This is the second Intensity-Clarity Split on the April Switch (after Bukure Natural Lot 21 earlier the same day) and the first ICS confirmation on a PURCHASED light-medium-roast lot (vs. the four prior ICS confirmations: two roast-tail drains from Untold, one process-imbalance from SG El Placer, one buried-producer-note recovery on the Latent Mt Elgon underdeveloped one-shot). Same architectural fix in a new diagnostic shape: closed immersion reaches a buried aromatic register that gentle percolation cannot find. Different shape from prior confirmations: this lot's burying problem was an aromatic-ceiling-limited-at-the-source signal (the lot read quiet on a third-party cupping too), not a heavy register suppressing a brighter one. The closed immersion still does the structural front-loading work; the open phase here is a clean finish (no late cut needed, no heavy tail to drain).

Four things this lot adds:

1. **The cupping-muted diagnostic is a new Intensity-Clarity Split trigger.** When a coffee reads quiet on both the brew AND a cupping (cupping = maximum-extraction reference), the cup's aromatic ceiling is at the source - parametric extraction-push (finer / hotter / more agitation) cannot find what is not there. Structural front-loading via closed-immersion-through-Pour-1 surfaces the buried-but-present register without overshooting. Generalizes ICS from "register-suppression" + "underdeveloped-recovery" to a third trigger family: aromatic-ceiling-recovery on otherwise transparent variety/process combinations. (Carried forward to cross-coffee-insights.md Pattern 7.)

2. **April Switch closed-immersion-through-Pour-1 is the canonical Intensity-Clarity Split execution on the flat-bottom switch.** Both April Switch confirmations to date (Bukure Lot 21 and this one) ran closed bloom + closed Pour 1, with the valve opening only at the Phase 1 -> Phase 2 boundary. The closed steep across 150g of water (vs. 50g on a bloom-only Selective Bloom build) is what does the structural front-loading work - the larger water volume held in immersion is what reaches the buried register. On both data points, the spec-as-drafted leaned closer to Selective Bloom (closed bloom only); the execution drifted to closed-through-Pour-1 and that drift WON both cups, suggesting the operational-guide default for ICS on the April Switch should standardize on closed-through-Pour-1 rather than closed-bloom-only.

3. **94°C kettle-on-base held flat through both phases, no Temperature-Staged escalation needed.** Unlike the Component Pink Bourbon Anaerobic Natural (which needed 95°C P1 / 90°C P2 because the two phases wanted different temperatures), this lot worked with a single flat temperature. The Agtron 62.4 (visibly darker than typical Sidra in inventory, where most Sidras live at 76-90) raised a 91°C "protect against roast" instinct on v1 - but v2 onward at 94°C surfaced no roast bleed across the cooling arc, confirming the darker-than-typical-for-variety roast did NOT mean a lower temperature ceiling. The low-temp default was suppressing fruit, not protecting against roast. Carry forward: do not assume an Agtron-based ceiling on a coffee whose variety baseline runs lighter; test 94°C and let the cup tell you.

4. **Tasting the slow-final-drip output separately during the brew is a useful diagnostic for structural experiments.** Chris caught the late drainage in a separate vessel before mixing back, confirmed not bitter, then recombined - no late_cut needed. On the four prior heavy-register ICS confirmations, separating the tail confirmed it was concentrated in roast/heavy fraction (justifying the late cut). Here, separating the tail confirmed the OPPOSITE - the slow output was within band, justifying the no-cut decision. Same diagnostic move, opposite conclusion.
