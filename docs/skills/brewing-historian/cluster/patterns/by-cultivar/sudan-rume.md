# Sudan Rume — per-cultivar brewing patterns

*Coffee Research · Latent · Brewing Historian cluster · by-cultivar*

**Corpus:** N=3 brews.

**Current rollup:** see [cross-coffee-insights.md § By Variety > Sudan Rume](../cross-coffee-insights.md#by-variety) — ancient Ethiopian landrace, Boma Plateau origin; parent of SL-28. Transparency-driven aromatic landrace with variety-intrinsic light brown-tea structure.

**Per-strategy entries:**

- [Balanced Intensity](../by-strategy/balanced-intensity.md) — CGLE Las Margaritas Sudan Rume Natural (Special Guests, Edition 0326-42) confirmed at light end. EG-1 6.5 / 91°C / April Brewer Glass + April Paper / three-pour structure.

**Vehicle-dependency rule (Sudan Rume + Thai cultivar-mix, two-data-point confirmed 2026-05-17):** SL-lineage and aromatic-landrace varieties on fast-cone vehicles (Orea Glass + Sibarist FAST or B3 Cone) produce phase-separated cups - pungent lemongrass / dominant ginger / sweetness sitting adjacent rather than woven through. April Brewer Glass + April Paper (flat bed, medium-slow drain) is the corrective vehicle. Promoted from one-data-point hypothesis to working rule with the Newbery Street Nawin Doi Chang Washed second data point. Original three Ethiopian-landrace candidates (74110/74112, SL-28, Wush Wush) remain individually untested - confirm on a lot from each before treating the pattern as fully generalized.

**Body intuition rule:** Do not chase weight via grind when aromatic integration and cooling behavior confirm full expression. Same principle as ≥1,900 masl Panama Gesha - variety-intrinsic light body is the variety, not the recipe.

**Temperature ceiling:** 91°C. At 92°C, lemongrass sharpens on cooling.

Patterns specific to Sudan Rume processing sub-types or origin variation (Boma Plateau vs Café Granja La Esperanza Colombia plantings) will accrue here as the corpus grows.
