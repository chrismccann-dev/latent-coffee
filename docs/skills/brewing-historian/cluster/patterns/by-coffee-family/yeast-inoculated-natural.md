# Yeast-Inoculated Natural — per-coffee-family brewing patterns

*Coffee Research · Latent · Brewing Historian cluster · by-coffee-family*

**Corpus:** N=3 brews on Natural base + Yeast Inoculated fermentation modifier.

**Current rollup:** default strategy + cooling discipline below; the cross-experimental context (thermal shock + yeast-inoculated → Balanced Intensity) lives in [cross-coffee-insights.md](../cross-coffee-insights.md) + sibling [yeast-inoculated-washed.md](yeast-inoculated-washed.md).

**Default strategy:** Balanced Intensity. Yeast inoculation adds enough complexity to require Balanced Intensity support even on transparency-driven varieties (Gesha, white honey processing). Don't assume Full Expression.

**Per-strategy entries:** [Balanced Intensity](../by-strategy/balanced-intensity.md) (Moonwake Peach Oolong yeast-inoculated anaerobic natural on Catimor at EG-1 6.3 / 96°C; Don Eduardo yeast-inoculated natural with washed finish on Gesha at EG-1 6.4 / 94°C).

**Cooling discipline:** Resolves best cold (~45°C and below); aromatic top is temperature-gated, not extraction-gated. Per-lot: Moonwake Peach Oolong (anaerobic natural) resolves best cold — do not evaluate before 45°C. Don Eduardo (natural with washed finish) improves meaningfully below ~50°C as fruit integrates and spikiness resolves — not extreme, 45-50°C is the accurate window.

**Honey vs anaerobic-natural finish split on the same producer/variety:** shifts temperature ceiling 1°C (honey 95°C, natural 96°C) without changing grind position. Confirmed across Olina Cai Yunnan Catimor (Peach Oolong vs Blue Iris).

Patterns specific to yeast-inoculated-natural sub-types (straight anaerobic natural vs hybrid finishes vs alcoholic-fermentation flavor signatures) will accrue here as the corpus grows.
