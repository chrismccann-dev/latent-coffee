# Double Anaerobic Washed — per-coffee-family brewing patterns

*Coffee Research · Latent · Brewing Historian cluster · by-coffee-family*

**Corpus:** N=3 brews on Washed base + Double Anaerobic fermentation modifier.

**Current rollup:** the heavy-end of the anaerobic-washed continuum (sealed-tank double-stage fermentation typically lands here); default Full Expression evidence in [by-strategy/full-expression.md](docs/skills/brewing-historian/cluster/patterns/by-strategy/full-expression.md). Heavy co-ferment washed lots that don't carry a double-anaerobic label also land here (see Heavy co-ferment below). Cross-anchor notes in [cross-coffee-insights.md](docs/skills/brewing-historian/cluster/patterns/cross-coffee-insights.md).

**Default strategy:** Full Expression (heavy fermentation logic — same as the heavy Colombian Huila/Cauca anaerobic washed pattern). Confirm with roaster guide for any double-anaerobic-washed lot before committing to a recipe; the second fermentation stage can push the cup further than single-anaerobic washed and may benefit from Output Selection (late cut) or SWORKS Dial 5 contact-time control.

## Heavy co-ferment washed

Full Expression default (heavy-ferment logic); confirm with roaster guide. **Risk:** sour if under-extracted, boozy if over-extracted. May benefit from **Output Selection (late cut)** — informally confirmed on Moonwake El Eden Tamarind Washed (Purple Caturra + Bourbon, Huila, Colombia): stopping the brew before full target weight produced a cleaner finish without compromising body.

**Cooling:** El Eden Tamarind follows the same cooling pattern as Jeferson Motta anaerobic washed — all three flavor targets (lychee, baklava, rose water) read muted or unresolved above 50°C; lychee integration happens late. Do not evaluate before 50°C. The ginger-spice finish across all brews is a tamarind process characteristic that softens and integrates on cooling but does not disappear — treat as intended complexity, not an extraction defect.
