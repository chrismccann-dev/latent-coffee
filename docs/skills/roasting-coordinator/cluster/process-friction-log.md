# Process-friction log (append-only)

The cross-lot, cross-session friction log for the Coordinator / V-Set Assistant workflow itself - the meta-layer mirror of the Roasting Brief. The Brief holds what we learned about the COFFEE; this log holds what we learned about the PROCESS (role boundaries that chafed, packets that carried too little or too much, reconstruct steps that failed, gates that fired wrong, anything the architecture made harder than it should be).

Why a file and not a meta-thread: sessions are transient by design (Model B, [ADR-0024 § 2](docs/adr/0024-lot-coordinator-claude-code-native.md)) - a standing feedback thread would rebuild the exact long-lived-session bloat this architecture escaped. Persistent artifact, transient sessions, same trick as the Brief. Instituted as a standing mechanism (not dogfood-only) at dogfood kickoff, 2026-06-11, Chris-ratified: "always good to record any friction points at each turn so each session can improve itself over time."

## Discipline

- **Who writes:** Coordinator sessions append directly, at every natural break (same write-discipline as the Brief). V-Set Assistants do NOT edit this file (role discipline - no doc edits); they report friction in the optional `process friction` line of the Results Packet (see [packet-shapes.md](docs/skills/roasting-coordinator/cluster/packet-shapes.md)), and the consuming Coordinator transcribes it here with attribution.
- **What qualifies:** friction about the WORKFLOW, not the coffee. "The Handoff Packet didn't tell me X" belongs here; "this natural needs less early energy" belongs in the Brief / DB.
- **Entry shape:** one dated bullet - `- **YYYY-MM-DD · <lot-slug> · <role>** - <friction, 1-3 sentences>. <optional: proposed fix>.` Append at the bottom of § Entries. Never edit or remove prior entries.
- **Graduation:** at each lot-close retro the Coordinator scans this log; a friction recurring across lots graduates into a cluster primitive / SKILL.md edit at the N=3 threshold ([ADR-0022](docs/adr/0022-formalization-tax-and-self-improvement-counterbalance.md)); the anti-lawyer-redline safeguard applies (make the skill better, not bigger - [ADR-0023](docs/adr/0023-self-improving-skill-loop.md)). Graduated entries get a trailing `→ graduated: <where>` annotation rather than deletion.
