## Critical V1 architectural finding

FC arrives at 204-206°C across all three peak inlet levels. **Higher peak pulls FC TIME earlier but does NOT pull FC TEMP lower.** Same architectural failure pattern as Sudan Rume Natural V2 reproduced on a different cultivar / different lot / different terroir. **Propagated to [cross-coffee-insights.md § FC-Temp Architectural Constraint on Naturals](../patterns/cross-coffee-insights.md#fc-temp-architectural-constraint-on-naturals).**

## V2 design BLOCKED on cup-vs-reference calibration

The cup-vs-structure inversion + absent producer-note descriptors mean V1 data alone cannot distinguish:

(a) coffee genuinely prefers low-energy long-Maillard (SR Natural V3/V4 architecture transfers despite cultivar difference), OR
(b) all three batches reached the wrong region of the response surface and Chris ranked by least-objectionable.

**Cup the Untold paired roasted reference BEFORE V2 design** to resolve which region this coffee actually wants.

## V2 hypothesis paths to evaluate after Untold-reference cupping

- **Path 1, higher-information:** Lower peak floor across spread (240/242/244°C), hard post-peak cliff, bean-temp end condition — direct SR Natural V3/V4 architectural transfer, accepts Maillard 50%+ and extended total time as deliberate.
- **Path 2, lower-risk:** Hold peak at 245°C, restructure early ramp (00:00 to 01:15) to push FC arrival to 200-202°C — isolates the architectural FC-temp variable.
- **Path 3, control experiment:** Replicate v1a's 245°C peak twice with marginal early-ramp variation to confirm the 'cup-vs-structure inversion' isn't single-batch noise.

Path selection contingent on Untold-reference comparison.
