## The arc (V1 → V4)

Four V-sets. The lot's defining lesson: this heavy-anaerobic Gesha wanted markedly MORE core development than its washed anchor predicted, and the lever that delivered it was the **bean-temp end-condition target (drop point) on a fixed cooler-peak curve** - not peak inlet (the V1/V3 search lever, resolved at 250°C) and not post-peak decline shape (the V2 lever, refuted).

- **V1 (242/247/252°C peak sweep):** #162 won the Day 7 pourover gate (cupping table picked #163; pourover reversed it). Tightest WB-to-Ground delta (+2.5) tracked the winning cup. Surface Agtron ran unusually high (84-91 WB) vs the Sudan Rume Washed range - coffee-specific surface signature; delta tracked internal development, surface Agtron alone did not.
- **V2 (post-peak decline sweep):** universally underdeveloped. Gentle-decline hypothesis refuted - v2b (middle of spread) was the worst cup. But #172 (v2c) at a UFO Extraction Push real pourover surfaced producer notes (tangerine/rose/raspberry-kiwi) for the first time on the lot → confirmed the aggressive direction. Adopted bean-temp end condition (dev-timer overshoots on silent FC).
- **V3 (250/252/254°C micro-sweep, 209°C bean-temp end condition):** v3c (250°C, cooler peak) won the xBloom gate AND the Simulated Pourover Gate, but read **not-fully-developed** at real pourover - extra extraction muddied rather than cleaned (under-development, not under-extraction). The 209°C target fired ~4:10-4:20, right behind FC, forcing manual holds on all three. v3b (191) was an xBloom finalist that **collapsed to body-centric dark-tea at the SPG** - the gate-flatters-surface lesson. SPG also confirmed the rose/drying tail is a **brew artifact** (Hario Switch late cut removes it; cut-off fraction was the bitterness source) - not a roast defect.
- **V4 (211/212/213°C bean-temp end-condition sweep, held v3c curve):** **Batch 200 (V4A) = reference roast.** First clean auto-drop on the lot (211°C / 5:09, dev 34s, Maillard 50.2%, WB 82.9, WB→Gnd delta +7.3, FC 4:35/209.2°C audible 13 cracks). Landed the producer notes most completely of any batch across four V-sets - sweet/bright, raspberry/cherry/plum, lemongrass, rose, tangerine on cooling, supportive body. 201 (manual pull, anomalous 24-crack/45.5%-Maillard/53s-dev phase) read flat-harsh/disintegrated; 202 (darkest bean WB 79.4, stall-prevention drop) read flat-off. Both sit just past the 200 sweet spot. NOTE: V4 effectively tested ~211°C three times (auto / manual / stall-prevention), not the designed 211/212/213°C sweep - the higher targets wouldn't fire cleanly before the time ceiling on this curve.

- Why it won: the first roast to deliver sufficient core development cleanly via a clean auto-drop (not a manual rescue), at the cooler peak that preserves complexity. The developed-and-clean target v3c was reaching for.

## Optimized brew

- **brew_id:** 98a46183-f57f-4918-aec7-55af7f829806 · Temperature-Staged Hybrid on the Hario Switch (CONE FAST), two-kettle: 96°C intensity / ~88°C clarity, EG-1 5.6, 1:15, ~185g hot / ~40g cool split, total 2:40.
- Confirmed Batch 200 holds at REAL pourover (did NOT slide into the 191 body-centric/dark-tea failure). The late cut carried from the V3 SPG became unnecessary once the dual-temp split was in place. Phase balance (~185/40 hot/cool) is the load-bearing lever; off-base single-temp decline under-extracted.

## Carry-forward (for the next heavy-anaerobic / floral natural)

- **Primary lever:** bean-temp end-condition target on a fixed curve. Set it ~3-4°C above observed FC temp so the auto-drop extends the post-FC window rather than firing right behind FC.
- **Roast window:** Narrow. Peak pinned at 250°C (254°C produced the lightest, least-developed bean and lost at SPG; upward peak headroom is dead).
- **Underdev cup signal:** nutty / dark-brown-tea / body-centric, florals suppressed under body, hollow finish; worst case "underexpressed and overexpressed at the same time." Roast correlate: short post-FC dev (<~20s) REGARDLESS of surface Agtron (underdeveloped V2 cups had the LIGHTEST WB, 94.7-95.4) - use dev time / time-at-FC, not Agtron, as the development proxy on this process family.
- **Overdev cup signal:** flat-but-harsh/disintegrated OR muted/flat-off (loss of integration + brightness, not roasty/ashy). Roast correlate: ground Agtron into the high-60s with WB→Gnd delta past ~+10 on the dark-grounds side (201: ground 68.6, delta +13.2 - widest on the lot, worst cup).
- **WB→Gnd delta sign matters:** wide delta with LIGHT grounds is the lot signature and non-disqualifying (+7/+9 winning cups); wide delta with DARK grounds is over-development. Magnitude alone doesn't discriminate.
- **Evaluation:** Day 7-8 xBloom gate is directionally useful but FLATTERS the surface on this process family - confirm the leading slot at a real-pourover gate (SPG or optimized-brew dial-in) before the reference call.
- **Brew:** Intensity-Clarity Split / Temperature-Staged on the Hario Switch; the drying tail is a brew artifact (mechanical late-cut fix), do NOT design the roast against it.

## Cross-lot framing

- Corroborates the xBloom-dark-tea-ceiling / gate-misranking pattern seen on COS-HIG-BOR-2026 (Higuito) and RWA-NOVA-NAT21-RB-2026 (Bukure) - three lots now where the xBloom gate under-discriminates on dark-tea-prone naturals.
- Extends the heavy-ferment "worst roast metrics → best cup" inversion (Mandela XO, Sudan Rume Natural) to a fourth process family.
- Open cross-lot question carried forward: does the bean-temp-target-above-FC mechanism + wide-delta-with-light-grounds signature generalize across ALL heavy-anaerobic naturals, or is some of it Gesha-specific? Needs the next anaerobic-natural lot (Finca Deborah Geisha Natural is the queued high-stakes candidate) to test.
