## Cross-lot framing — what this lot taught the Historian

Mandela XO is the **canonical Heavy-Ferment / XO-Family lot in the archive**. Three patterns landed at lot close that propagated into [cross-coffee-insights.md](../patterns/cross-coffee-insights.md):

1. **XO-fermented drop ceiling ~205-206°C** (lower than washed high-density ~208°C). The fermentation layer has already done significant development work on the bean structure; pushing past the ceiling produces overdevelopment with cinnamon/cardamom spice-dominance.
2. **Silent FC is the rule, not the exception, for XO-process lots.** Bean-temp end condition is the categorical default; dev-time end conditions on silent FC produce Maillard overrun (V4 reached 51-58% Maillard when dev time fired at machine-estimated FC).
3. **Fan curve shape was the single most impactful variable** across the entire 13-batch / 4-experiment-set arc. Flat 90% fan → shaped curve (80→68→63→70→73%) was the V3a→V3b shift that took the cup from front-loaded-pungency to integrated-body-distribution.

The varietal aromatic fingerprint (caramelized pineapple / lemongrass / barbecue caramel / milk tea body) lives in [cross-coffee-insights.md § Varietal Aromatic Fingerprints](../patterns/cross-coffee-insights.md#varietal-aromatic-fingerprints). The "lemongrass = varietal complexity marker, NOT defect" framing is shared with Sudan Rume Natural (same CGLE farm).
