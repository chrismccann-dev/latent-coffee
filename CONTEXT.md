# Latent Coffee Research

Latent Coffee Research is Chris's personal coffee research journal. Two compounding workflows: **brewing is archive-driven** (claude.ai iterates, the app gets the final brew); **roasting is iterative** (lots evolve through experiment-rounds until a reference roast emerges). This glossary captures Latent-specific terminology — terms whose meaning is non-obvious to a first-time reader of the codebase or the foundational docs.

Single context (no `CONTEXT-MAP.md`). Grown via `/grill-with-docs` sessions, never bulk-authored. Implementation details live in [CLAUDE.md](CLAUDE.md), not here.

## Language

### Roasting

**V-set**:
One round of roasting experimentation on a green-bean lot, labeled `V1` / `V2` / ... and typically containing 3 recipe variants (with 1–4 as the legitimate range). Each V-set carries one primary question + an optional control baseline + one lever being tested.
_Avoid_: "experiment set", "iteration", "round", "batch series"

**Batch slot**:
One recipe variant within a V-set, labeled `a` / `b` / `c` (or sometimes `d`). The full identifier `{V-set}-{slot}` (e.g. `V1a`) is the same string carried through spreadsheet, Roest machine profile name, app records, and cupping notes — one label end-to-end.
_Avoid_: "variant", "batch", "recipe variant"

**Experiment frame**:
The design-time framing of a V-set: six fields set before any roasting happens — the primary question (what's being tested), an optional control baseline (if comparing to a prior roast), the shared constants held steady, the levels being varied, the expected outcomes at both the roast layer and the cup layer, and the failure boundary if a variable is pushed too far. Required fields are primary question, levels, and expected outcomes (both layers); shared constants and control baseline are usually given by the question; failure boundary is nice-to-have.
_Avoid_: "experimental design", "experiment setup", "V-set design", bare "frame"

**Variable**:
A roast parameter being deliberately moved across batch slots within a V-set's levels-tested (peak inlet, dev time, drop temp, charge temp, ROR shape, fan curve, etc.). Design-time term — pre-judgment, before outcomes are in. A V-set tests 1–2 variables; everything else is held still as shared constants.
_Avoid_: "knob", "dimension", "parameter" (when referring to the thing being moved), "lever" (lever is post-hoc — see below)

**Lever**:
A variable that, after roasting and cupping, turned out to produce an observable difference in the end cup — a post-hoc promotion from variable status. Subdivided into **primary lever** (the biggest difference-maker for this lot) and **secondary lever** (meaningful but smaller). A variable only earns lever status when cross-batch-slot or cross-V-set evidence supports the promotion.
_Avoid_: "key variable", "winning variable", "critical parameter"

**Non-factor**:
A variable that was tested across batch slots but produced no clear effect in the end cup. Doesn't get promoted to lever status, but the absence of effect is itself a useful lesson — recorded as a carry-forward learning.
_Avoid_: "null variable", "inert variable", "dud", "what didn't matter" (use the noun form)

**Roast→cup trace**:
The causal-attribution chain across two layers — roast (predicted → actual → delta) and cup (predicted-initial → updated-after-roast → actual → delta) — that lets observed surprises be localized to either the roast deviating from plan or the prediction itself being wrong. The whole point of comparative 3-batch V-sets is that the trace becomes tasteable across slots.
_Avoid_: "prediction trace", "delta chain", "expectation pipeline", "predict-observe loop"

**Taste-for**:
A focal-attention hint set per batch slot before cupping (one entry per slot in the experiment row) — *not* a prediction of what the cup will taste like, but a directional list of what to *listen for* on the cupping table. Carries three reference points: producer tasting notes (external ballpark check), prior-V-set tasting memory (where am I vs the last try on this lot), and the specific adjustment being tested this round (where the lever is supposed to move the cup).
_Avoid_: "cup prediction" (different slot — that's `updated_cup_prediction`), "tasting plan"

**Producer tasting notes**:
The flavor notes the producer / farm / exporter prints on the bag or website, recorded against the green-bean inventory row. Used as an external *ballpark check* during cupping ("am I in the right zone?"), never as a target — tasting is single-palate (Chris is the only taster) and the goal is calibrated self-experience, not producer-match.
_Avoid_: "producer notes" (ambiguous — could mean roast notes), "vendor description"

**Reference roast**:
The single batch slot (one roast execution in one V-set) designated at lot-close as the winning expression - the roast Chris would repeat if he had more green. Chosen by judgment call when the cup matches producer-notes ballpark + Chris's expectations + diminishing returns set in; typically locked in by running one final **control experiment** (a replicate V-set with two slight adjustments) before declaring the winner. Deliberately called "reference" not "best" - tasting is subjective and there is no objective best. **Distinct from leading slot**: the leading slot is V-set-scoped (winner of one V-set's batch-slot comparison); the reference roast is lot-scoped (one per lot, declared at close-out). The reference roast is typically the leading slot of the final V-set, but the concepts are not interchangeable - a control experiment V-set can confirm a leading slot from a prior V-set as the reference roast, in which case the leading slot of the control V-set may or may not be the same.
_Avoid_: "best roast", "winning batch", "final roast", "optimal roast", "leading slot" (V-set-scoped, see separate entry)

**Leading slot**:
The winner of one V-set's batch-slot comparison - the single batch slot within V_n that came out on top at Day 7 cupping. Lives in `experiments.winner` (one per V-set). Phrased as `V<n><letter> (Batch <Roest#>)` so it's unambiguous and distinguishable from the lot-level reference roast. Changes V-set to V-set as the iteration progresses; the leading slot of V1 may or may not also be the leading slot of V2.

**Distinct from reference roast**: the leading slot is V-set-scoped. The reference roast is lot-scoped - designated exactly once at lot close-out, can be (and often is) the leading slot of the final V-set, but the two concepts must not be conflated. The lifecycle has *N* leading slots (one per V-set) and exactly 1 reference roast.
_Avoid_: "winner" (ambiguous against reference roast), "best batch", "V-set winner" (the schema column name; vocabulary preference is "leading slot")

**Reference cup**:
The xBloom pour-over cupping of the reference roast at day-7 of rest — one standardized, mechanically-repeatable evaluation that defines what this coffee tastes like at peak roasted-bean state. Replaces an earlier 2-stage protocol (cupping-bowl day-4 + xBloom day-7); the bowl methodology was deprecated because too many coffees that tasted great on the bowl table "fell apart" at the xBloom step and couldn't hold up during the optimized brew — i.e. the bowl signal had too much brewing-tolerance noise mixed in. xBloom's mechanically-consistent recipe specifically isolates cup-side signals from brewing variance, which is what makes the underdev/overdev signal attribution possible.
_Avoid_: "cupping bowl cupping" (deprecated), "tasting", "best cup", "final cup"

**Optimized brew**:
The pour-over recipe Chris dials in for daily consumption of the **reference roast** — real dripper, real filter, real water, finalized brewing variables. Produced by handing the reference roast into the brewing-side workflow (same flow used for purchased roasted beans — same iteration loop, same Coffee Brief, same Step 4 resolved-brew artifact). The **consumption-condition endpoint** of the full self-roasted pipeline; all post-hoc attribution traces backward from here, since this — not the reference cup — is what the lot actually tastes like when drunk normally. **The self-roasted variant of `reference brew`** — same workflow shape, distinct name because the word "optimized" emphasizes that this recipe pushes the bean past the constrained xBloom reference cup that was used to designate the reference roast in the first place.
_Avoid_: "optimized recipe" (recipe is one component, not the whole), "final brew", "best brew", bare "reference brew" when context is specifically self-roasted (use "optimized brew" to keep the calibration-vs-consumption distinction crisp).

**xBloom**:
A physical automated pour-over machine that runs one mechanically-consistent recipe every time (same temperature, pour pattern, agitation, ratio). Used as the **gate** for reference-cup evaluation — every reference roast gets exactly one xBloom pour-over at day-7 of rest, and that result *is* the reference cup. Not the consumption-condition cup (that's the optimized brew), but a standardized cross-lot anchor that's repeatable.
_Avoid_: "automated pour over" (xBloom is a specific machine), "pour over machine"

**Control experiment**:
A V-set whose purpose is to *lock in* a candidate reference roast rather than explore new variables — replicates the leading batch slot with two slight adjustments to confirm the winner before lot-close. Distinct from an exploratory V-set (which tests variables to discover levers).
_Avoid_: "confirmation V-set", "final V-set", "verification roast"

**Acceptable roast window**:
The range of values for the primary lever within which the cup stays in the desired zone for this lot — e.g. "dev time 25–35s, with 30s as the best point; below 25s tastes grassy, above 35s tastes roasty". The playable zone for repeating the reference roast. Narrow window = unforgiving lot; wide window = lot tolerates roast deviation.
_Avoid_: "roast window" (under-specified — the qualifier is load-bearing), "tolerance band", "playable zone", "latitude"

**Brewing tolerance**:
How well the reference roast holds up when brewing variables are pushed toward extremes (Full Expression / Suppression / Extraction Push / etc.). High brewing tolerance = the cup stays coherent across a wide brew range; low brewing tolerance = the cup "falls in on itself" when pushed. Distinct from acceptable roast window: this is *brew* deviation latitude, that is *roast* deviation latitude. A 3-axis "Roast Character" attribute alongside primary lever and acceptable roast window.
_Avoid_: "elasticity" (deprecated — physics metaphor was confusing and the polarity was ambiguous), "brew robustness", "flex", "roast resilience"

**Roasted bean characteristic**:
The attributes of the reference roast that carry through to the brewing step — its expressiveness (loud / muted), its preferred brewing direction (suits Full Expression vs Suppression vs Clarity-First, etc.), and its brewing tolerance. Determined by the roast end-state; consumed by the brewing-side workflow when dialing in the optimized brew.
_Avoid_: "roast profile" (overloaded — already means the Roest machine recipe), "bean character", "roast result", "post-roast profile"

### Lifecycle states (4-state machine per green-bean lot)

**In inventory**:
A green-bean lot that has been purchased and is physically at home but has not yet been onboarded into the V-set workflow — no experiment frame, no roast profile, no Latent app row. Tracked in the claude.ai project's inventory sheet, not in the Latent app today (deliberate scope choice — may surface later).
_Avoid_: "uncommitted", "pre-experiment", "not started", "queued", "fresh"

**Waiting for next roast**:
A green-bean lot whose latest V-set has been designed and the recipes pushed to the Roest machine, but the physical roasting hasn't happened yet. Covers both the first V-set (just-onboarded lot, V1) and mid-cycle V-sets (V_{n+1} designed by claude.ai right after V_n's cupping update). The next move is physical: Chris roasts at the machine.
_Avoid_: "ready to roast", "roast pending", "in queue"

**Waiting for next cupping**:
A green-bean lot whose latest V-set has been fully roasted but not yet xBloom-cupped at day-7 of rest. Roast data is back in the app; the next move is physical: Chris does the day-7 cupping (which then triggers claude.ai to design V_{n+1} and the lot bounces back to "Waiting for next roast"). Day-7 is the target; actual days-post-roast varies with calendar affordances and gets recorded per cupping.
_Avoid_: "ready to cup", "cupping pending"

**Resolved**:
A green-bean lot whose lifecycle is closed: a reference roast has been declared after a winning xBloom cupping, the reference roast has been handed off to the brewing-side workflow and an optimized brew has been dialed in, and all learnings have been rolled up. No further V-sets planned. Replaces the active sage-tile rendering with the green-tile + "Resolved" badge.
_Avoid_: "closed", "complete", "done", "archived", "final"

### The unit

**Lot**:
A specific green-bean purchase from one producer/farm at one point in time — uniquely identified by its `green_lot_id` (the seller's lot reference). One purchase = one lot, even when the same farm + same cultivar is bought twice in different seasons or from slightly different sub-locations on the farm: agricultural variability (moisture, density, soil, sun, processing) means the two will behave and taste differently. The unit of all roasting research in Latent.
_Avoid_: "bean", "coffee", "batch" (batch is a slot inside a V-set), "SKU", "purchase", "variety" (variety is the cultivar, not the lot)

**One-shot lot**:
A green-bean lot purchased in a single small sample (typically 100-120g) sufficient for only one roast. Origin: auction-lot sample sets where the full lot isn't yet sold, farm-direct sample sets sent during sourcing negotiations, rare allocations from limited drops. The constraint is structural, not stylistic: no iteration possible, no V2 recovery, no cross-batch variance attribution, no margin for error. Distinct from V-set lots (5-10+ batches of 100g, supporting V1 / V2 / ... / V_N iteration through the comparative pipeline). Flagged via `green_beans.is_one_shot: true` at intake (migration 054, 2026-05-15). Routes through `docs/prompts/one-shot.md` + `docs/prompts/one-shot-closeout.md` instead of the 4-prompt V-set lifecycle.
_Avoid_: "sample" (every green-bean purchase is a sample at some scale; the distinguishing feature is single-roast-capacity), "single-batch lot" (every batch slot is single-batch by definition; one-shot is single-batch-AT-LOT-LEVEL)

### Lot-close synthesis

**Lot-specific learnings**:
The full reflective synthesis of one closed lot — its character (primary lever, acceptable roast window, brewing tolerance), its variable promotions (secondary levers + non-factors), and its diagnostic + behavioral fingerprint (underdev signal, overdev signal, aromatic / structural / rest behavior). Backward-looking audit-trail, scoped to this lot. Written by claude.ai at lot-close based on the full roast→cup trace; Chris does not hand-author.
_Avoid_: "lot learnings", "audit trail", "retrospective", "lot summary"

**Carry-forward learnings**:
The compressed subset of lessons that generalize beyond this lot to future ones — what to try first when next roasting a similar cultivar, terroir, process, or general roast-style. Forward-looking, designed to shorten time-to-reference-roast on the next lot. The **compounding-knowledge primitive** of Latent: claude.ai consumes prior lots' carry-forward when designing V1 on a new lot with overlapping attributes. Four conceptual axes in Chris's mental model — cultivar takeaway, terroir takeaway, general takeaway, starting hypothesis for similar lots — though the schema today is missing the terroir takeaway field.
_Avoid_: "lessons learned" (too generic), "general learnings", "forward recipe", "roadmap"

**Closed without reference**:
A close-out state where the lot did not produce a reference-quality roast. Two paths arrive here: (1) a V-set lot exhausted its green across multiple V-sets without arriving at a roast Chris would repeat - the cup never crystallized into "yes this is the lot's voice"; (2) a one-shot lot's single attempt didn't nail it - typical when the carry-forward anchor was off-target or the design hit a tolerance edge. The lifecycle state stays `resolved` (rule (a) in `lib/lifecycle-state.ts` only requires a `roast_learnings` row exists, not that the row names a winner). The page renders normally via Sprint 3.2 #18's "Closed without reference" sub-card on ResolvedView, triggered by `roast_learnings.why_this_roast_won = NULL`. The salvageable artifact for these lots is often the **optimized brew** - dialed in to compensate for the non-ideal roast via the brewing-side workflow. The brew row's `what_i_learned` captures the compensation reasoning; that prose becomes carry-forward for future similar lots even when the roast itself wasn't reference quality.
_Avoid_: "failed lot" (pejorative; the lot still produced learning), "unresolved" (the lifecycle state IS resolved), "abandoned", "incomplete"

**Development** (roasting sense):
The whole-curve completeness of a roast — *not* the classical post-first-crack-only definition, but the full curve shape from charge → Maillard → FC → drop, *plus* the internal-vs-external uniformity measured by the Agtron delta between whole bean (WB) and ground bean (Gnd). A large WB→Gnd delta means the bean looked roasted on the outside but stalled inside; a small delta means even development.
_Avoid_: "dev time" (too narrow — that's only the post-FC slice), "roast completeness" (close, but doesn't capture WB/Gnd uniformity), "roast level" (refers to color, not completeness)

**Underdev signal**:
The **cup-side** sensory marker that appears in batches of this lot when development is too low — too-short whole-curve completeness OR uneven development (high WB→Gnd Agtron delta). Lot-specific fingerprint (grassy / hay / sour / underextracted-acidity vary by cultivar/terroir). Distinct from roast-side observations like "the roast looked stalled" (different concept).
_Avoid_: "under-roast", "stalled roast" (roast-side, not cup-side), "underextraction" (brewing concept)

**Overdev signal**:
The **cup-side** mirror — sensory marker that appears in batches of this lot when development runs too long or too hot. Lot-specific (roasty / nutty / ashy / muted / dark-chocolate-heavy vary by cultivar/terroir). Same cup-side framing as underdev signal.
_Avoid_: "over-roast", "burnt", "carbonized" (roast-side or pejorative-aesthetic)

### Cup character (currently misplaced in schema)

These describe what a cup IS when roasted correctly, distinct from diagnostic signals (which describe failure modes). Conceptually they belong on cupping records or the reference cup synthesis, not on `roast_learnings` — schema relocation is a flagged follow-up.

**Aromatic behavior**:
How a cup's aromatics present in time and intensity — immediate vs late-blooming, expressive vs muted, lifted vs grounded, sustained vs transient. Cup-side character data tied to a specific cupping (or synthesized from the reference cup), not a lot-level lesson.
_Avoid_: "aroma profile", "aromatic signature"

**Structural behavior**:
How a cup presents structurally — the shape and balance of acidity, body, and finish, separate from flavor. Cup-side character data; what the coffee tastes like when dialed in well.
_Avoid_: "mouthfeel" (too narrow — that's just body), "cup structure"

**Rest behavior**:
How a roasted lot evolves across post-roast rest days — peaks early (day-7), peaks late (day-21+), holds a long plateau, or declines sharply. Currently *not* actively used as a variable in Chris's workflow (he targets day-7 xBloom and moves on); on the long-term radar as WBC-competition work often treats rest time as an explicit lever. Sits awkwardly in `roast_learnings` today since it's not really a lesson and isn't actively measured.
_Avoid_: "rest curve" (already used elsewhere as `RoasterEntry.restCurve` — different concept), "aging behavior"

### Forward design

**Adjustment**:
The deliberate design move from V_n to V_{n+1} that changes one or more variable levels with the intent of moving the cup in a specific direction. Informed by V_n's cupping deltas + carry-forward learnings of prior lots + the producer-notes ballpark check. **Scale-dependent - concretely mapped to V-number bands**:
- **V1 (and often V2)**: wide-variance, multi-variable exploratory. Spread can be wide on multiple axes simultaneously (lower / medium / higher peak AND faster / slower decline across the same three slots). The point is to *find the response surface*, not to narrow on it. ~5°C+ peak inlet spread is fine and often correct.
- **V2 → V3**: narrow on V2's leading slot, usually single-variable. 1-2°C peak spread is typical, or replicate V2's leading slot with two slight adjustments (a control experiment). V2 stays wide-ish when V1's signal was ambiguous (still in search space).
- **V3+**: probe a NEW variable held constant in V_1…V_3 (fan curve through development, drop temp ceiling at fixed peak inlet, charge temperature, hopper pre-load), or run a control experiment to lock in the reference roast.

Override: if V_n's `open_questions` explicitly demand re-bracketing ("we don't know if the window is in this range at all"), widen the spread regardless of V number. The unit of forward design between V-sets - authored by claude.ai as part of the post-cupping update.
_Avoid_: "tweak" (too casual, implies small), "iteration" (V-set is the iteration), "variation"

**Tolerance-anchored design**:
The roast-design philosophy for one-shot lots (single batch, no iteration possible). Distinct from V1's wide-variance exploratory framing AND from V_{n+1}'s narrow-convergent framing. The anchor is the carry-forward window from similar prior lots (cultivar / terroir / process matches); the design rule is to land central in that window with deliberate margin on both sides. Concretely: NOT a super-fast / very-low-tolerance roast (no room if FC arrives late), NOT a super-long / pull-at-the-last-second roast (no room if FC arrives early), middle-of-the-road for operator grace. The goal isn't to find a response surface or to converge on a reference - it's to produce something workable on the single attempt, accepting "workable" may be less than reference quality. Pairs with the schema constraint on `roast_learnings` (migration 054) that one-shot lots cannot populate lever-attribution fields - the design philosophy and the data model both acknowledge that N=1 doesn't support variance attribution.
_Avoid_: "safe roast" (not specific enough about the carry-forward anchor), "middle path" (loses the anchor framing), "exploratory single-shot" (contradictory - one-shots aren't exploratory)

### Brewing

**Extraction Strategy**:
The named extraction logic a brew is designed around — exactly one of six canonical values (`Suppression` / `Clarity-First` / `Balanced Intensity` / `Full Expression` / `Extraction Push` / `Hybrid`) chosen at the Coffee Brief Step 1d before any equipment or parameters are decided. **Intent-defined, not mechanics-defined**: the same mechanical recipe can name different strategies depending on what the coffee needs. Suppression and Clarity-First share coarse-grind / low-temp / low-agitation mechanics but differ in intent (holding an over-expressive coffee back vs preserving a delicate one's transparency); Full Expression and Extraction Push share fine-grind / high-temp mechanics but differ on the agitation lever (push the bed vs Melodrip-controlled turbulence). Five describe extraction *intensity* (one logic running throughout the brew); the sixth (Hybrid) describes extraction *structure* (phase boundaries where the brewer changes mode mid-brew). The primary act of recipe design — equipment and parameters fall out of it.
_Avoid_: "approach", "method", "brewing strategy" (overloaded), "recipe family", bare "strategy" when context could mean anything else.

**Two-Axis Framework**:
The structural backbone of Latent brewing — every brew names exactly one position on **Axis 1 (extraction strategy)** plus zero or more **Axis 2 modifiers**. A deliberate **learning-stage compression** of the WBC five-axis brewing taxonomy (Extraction Intensity / Time Distribution / Physical System / External Control / Output Selection) into a flatter 2-axis shape that can be represented cleanly in the app: Axis 1 corresponds to WBC's Extraction Intensity axis; Axis 2 folds External Control + Output Selection + parts of Time Distribution into a single heterogeneous "modifier" slot. The compression is intentional, not aspirational — Chris isn't actively pursuing many WBC subtypes (thermal mixing, tool-assisted moves, intentional blending), and the modifier system represents those moves without committing to first-class status. WBC's Physical System axis (Structural Systems family — blend-engineering, layered beds, dual-chamber extraction) is explicitly **out of scope** for Latent's single-origin home brewing context. Several cross-cutting WBC moves (water strength / agitation taper / filter behavior / pre-brew conditioning / time-distribution templates beyond Role-Based Pulse) live as **doc-layer reference patterns** in [docs/brewing/wbc-reference.md](../../docs/brewing/wbc-reference.md), deliberately NOT promoted to canonical modifiers. **Disposition**: the framework is expected to evolve — a modifier can be **promoted** to a strategy when usage volume + cross-coffee pattern justify it (v8.4 Hybrid promotion is the canonical example), and the 2-axis shape itself may expand to 3 or 5 axes in the future as Chris's brewing scope broadens.
_Avoid_: "the framework", "the 6+4 framework" (compact but loses the compression insight), "strategy + modifier system", "the Latent brewing taxonomy".

**Modifier** (Axis 2):
An optional, stackable layered technique applied to a brew on a different mechanical axis than the chosen extraction strategy. Default empty (most brews carry none — modifiers are deliberate additions, not defaults). Four canonical types post-v8.5: `output_selection`, `inverted_temperature_staging`, `aroma_capture`, `role_based_pulse`. Heterogeneous bucket — folds 4 of WBC's 5 control axes (Time Distribution / Physical System / External Control / Output Selection) into one slot rather than giving each its own axis. The modifier system plays two roles: (1) **representation** of WBC moves Chris isn't actively pursuing, so the brew vocabulary can name them without committing to first-class status, (2) **staging ground** for techniques that may later be **promoted** to strategies. Justification required at Step 1d, even when the answer is "none" — null modifier results are signal.
_Avoid_: "Axis 2 strategy" (modifiers are not strategies), "extraction modifier" (too narrow — modifiers operate on multiple non-extraction axes), "add-on", "technique".

**Strategy promotion**:
The deliberate elevation of an Axis 2 modifier (or unrepresented WBC subtype) into an Axis 1 extraction strategy when sustained usage and cross-coffee pattern justify dedicating it a first-class slot. Judgment-based but consistent criteria: (1) repeated active use across multiple coffees, (2) the modifier is increasingly load-bearing in recipe design rather than incidental, (3) a coherent strategy intent has emerged that's distinct from the existing strategies. Canonical example: v8.4 (2026-05-06) promoted the **Immersion modifier → Hybrid strategy**, triggered by Chris's heavy SWORKS Bottomless Dripper use at the office (an immersion-with-valve system) plus selective Hario Switch use at home reaching threshold. Promotion churns existing brew records — three lots previously archived as "Balanced Intensity + Immersion modifier" reclassified to "Hybrid (Sequential)" at promotion time — so the bar is deliberately high; the modifier system carries undeveloped techniques cheaply until usage justifies the schema cost. Distinct from the roasting-side variable → lever promotion (different domain, different mechanism — same word reused).
_Avoid_: "modifier upgrade", "axis promotion", bare "promotion" without qualifier (could mean variable → lever — that's roasting-side).

**Phase**:
A discrete segment of a brew separated from adjacent segments by a **phase boundary** — the moment when the brewer's mode, valve state, agitation, or temperature changes. Typical phases include the **bloom**, each numbered **pour**, the **drawdown**, and (for Hybrid brews) the **immersion** and **percolation** legs. The unit on which Phase-Mapped Hybrid, Temperature-Staged Hybrid, Intensity-Clarity Split, and Role-Based Pulse all operate. **Two parallel labels apply to every phase** (formal split, locked 2026-05-15): a **mechanical role** (what the brewer is doing — which WBC foundational axis is being engaged) plus a **cup-side target** (which sensory attribute the phase is meant to deliver). The two labels are orthogonal — recipe-design picks both — and were collapsed into "sensory target" phrasing in BREWING.md (`saturation / body / clarity / finish`) in a way that lost the design logic.
_Avoid_: "stage" (used loosely in BREWING.md; phase is the precise term), "pour" (a pour is one kind of phase — bloom and drawdown are also phases), "step", "segment".

**Phase boundary**:
The moment within a brew when one phase ends and another begins, marked by a deliberate change in the brewer's mode (immersion ↔ percolation), valve state (closed / restricted / opened), agitation (push the bed ↔ Melodrip), temperature, or kettle position. **The defining structural feature of Hybrid strategy** — Hybrid is exactly the case where phase boundaries exist by design and change the brew's extraction logic mid-stream; the 5 intensity strategies have implicit phase boundaries (each pour ends and the next begins) but they don't change extraction logic, just continue it.
_Avoid_: "transition", "stage break", "mode change" (mode change is one *kind* of phase boundary; phase boundary is the umbrella).

**Mechanical role** (per phase):
What the brewer is mechanically doing during a given phase — which WBC foundational axis is being engaged on that phase. Open-ended vocabulary tied to the 5 WBC axes: extraction-intensity moves (grind / ratio / temp shifts), time-distribution moves (pulse / hold / drain), physical-system moves (valve restriction / immersion / percolation mode), external-control moves (Melodrip agitation taper / Paragon chilling ball / kettle off-base). The **design-time label** picked during recipe construction. Distinct from the cup-side target (what the phase is supposed to *deliver* in the cup), though the two are paired during Phase-Mapped Hybrid design.
_Avoid_: "phase mechanic" (clunky), "what the brewer does" (too informal), the BREWING.md shorthand `saturation / body / clarity / finish` (mixed-vocabulary; retired in favor of this formal split).

**Cup-side target** (per phase):
What sensory attribute a given phase is meant to extract / deliver in the final cup. Used during recipe design (Phase-Mapped Hybrid, Role-Based Pulse) and during iteration (Step 3 retro — "the finish pour didn't deliver clean finish, adjust"). **Canonical attribute set is the fixed 5**: `aroma / attack / mid-palate / body / finish` — the same vocabulary used by Step 3 tasting and the Step 4 resolved-brew output, locked 2026-05-15 for design / iteration / archive consistency. The 5-attribute set is deliberately general — coffees vary heavily by profile (acidity-bent / sweetness-bent / funky / nutty-chocolatey / etc.) and a narrower vocabulary like `acidity / sweetness / clarity` would fail to describe many of them. **Phase → attribute mapping is soft, not 1:1**: typical pairings (bloom → aroma, last pour → finish) are design-time aspirations and "generally what you're targeting" rather than guarantees, and recipes often have fewer or more phases than 5 (single-pour, 2-pour, 3-pour, 4-pour all common; bloom and double-bloom variants are future-scope). The two-label formal split keeps cup-side targets strictly sensory; mechanical roles carry the "what you do" vocabulary.
_Avoid_: "sensory target" (BREWING.md's prior framing — fine as a category but ambiguous in practice because the doc's actual values mix sensory and mechanical), "phase outcome", "cup attribute" (too generic).

**Coffee Brief**:
The design-time framing of a brew, authored by **claude.ai** at the start of every brewing session before any recipe parameters are touched — Latent's input-side is MCP-only, and the Coffee Brief is Step 1 of that input workflow. Four sub-steps run in sequence: **(1a)** web search for the roaster's published brew guide, **(1b)** evaluate process + variety risk flags against the Process/Variety Signal table, **(1c)** write a 3-5 sentence brief synthesizing terroir / cultivar / process / roaster intent (cross-referencing prior Latent archive on related coffees), **(1d)** propose an extraction strategy + zero-or-more modifiers + named considerations (Cooling-Curve Design, WBC corpus check), with explicit justification + risk-if-wrong notes — then pause for Chris's "yep go for it" confirmation before producing the Step 2 recipe. **Operationally analogous to Claude Code's plan mode** — claude.ai plans, Chris confirms, then execution begins. Chris confirms ~99% of the time without changes, by design: the system is built to internalize judgment, not require manual override at runtime.
_Avoid_: "Step 1" (under-specified outside the document), bare "brief" (overloaded — could mean any prose summary), "coffee brief" lowercase (canonical with capitals as a workflow stage name), "design pass".

**Reference brew**:
The recipe Chris commits to for a coffee at the end of the brewing iteration loop — the terminal output of Step 3, recorded via the Step 4 resolved-brew artifact. Recognized (not designated via control experiment) when iteration deltas flatten and the cup matches the targeted profile. **Default term for purchased roasted beans** — for self-roasted reference roasts the same workflow output is called the **optimized brew** to emphasize that it pushes past the constrained xBloom reference cup baseline. Both names describe the same artifact shape and the same iteration-loop output; the distinction is the calibration-vs-consumption framing on the self-roasted side. Subjective by nature: coffee in cup form is taste-driven, no objective best, and the same coffee can land beautifully across multiple extraction strategies — "reference" reflects the calibration role this recipe plays for this coffee, not a claim that it's the only valid expression.
_Avoid_: "best brew", "final brew", "winning brew", "the recipe" (under-specified), "optimized brew" when context is specifically a purchased bean (use reference brew — optimized brew is reserved for the self-roasted handoff to emphasize the calibration-to-consumption pushdown).

**Signature method**:
A proper-name proprietary process — a producer- or facility-specific processing signature that's both **(1) branded under a proper-noun name** and **(2) mechanically opaque** (the producer markets it under the brand but doesn't fully disclose the underlying recipe). The two criteria together are what distinguish signatures from the structural process modifier axes (fermentation / drying / intervention / experimental): if the mechanical recipe is fully decomposable into canonical axes, the move lives in those axes — even when it's elaborate. The signature method axis exists precisely for the *black-box-with-a-brand* case. Persisted on `brews.signature_method` + `green_beans.signature_method` as a single canonical value; null when the process doesn't carry a proprietary signature. **Hint, not auto-fill**: when a signature is selected on a brew, the picker surfaces a hint like "Moonshadow is typically Washed at Sebastian Ramirez" but does NOT auto-fill `base_process` — rare variants (Moonshadow Washed vs Moonshadow Honey) are legitimate. The signature names *who and what they call it*; the structural fields name *the approximated mechanical recipe* (best-effort interpretation, not producer-disclosed).

**Canonical working list (Chris's, 2026-05-15): 14 signatures** — Moonshadow (Dark Room Dried + Extended Drying), TyOxidator (Aerobic Phase + Anaerobic Fermentation), Alchemy (Carbonic Maceration + Yeast Inoculated), TIM (Yeast Inoculated + Mossto), XO (Extended Anaerobic + Dynamic Cherry — CGLE Mandela XO is the reference example), Enzyflow (Enzyme Assisted + Aerobic Phase), Bio-innovation (Anaerobic + Biological Fermentation), Sous-vide (Enzyme Assisted + Thermal Control), Amazake (Koji + Yeast Inoculated), Anti-maceration (Anaerobic vacuum + Ozone + Zeolite Drying), Dynamic cherry (Anaerobic + Dynamic Cherry), Dry fermentation (Mossto + Cold Room + Natural), Splash (Anaerobic + Lactic Fermentation), Symbiotic (Mossto cross-varietal). **Wave Hybrid** (Finca Soledad / Pepe Jijón) is a 15th candidate flagged for addition. **The Latent app registry currently encodes only 3** (Moonshadow / TyOxidator / Hybrid Washed) — significant gap flagged for sprint. **Hybrid Washed** appears in the current Latent registry but NOT in Chris's working canonical list — likely deprecated; verify before any registry edit.

Promotion / addition discipline: low ceremony — Chris adds signatures manually as he encounters them. No `propose_canonical_addition` queue planned for this axis specifically because the rate of new-signature discovery is low.
_Avoid_: "proprietary process" (descriptive but under-specified — signature is canonical), "branded process" (close but the canonical noun is signature), "named process" (overloaded — all canonical processes have names; signature is the *proper-noun-branded-and-opaque* sub-case), "producer signature" (different — that's `ProducerEntry.referenceRole` Anchor/Signal/Experimental).

**Process-Dominant**:
A boolean flag on the resolved brew row (`process_dominant`) that records whether the cup's flavor was driven primarily by **processing** rather than by terroir, cultivar, or roaster house style. **An aggregation trust signal**, not a Step 1d signal-arbitration reflection: `true` = this brew's learnings are *process-specific* and should NOT be aggregated into the cultivar / terroir / roaster pages' compounding-knowledge surfaces; `false` = the brew's learnings apply broadly to those surfaces. Canonical example: Moonwake Lord Voldemort (yeast-inoculated watermelon-fermented Ombligon, Colombia) — the process is so heavy that lessons from it tell you nothing about Ombligon-the-variety, Colombian terroir, or even much about anaerobic-washed processes broadly; they tell you about *that specific yeast-inoculated watermelon-ferment signature* and nothing else.

**Tight in practice with Step 1d process-override, distinct conceptually**: when the process signal overrode at Step 1d (intervention-heavy process won the strategy-selection contest), the brew is *usually* also process-dominant at Step 4. But conceptually the two surfaces are different — Step 1d signal override is a *design-time judgment* about which signal wins for strategy selection, `process_dominant` is a *post-hoc aggregation hint* about whether to trust the brew's learnings to generalize beyond its process. Both happen to fire on the same heavy-process coffees in practice.

**Origin + current status**: predates the Latent app — emerged in the claude.ai-local-app precursor that became Latent, when Chris's compounding-knowledge sheet was small enough that one Lord-Voldemort-tier brew could skew cultivar / terroir aggregations. With today's larger corpus, the signal may be becoming less load-bearing; flagged for review.
_Avoid_: "process-driven" (informal — used in BREWING.md prose but `process_dominant` is the canonical field name), "process-forward" (refers to flavor presentation, not aggregation logic), "process flag" (under-specified), "process-specific" (close, but `process_dominant` is the canonical Boolean).

**Cooling-Curve Target** (a.k.a. **Cooling-Curve Design** at Step 1d):
A WBC-derived Thermal Systems concept — *"design the brew, service, or wait time around the temperature window where the cup peaks"* — evaluated as a Named Consideration at Coffee Brief Step 1d and persisted on the resolved brew row as free-text `cooling_curve_target` (e.g. `"40-45°C peak"`, `"evaluate below 50°C"`). **Cooling-Curve Design** = the design-time question framing at Step 1d ("is the peak evaluation window IS the strategy for this coffee?"); **Cooling-Curve Target** = the declared answer recorded at Step 4. Two phrasings, one concept, paralleling the question → answer lifecycle. **Governs perception and service timing, not only brewing temperature** — applies at three moments: (a) brewing-time temp choice when peak-window is load-bearing, (b) evaluation-time discipline ("don't iterate before the cup cools to 45°C"), (c) service-time temp management ("rapid-cool with a PC fan to ~55°C before serving so guests taste the target end notes"). Only (a) and (b) intersect the Latent data model — service-time discipline is a Chris-side practice that doesn't currently get logged. Most brews omit/null because Chris's empirical default drinking window is 50-55°C; the field fires when a coffee's peak window diverges from that default (El Paraíso thermal-shock washed, Garrido Mokka/Mokkita naturals, anaerobic naturals, anoxic naturals, Picolot competition lots with S7X cooling signature).

Historical origin: the field name predates the WBC framing — emerged from Chris's early-Latent precision-tasting habit (handheld IR thermometer gun, recording per-coffee taste at 60°C / 55°C / 50°C / 45°C and declaring the optimal window). Years of precision-tasting collapsed into the 50-55°C default; the field today is the divergence signal, not the universal record.
_Avoid_: bare "cooling curve" (under-specified — could mean kettle decline, cup cooling arc, or this field), "evaluation window" (close, but Cooling-Curve Target is the declaration of that window, not the window itself), "peak temp" (informal — not canonical), "service temp" (third dimension of the broader concept but not the same as the brew-row field).

**Variety signal**:
A strategy bias derived from the coffee's cultivar genetics — what extraction strategy the cultivar's expressive ceiling and structural tendencies naturally fit. Transparency-driven varieties (Gesha, Sydra / Typica Mejorado, Sudan Rume, Pink Bourbon, Rosado, Laurina) → Clarity-First default; dense high-elevation varieties (Pacamara, Mokka, Mokkita) → Balanced Intensity default; broad-tolerance varieties (Bourbon, Catuai, Catimor) → context-dependent. **The default signal source** at Coffee Brief Step 1c — variety speaks first unless process or roaster override.
_Avoid_: "cultivar signal" (sometimes used informally — variety is canonical), "varietal signal", "variety bias".

**Process signal**:
A strategy bias derived from the coffee's processing — what extraction strategy the fermentation / drying interventions point toward. Read at Coffee Brief Step 1b via the Process/Variety Signal table. **Strength varies with intervention depth**: mild processes (washed, controlled DRD natural, climate-controlled NC natural) → low-strength signal, often defers to variety; intervention-heavy processes (anoxic natural, heavy co-ferment, anaerobic washed Huila/Cauca, yeast-inoculated lots) → high-strength signal, can override variety. Anaerobic naturals are a special process bucket → Suppression default. The **canonical heaviest-process-wins example: Moonwake Lord Voldemort** (Deiro Garcia Finca yeast-inoculated Ombligon, Colombia) — the process is so intervention-heavy it overrides cultivar, terroir, and even the roaster's own brew guide; the coffee becomes "its own beast."
_Avoid_: "fermentation signal" (too narrow — drying matters too), "process flag" (could mean an individual modifier), "process tag".

**Roaster signal** (a.k.a. **House style**):
A strategy bias derived from the roaster's house style and / or published brew guide — what extraction strategy this roaster targets when they serve this coffee. Encoded in [docs/brewing/roasters.md](../../docs/brewing/roasters.md) per roaster card; ~70 canonical roasters with strategy tags spanning Clarity-First / Balanced / Extraction-Forward / System / Varies / Self-Roasted. **House style** is the same concept framed as a roaster's *persistent extraction direction* across lots — used when the override is driven by the roaster's general bias rather than a specific brew guide. **Strong house styles point at specific strategy zones, not generic strong-vs-weak preferences**: Sey = Extraction Push-leaning ("light, very high extraction, very low agitation"); DAK = Full Expression ("you need to push it to get out what you want"); Picolot Garrido raised-bed naturals = Full Expression with fast/fast/slow valve discipline. Weak / varies-style roasters defer to variety + process; strong house-style roasters routinely override both.
_Avoid_: "roaster bias", bare "brew guide" (under-specified — could mean the document itself), "roaster intent" (close, but less precise — house style is the canonical synonym).

**Signal override**:
The arbitration outcome when two or more signals (variety / process / roaster) point at different strategies and one wins. **Judgment-based weighting at Coffee Brief Step 1c, not strict precedence**: each signal carries weight that depends on its specificity and reliability for the coffee at hand. Three reliable override patterns: **(1) Process > Variety** for intervention-heavy processes (anoxic natural overrides Pink Bourbon's transparency ceiling; Moonwake Lord Voldemort overrides everything); **(2) Roaster > Variety + Process** for strong-house-style roasters (DAK = Full Expression on clean Ethiopian anaerobic washed, overriding the Balanced Intensity default that variety + process would otherwise call); **(3) Variety > Process** for transparency-driven varieties with mild processes (Sydra TyOxidator confirmed Clarity-First despite the experimental process flag). When signals agree, no override is needed and the default applies cleanly. **Default = variety-driven** when no override condition fires.
_Avoid_: "signal conflict resolution", "override logic", "signal arbitration" (the umbrella concept — fine as a doc-section header but "signal override" is the operative term), bare "override" without a qualifier.

**Strategy zone**:
The region of recipe space defined by a given extraction strategy choice — the "country" in Chris's geography mental model where strategy = country, Hybrid sub-form / modifier choice = state, and iteration loop = city. Five intensity strategies + one structure strategy define six non-overlapping zones; brews live inside one zone at a time. **Iteration tightens within a zone, it does not transit between zones** — the iteration loop can dial from one city to another inside the same country, but it cannot move you from North America to Asia by changing one variable per turn. Wrong-zone strategy choices are the most expensive mistake the brewing workflow can make, because no iteration budget recovers them; the Coffee Brief's Step 1d confirmation gate exists specifically to catch this before iteration starts.
_Avoid_: "extraction zone" (close, but conflates the strategy's region with the chemistry of extraction itself), "neighborhood" (BREWING.md uses this loosely; "zone" is the precise term), "strategy region", "strategy territory".

**Extraction Confirmed**:
A reconciliation field on the resolved brew that records the **strategy actually validated by the iteration loop, only when it diverged from the strategy proposed at Coffee Brief Step 1d**. If the planned strategy matched the tasted result, the field is empty — the brew's `extraction_strategy` column already records what was confirmed, so no extra prose is needed. **Legacy field with declining relevance**: originated pre-Latent on Chris's spreadsheet as a wrong-zone gut-check after the Jeferson Motta wrong-zone trap (Clarity-First iteration on a coffee that needed Full Expression — no number of within-zone tweaks would have recovered it). Its original purpose has been **subsumed by the Coffee Brief upstream** — wrong-zone strategies now get caught at Step 1d via prior-archive lookup + WBC corpus + roaster brew guide cross-check, so the iteration loop rarely transits zones anymore. When the field does populate today, it's a signal worth taking seriously: the upstream prevention layer missed something, and the archive should carry the lesson forward.
_Avoid_: "extraction strategy" (that's the design-time field), "actual strategy" (under-specified), "confirmed strategy" (ambiguous — could mean confirmed-as-planned vs confirmed-after-divergence).

**Modifiers Confirmed**:
A reconciliation field on the resolved brew that records, in one closing line, which modifiers were validated and whether each resolved what it was meant to resolve. **State `None` explicitly when no modifiers were used** — null modifier results are signal, because they confirm the modifier check at Step 1d ran and the brew didn't need any. **Active experimental field**, distinct from the legacy Extraction Confirmed: modifiers are "add-ons / slight variations of the core recipe," WBC competitor recipes use them heavily, and Chris is actively expanding modifier experimentation rather than relying on the pre-iteration design pass to settle them. Examples: "Output Selection (late cut) confirmed — drying tail resolved" (validated as intended); "Aroma Capture confirmed bloom + Pour 1, cooling-arc artifact noted" (validated with new sub-finding); "None — Suppression alone resolved the bitter tail; ITS not needed" (null result, still recorded).
_Avoid_: "modifier outcome", "modifier validation", "applied modifiers" (under-specified — could mean planned, applied, or confirmed), "modifier null" (jargon for the `None` case).

**Resolved brew**:
The concrete Step 4 structured write-up of a reference brew (or optimized brew) — the key/value list (Coffee Identity / Origin / Process / Recipe / Roast Level / Tasting / Flavor Notes / Learnings) that gets validated against canonical registries and synced into the Latent app via the `push_brew` MCP Tool. **The artifact form of the reference brew** — one reference brew = one resolved brew row in the `brews` table. The artifact-vs-recipe distinction matters because the resolved brew is what gets archived (sync row, validated against canonicals, surfaced on `/brews/[id]`); the reference brew is the abstract recipe-concept the resolved brew represents. Latent records only the resolved brew today — intermediate iteration brews and tasting deltas live in the claude.ai thread and are lost when it rolls off (flagged ambiguity; Chris is open to reconsidering).
_Avoid_: bare "the brew" (ambiguous — physical brew vs recipe vs artifact), "Step 4 output" (under-specified), "sync row" (implementation-flavored), "brew record".

**Iteration loop** (brewing):
The dial-in cycle between Brew 1 and the locked reference brew — Step 3 of the brewing workflow. One iteration = claude.ai produces a recipe → Chris executes it physically → Chris records verbal/written tasting notes back into the claude.ai thread → claude.ai proposes adjustments → next recipe. Loop terminates at **diminishing returns** ("close enough to the end cup I'm looking for") or at a strategy pivot (if 2-3 brews suggest the original strategy is structurally wrong). Typical iteration count: **2-4 brews, ~3 average**, occasionally one-shot success (in which case Chris does one confirmatory test before locking), occasionally many turns on a difficult coffee. Bounded by physical constraints — most roasted-bean purchases are 125-500g, narrowing the iteration budget compared to roasting's ~10 V-sets per lot. **Distinct from the roasting-side V-set loop**: brewing iterations are sequential (single recipe per turn, not parallel batch slots), focused on the brewing recipe only (the bean is fixed input from the roaster), and cup-side feedback drives recipe adjustment rather than physical roast variables.
_Avoid_: "dialing-in", "trial-and-error", bare "iteration" (ambiguous with the roasting V-set iteration — qualify when context could mean either), "iteration cycle".

**Hybrid sub-form**:
A strict 5-value canonical enum that's **required and only required** when extraction strategy is Hybrid; null on every other strategy. Names which kind of structural compositing the brew is doing across its phase boundaries. Unique architectural anomaly in the taxonomy — Hybrid is the only strategy with a mandatory second-level pick, because **Hybrid itself is a pointer, not content**: the strategy says "the brew's extraction curve changes mode mid-stream" but carries no recipe shape until the sub-form names what kind of change. The five sub-forms:
- `sequential` — if-this-then-that switch between two modes (immersion → percolation), pure on/off, no gradient. Canonical Hario Switch pattern (closed bloom + main, opened drawdown) and SWORKS slow/slow/open.
- `phase_mapped` — each pour is tied to a cup-side attribute it primarily extracts: bloom → aromatics, Pour 1 → acidity, Pour 2 → sweetness, last pour → finish. The brewer's valve / agitation / flow per phase is tuned to emphasize the targeted attribute (e.g. SWORKS Dial 5 restriction on Pour 1 to develop acidity, opened later for sweetness).
- `selective_bloom` — bloom liquid separated from the main brew, evaluated independently, then either recombined (whole or in proportion) or discarded. Ferket 2025 pattern. ⚠ Boundary against Output Selection is intentionally drawn: this stays in Hybrid because it's about *role assignment* (the bloom fraction participates differently in the cup design), while Output Selection is about *fraction discarding* (kept some, threw some away).
- `intensity_clarity_split` — phase-mapped with a specific composition: immersion (or restricted) phase builds body / intensity early, percolation (or opened) phase recovers clarity late. Phase order matters. ⚠ Probably collapsible into `phase_mapped` as a sub-case — flagged.
- `temperature_staged` — combines valve/phase work with kettle temperature modulation across phases. Simplest case: kettle-off natural decline (~2-3°C drop) across the brew. Distinct from the Inverted Temperature Staging modifier because the temperature change is *bound* to the phase boundary rather than running independently.

The sub-form picker exists because **Hybrid alone among the 6 strategies sits on the structural-composition axis** rather than the extraction-intensity axis. The other 5 strategies are self-contained ("Suppression" tells you what the brew is doing"); Hybrid only becomes meaningful once you name the kind of compositing.
_Avoid_: "Hybrid variant", "sub-strategy", "Hybrid type", bare "sub-form" (which sub-form?).

### MCP / Sync Architecture

**Championship-mode**:
The disposition Latent operates in: training for **World Brewers Cup** / **World Roasting Championship**-level mastery — not cafe-repeatable, not commercial, not chasing a universal "best" roast or brew. Every coffee is treated as a unique material whose ideal expression has to be found per-coffee, and every brew or roast is an opportunity to learn something generalizable. Drives the per-coffee optimization stance, the willingness to push extreme axes (Full Expression / Extraction Push on one end, Suppression / very dark on the other), the **archive-driven brewing** + **iterative roasting** workflows, and the system-level investment in compounding knowledge rather than locking down a single recipe. Articulated across PRODUCT.md / BREWING.md / ROASTING.md / CLAUDE.md in slightly different phrasings; all of them name the same disposition.
_Avoid_: "competition-mode" (close but champion is Chris's canonical framing), "pro-mode", "championship-grade" (the discipline is championship-LEVEL, not grade), "WBC-mode" (Brewers Cup is one half — Roasting Championship is the other), "hobby-mode" (the disposition is explicitly serious, not casual).

**System self-improvement loop**:
The compounding-knowledge mechanism the whole Latent architecture is designed to make possible: every brew or roast that lands in Latent feeds the next one, both through **structured aggregation** (per-cultivar / per-terroir / per-process / per-roaster pages, carry-forward learnings on closed lots) and through **prose improvement of the living docs** (BREWING.md, ROASTING.md, per-roaster cards, taxonomy markdown) via `propose_doc_changes`. The **MCP-only input principle** keeps the loop coherent (one canonical author, one canonical taxonomy); the **propose-doc-changes pipeline + arbiter procedure** closes the loop (claude.ai stages improvements during a workflow, Claude Code arbitrates them in batch, Vercel auto-deploys the merged result, claude.ai's next session reads the improved doc). End-state: time-to-reference-roast and time-to-reference-brew shrink with every closed lot / closed brew, because the substrate is smarter than it was last week.
_Avoid_: "self-improving system" (close but the noun is the LOOP — the system is Latent), "feedback loop" (generic), "compounding-knowledge loop" (close — but compounding knowledge is the SUBSTRATE of the loop, already covered by `carry-forward learnings`), "the loop" (under-specified — there are at least 3 named loops: the brewing iteration loop, the V-set roasting loop, and this one).

**Role separation**:
The 4-way separation of labor that lets Latent function as a self-improving system. **claude.ai** (specifically the `claude.ai/projects` surface — the brewing + roasting project threads, not the bare model API) is the **input** — authors every entity that lands in Latent (brews / roasts / cuppings / experiments / roast_learnings) plus every doc-change proposal, validating each write against canonical taxonomies. **Claude Code** (running locally in a Chris session) is the **builder + arbiter** — its dominant role is building the Latent codebase (features, schema, page shapes); **arbitration is one specific operational routine within that broader builder role**, triggered by "process pending arbitration" and walking the `doc_proposals` + `taxonomy_overrides_queue` queues to keep DB / docs / taxonomies in sync. **Latent** (bare noun — the application + Postgres + the MCP server itself) is the **system** — encoded in code, lives on independent of either Claude, single source of truth for state. **Chris** is **physical execution** — roasts, brews, cups, tastes, judges, makes the judgment calls; does not author records, does not insert into the app, does not edit. The boundary between input and physical execution is enforced by the **MCP-only input principle**; the boundary between builder and arbiter is internal to Claude Code (same model, two operational modes).
_Avoid_: "the workflow" (under-specified), "the actors", "four-role model" (jargon — the canonical noun is just "role separation" or "division of labor"), "Claude" without qualifier (ambiguous between claude.ai-input and Claude-Code-builder/arbiter — always pin which surface).

**Latent MCP server**:
The single canonical input/output plane between the Latent application and every Claude system that interacts with it — **claude.ai** (the brewing + roasting workflow input), **Claude Code** (the builder + arbiter), and the **Roest L200 machine** (the external roasting device, via the Roest API integration). Hosted as Next.js API routes on the same Vercel deployment as the Latent app (co-located, not a separate process); single-tenant + single-user by deliberate choice. Exposes two atomic primitives — **Tools** (named write actions + Tool-shaped reads) and **Resources** (URI-addressable read-only catalog/context). Two auth paths land at the same `user_id` for RLS scoping: OAuth 2.1 + PKCE for the claude.ai web client, bearer token for Claude Code + desktop MCP clients.

**Primary direction is input** — claude.ai writes brews / roasts / cuppings / experiments / roast_learnings into the Latent app via Tools — with two secondary write-back surfaces layered on: **`propose_doc_changes`** (internal — claude.ai stages prose improvements to living docs in a queue Claude Code arbitrates) and the **Roest API integration** (external — push/pull profiles + inventory + roast logs against the physical machine). Read surfaces (docs:// + canonicals:// Resources, plus Tool-shaped reads) serve the input side, not analysis: they exist so claude.ai writes against the same canonical vocabulary the Latent codebase enforces. The server is the load-bearing substrate for the **MCP-only input principle** (per `feedback_mcp_only_input.md`) and for the **system self-improvement loop** (input + arbitration + doc-improvement compounding to shrink time-to-reference on every future lot / coffee).
_Avoid_: "the app's MCP server", "Latent's MCP server", "the MCP layer" (bare), "sync server" (legacy from the pre-MCP V1 sync attempt — superseded by V2 in Sprint 2.2, 2026-04-28).

**MCP-only input principle**:
The standing rule that **claude.ai writing via the Latent MCP server is the canonical input path for every entity that lands in Latent** — brews, green_beans, roasts, roast_recipes, cuppings, experiments, roast_learnings, doc proposals, inventory rows, Roest profiles. No manual DB inserts; no paste-into-spreadsheet flows; no hand-authoring; no drafting; no manual editing. The principle has two paired motivations: **(1) the opportunity** — claude.ai writes flow through canonical-validation + `propose_doc_changes`, so every input contributes to the **System self-improvement loop** rather than just landing inert in the DB; **(2) the constraint** — the canonical taxonomy + process decomposition + signature method + Hybrid sub-form picker + 4-axis modifier stack + 4-state lifecycle + per-roast / per-cupping / per-experiment / per-recipe schema have collectively gotten complex enough that no human (including Chris) can author a write that resolves cleanly against every canonical surface in their head. Giving the input role to the system component that holds the most context (claude.ai, with docs:// + canonicals:// Resources and read_doc / read_canonical Tools available) is the only tractable path.

Historical context: pre-Latent, Chris hand-entered every row on a spreadsheet; his EA Belle compiled context, Chris arbitrated. The first Latent precursor (a Claude project) mimicked that workflow with Chris-as-arbiter inserting EA-compiled records into the app. The MCP-only input principle emerged once the schema complexity outgrew the EA-arbiter workflow. **Going back is functionally not viable** (Chris, 2026-05-15) — the system has gotten complex enough that the spreadsheet path can't reproduce a valid write. Roasting-side `/add?type=self-roasted` deprecated 2026-05-13 (Sub Pages 6.6); purchased-brew `/add?type=purchased` and `/brews/[id]/edit` are queued future deprecations pending claude.ai brewing-write workflow stability — the principle is **partially enforced today** (roasting absolute, brewing aspirational).
_Avoid_: "MCP-only writes" (the principle is about input/authoring, not about all writes — physical roasting still produces writes, just via claude.ai-as-author), "claude.ai-only input" (under-specifies the path — claude.ai writes happen specifically via MCP, not any other channel), bare "MCP-only" (under-specified), "no-input rule" (Chris does provide input via voice/text to claude.ai — he just doesn't author DB records directly).

**Cross-system consistency principle**:
The standing rule that **claude.ai, Claude Code, and Latent must always share the same vocabulary, canonical taxonomy, and operational rules** — drift between any two breaks the System self-improvement loop because the logic stops working and the input starts assuming things that aren't true. Chris's framing (2026-05-15): the three system components have to stay in sync with each other and always have a shared language, or that's where the confusion starts. Concretely: every Latent-specific term defined in `CONTEXT.md` (registered as the `docs://context.md` Resource) is consumed by claude.ai during workflow authoring; every canonical taxonomy lives in `lib/*-registry.ts` + `docs/taxonomies/*.md` and is exposed via both the `canonicals://*` Resource family and the `read_canonical` Tool (the **dual-surface pattern**, a separate entry); every operational rule (lifecycle state machine, one-shot constraints, signature method canonical list, Hybrid sub-form picker, modifier types, lever vs variable post-hoc promotion) is encoded somewhere consumed by both the input side (claude.ai authoring) and the validation side (Latent rejecting non-canonical writes). Sync is maintained by four mechanisms: **(1) live Resource reads** — claude.ai pulls the canonical state at write time, never from a frozen copy; **(2) validation on push** — Latent rejects writes that don't resolve canonically; **(3) `propose_doc_changes`** — claude.ai stages doc improvements during workflows; **(4) the arbiter procedure** — Claude Code applies proposals, redeploys, and the next claude.ai session reads the updated doc. **The principle is why CONTEXT.md exists, why it's a Resource, why every taxonomy has both a markdown source and a registry mirror, and why the arbiter procedure was built.**
_Avoid_: "single source of truth" (Latent / the repo IS the SOT — the principle is about KEEPING THE OTHER COMPONENTS aligned with it, not establishing what's authoritative), "consistency rule" (under-specified), "vocabulary sync" (too narrow — it's vocabulary + taxonomy + rules), bare "shared language" (close but doesn't carry the operational sync mechanisms), "drift prevention" (close — drift is the failure mode the principle prevents, not the principle itself).

**Tool**:
A named atomic action surfaced by the Latent MCP server — either a **write** (push_brew, push_roast, patch_*, propose_doc_changes, resolve_queue_entry, push_roast_profile) or a **read** whose results need to be model-callable from claude.ai (read_canonical, read_doc, get_brew, list_recent_brews, list_taxonomy_queue, get_bean_pipeline, pull_roest_log). Each Tool carries an input schema (validated at call time via `zod`), a description string used by claude.ai's `tool_search` for ranking + discoverability, and a typed return shape. Tools are the **primary discoverability surface** for claude.ai — its MCP client lists Tools as actions the model can invoke; Resources are catalog/context but not always model-surfaced. **34 Tools live as of 2026-05-13** (post Sub Pages 6.1).
_Avoid_: bare "action", "MCP function", "API endpoint" (Tools are HTTP-transported but not REST endpoints — they're JSON-RPC over MCP), "command", "method", "RPC".

**Resource**:
A **URI-addressable read-only artifact** surfaced by the Latent MCP server — content endpoints any MCP client can list and fetch. URIs use scheme-prefix conventions: `docs://` (BREWING.md, ROASTING.md, per-roaster cards, WBC reference + recipes, per-taxonomy markdown, operational prompts, CONTEXT.md) and `canonicals://{axis}` (one JSON registry per taxonomy axis). Resources are **catalog + context**, not actions; client behavior varies — claude.ai's MCP client surfaces them less reliably than Tools, which is why Tool-shaped reads (`read_doc` / `read_canonical`) exist alongside the Resource equivalents (the **dual-surface pattern**). Resources stay registered for catalog/discovery and for clients that DO surface them.

Retired Resources (footnote for traceability): `brews://recent` + `brews://by-id/{uuid}` were live Resources in Sprint 2.3 but were retired in Sprint 3.0.5 (2026-05-03) when their Tool equivalents (`list_recent_brews` + `get_brew`) absorbed all use — surfaced as the canonical example of the Resource → Tool promotion path.
_Avoid_: "asset", "blob", "static file" (Resources are served dynamically — source may be a repo file, a DB query, or a registry export), "read-only API" (under-specified), "endpoint", "document" (Resources include canonicals:// JSON, not just docs).

**Dual-surface pattern**:
The deliberate architectural decision to expose the same payload via **both a Resource AND a Tool** when the payload is something claude.ai's model needs to call on-demand. Discovered in Sprint 2.4 dog-food (2026-04-29): claude.ai's MCP client treats Tools as the on-demand model surface but Resources as catalog-only — without Tool-shaped reads, `propose_doc_changes` citations couldn't be drafted against verbatim live-doc text and `push_brew` callers had to read full taxonomy markdown (133 anchors for producers!) to validate one canonical name. Concrete instances: `read_doc` / `read_doc_section` / `list_docs` / `list_doc_sections` Tools alongside `docs://*` Resources; `read_canonical` / `list_canonicals` Tools alongside `canonicals://{axis}` Resources. Brews followed the same path — `brews://recent` + `brews://by-id/{uuid}` Resources were **promoted to Tools** (`list_recent_brews` + `get_brew`) in Sprint 3.0.5 (2026-05-03) and the Resource side was retired. The pattern is the operational form of an architectural rule: **claude.ai surfaces Tools to the model; Resources are catalog only**. **Self-reflective sub-case** (Chris's framing, 2026-05-15): `propose_doc_changes` is the **inverse** of dual-surface — it's a Tool that targets a Resource for write-back, rather than a Tool that mirrors a Resource's read shape. The same architectural rule produces both — model-callable surfaces must be Tools, regardless of whether they read or write Resource content.
_Avoid_: "Tool/Resource parity", "mirror surface", "promoted Resource" (promotion is a path that produces a dual-surface — not the pattern itself), "Tool-shaped reads" (the sub-shape, not the pattern), "Resource shadow".

**Asymmetric write trust**:
The architectural decision that claude.ai's MCP writes are trusted differently depending on **what's being written**. Two trust tiers + one variant:
- **Direct write** (high trust, no intermediate review) — structured field-picking against canonical registries: brews, green_beans, roasts, roast_recipes, cuppings, experiments, roast_learnings, inventory rows, Roest profiles. Validated at push time against the canonical taxonomy (cultivar / terroir / process / roaster / brewer / filter / grinder / flavor / signature method); FK-enforced where applicable; rejected outright on canonical resolution failure. claude.ai is reliable here because every field has a known set of legal values and the validation pipeline catches everything else.
- **Propose-then-apply** (lower trust, Claude Code arbitrates) — free-form prose mutations to living docs (BREWING.md, ROASTING.md, `docs/brewing/roasters.md` per-roaster cards, `docs/taxonomies/*.md` source files). claude.ai authors **proposals with verbatim citations**; proposals land in `doc_proposals` (status='pending'); Claude Code walks the queue via the **arbiter procedure** and applies citation-by-citation. Multi-write contention is real — Chris often touches one coffee through 3-4 workflows the same day and may propose edits to the same doc section from different angles; the arbiter handles batching + supersede logic.
- **Canonical-promotion writes** (variant — same propose-then-apply shape, different substrate) — when claude.ai encounters a value not in the canonical taxonomy (a new producer, a new roaster, a new region, a new proprietary process, a new cultivar — override is broad, not axis-specific), the path is: direct-write the entity with an `*_override:true` flag (override-flag axes: producer / roaster / brewer / filter / grinder), OR fail fast and call `propose_canonical_addition` (strict axes: cultivar / terroir). Either way the net-new value lands in `taxonomy_overrides_queue` and Claude Code arbitrates registry promotion via the Phase 3 arbiter playbook (same procedure file, second queue type).

Why asymmetric: claude.ai's strong suit (validated across V2 dog-food) is canonical-registry field-picking — every field has a bounded set of legal values; the validation pipeline catches everything else; direct-write is safe. Free-form prose mutation is where **drift** and **bloat** happen (separate entries), so Chris explicitly chose Claude Code as the arbiter for that path. **The asymmetry is the load-bearing call of SYNC_V2 (Decision #5)** — it's what lets the system be both fully MCP-only-input AND prevent the doc + registry layers from degrading.

**Promotion-discipline rule** (Chris, 2026-05-15): when a canonical-promotion write does result in a registry addition (e.g. a new producer survives arbitration), the **full canonical entry must be populated at promotion time** — not just the bare-minimum required fields. The promotion is the only moment the entry's context is fresh; deferred fill-in is forgotten fill-in. Producer entries get 27 fields; roaster entries get 29; the discipline applies across all rich-registry axes.

**Saturation projection**: the volume of canonical-promotion writes is expected to **decay over time** as Chris's coffee exposure saturates the registries — at some point ~99% of new coffees encountered are already canonical, and promotion becomes rare. The registries are converging assets, not endlessly-growing ones.
_Avoid_: "two-tier writes" (loses the trust-is-the-variable framing), "trust split" (close but doesn't carry *what determines the tier*), "structured-vs-prose" (under-specified — canonical-promotion is a third shape that isn't strictly prose), "MCP write modes".

**Drift**:
The slow-motion failure mode where the same canonical thing gets referenced under slightly-different names across writes — "Volcan Baru Highlands" vs "Volcán Barú Highlands" (terroir), "Geisha" vs "Gesha" (cultivar), "Diego Bermudez" vs "Letty Bermudez + Finca El Paraiso" (producer drift across sources). Each reference is plausible in isolation; in aggregate they fragment what should be one canonical bucket and dilute the per-axis aggregation pages. **Real-world fuzz is the source** — coffee is an agricultural product, regions don't have clean boundary lines, cultivar identifications aren't always genetically confirmed — so taxonomies have to do active work to project messy reality onto a clean canonical surface. Chris experienced drift extensively in the pre-Latent spreadsheet era even working alone; it compounded when his EA Belle (no coffee context) joined the input loop, requiring Chris to act as arbiter on every batch. The MCP-only input principle + canonical-validation pipeline + alias-resolution maps are the architectural prevention of drift at the data layer; the **propose-then-apply tier** is the prevention of *prose drift* (same failure mode at the doc layer).
_Avoid_: "canonical drift" (drift IS the unit; adding "canonical" implies there's a non-canonical kind), "naming inconsistency" (under-specified), "taxonomy fragmentation" (this is the consequence of drift, not drift itself), "string drift".

**Category bloat**:
The opposite failure mode from drift — the canonical registry growing too many entries that should have been **aliased** or **rejected**, fragmenting what should be a unified canonical bucket. Without an arbiter step every `propose_canonical_addition` or `*_override:true` write would land verbatim in the registry; over enough writes the registry would become indistinguishable from the raw input string set, defeating the canonical aggregation purpose. The propose-then-apply variant for canonical-promotion writes (`taxonomy_overrides_queue`) exists precisely so Claude Code can decide **promote / alias / reject / duplicate** per queued entry. Chris's framing (2026-05-15): "you don't want to split categories too much" — there's an inherent tension between **fine-grained enough to be specific** and **coarse-grained enough to aggregate over**, and the arbiter is the human-in-the-loop that calibrates the boundary per axis. Terroir aggregates at the macro level (not country, not farm); cultivar aggregates at the lineage/family level (not species, not specific origin); the right scope is axis-specific and was deliberately chosen.
_Avoid_: "registry bloat" (close but the registry is the substrate, not the failure mode), "canonical fragmentation" (this is what bloat produces, not the bloat itself), "taxonomy inflation".

**State bloat**:
The doc-layer analog of category bloat — living docs (BREWING.md, ROASTING.md, per-roaster cards, taxonomy markdown) accumulating un-curated edits that fragment what should be coherent narrative sections. Without the arbiter every `propose_doc_changes` write would append-or-replace verbatim; over enough writes the docs would degrade into a sprawl of redundant bullets, contradictory framings, and stale references. The arbiter procedure looks at proposals in batch (1 proposal can have N citations across N sections; M proposals can touch the same section across a multi-day write window) and decides per-citation **apply / reject / edit**, plus enforces house style (hyphens-not-em-dashes per `feedback_hyphens_not_emdashes.md`) at write time. Chris's framing: "somebody has to be the one looking through the docs to make sure they stay clean" — without active curation the docs degrade as input volume grows.
_Avoid_: "doc bloat" (close but state is the better generalization — covers per-roaster cards + taxonomy markdown + CONTEXT.md), "narrative drift" (close but state bloat is the un-curated-accumulation problem, narrative drift is a sub-case), "edit sprawl".

**Arbiter**:
The role Claude Code plays when keeping Latent's docs + canonical registries clean against accumulating proposals — one of the two operational modes of the Claude Code half of Role separation (the other being builder). The arbiter is a **batch curator**: it doesn't run real-time after each proposal; it runs when Chris triggers it ("process pending arbitration" or aliases — see separate entry), reads everything pending, and walks the queues citation-by-citation. The arbiter's primary value-proposition is **preventing drift / category bloat / state bloat** (separate entries) — without it, every claude.ai proposal would land verbatim and the canonical layer would degrade. The arbiter is also the **multi-write contention resolver**: when Chris touches one coffee through 3-4 workflows the same day and overlapping proposals land, the arbiter batches them and decides what supersedes what.
_Avoid_: "reviewer" (under-specified — arbiter is specifically batched + queue-walking, not real-time), "curator" (close but curator implies pre-existing content; arbiter is queue-walking), "merger" (too narrow — most of the work is judgment, not merging), bare "Claude Code" (Claude Code has two operational modes; pinning which one matters).

**Arbiter procedure**:
The step-by-step playbook a Claude Code session follows when running as arbiter — codified in `ARBITER.md` at the repo root. Today **two parallel queue types**, one procedure: **prose proposals** (`doc_proposals`, migration 037) get an apply / reject / edit / orphaned-anchor disposition per citation; **canonical promotions** (`taxonomy_overrides_queue`, migration 045) get a promote / alias / reject / duplicate disposition per queue row. Both queue types are walked in the same session; if one is empty the procedure skips it and continues. The queue types are **extensible by design** — future curation surfaces (null-field reviews, missing-entity flags, other drift signals) can attach to the same trigger phrase without changing the procedure shape. Key procedural elements: read pending → group (by `target_doc` for prose, by `axis` for canonical) → present plan to Chris → Chris does a quick pass-through ("yep go for it" the typical response, ~99% of cases) → apply decisions → flip queue row status → commit / PR / merge → Vercel redeploys → next claude.ai session reads fresh state. **House-style normalization happens at apply time** (em-dashes / en-dashes → plain hyphens, per `feedback_hyphens_not_emdashes.md`). **Auto-supersede semantics**: when a newer prose proposal targets the same `(target_doc, section_anchor)` as an older pending one, the older row is auto-marked superseded by the `propose_doc_changes` Tool handler; the arbiter only sees the newer.
_Avoid_: "arbitration playbook" (close, but procedure is the canonical noun), "doc proposal workflow" (under-specified — covers both queue types and future extensions), "ARBITER.md" (that's the file; the procedure is the abstract concept the file documents), "arbiter flow".

**Process pending arbitration**:
The canonical **trigger phrase** that initiates an arbiter procedure run, plus its functional aliases ("process pending doc proposals", "arbitrate", "apply pending docs", "review the proposal queue", "review canonical proposals", "process pending taxonomy queue", etc.). All aliases route to the same playbook (ARBITER.md). The trigger is intentionally **Chris-initiated** — not auto-run on a schedule and not real-time per proposal — because batched curation is the right cadence: multi-day write windows produce contention the arbiter needs to see all at once. The phrase is documented in `ARBITER.md` § Trigger phrase and is a fixed protocol element of the system; the trigger surface is open to extension as new queue types are added.
_Avoid_: bare "process pending" (under-specified), "kick off the arbiter" (informal), "run the arbiter" (close but doesn't match the protocol phrasing), "arbitration command".

**`propose_doc_changes`**:
The Tool claude.ai calls when its current workflow surfaces a doc-improvement opportunity worth carrying forward — a new flavor pattern, a roaster-specific brewing lesson, a process-axis nuance, a taxonomy refinement, a cross-coffee insight. The propose-then-apply tier of asymmetric write trust (separate entry) — claude.ai writes the proposal as **N citations against specific section anchors** with verbatim `current_text` + `proposed_text` + `rationale` per citation; the proposal lands in `doc_proposals` with status='pending'. **Polymorphic Tool** — one schema covers all four target families (BREWING.md, ROASTING.md, per-roaster cards in `docs/brewing/roasters.md`, per-axis taxonomy markdown in `docs/taxonomies/*.md`). Citations can target **different files within a single proposal** (per-citation `target_doc` overrides the proposal-level default) because one brew debrief often touches a roaster card + a BREWING.md archive bullet + an Open Questions section simultaneously. **Auto-supersede semantics at insert time**: a newer proposal targeting the same `(target_doc, section_anchor)` as an older pending row marks the older row `superseded` automatically — the arbiter only ever sees the newest version.

**Proposal-quality discipline** (Chris, 2026-05-15): the Tool is **silent-and-optional** — claude.ai proposes only when a workflow has surfaced **net-new system improvement**, never speculatively. If a brewing completion or roasting close-out produced nothing the living docs don't already capture, **no proposal is the right answer**; writing a proposal for the sake of writing one is state-bloat at the source. The discipline is enforced at the proposer (claude.ai), not at the arbiter — by the time something lands in `doc_proposals` it should already pass the system-improvement bar.
_Avoid_: bare "propose doc changes" (informal — the literal Tool name with backticks is canonical), "doc-change Tool" (under-specified), "PR proposal" (these aren't PRs — they're queue rows the arbiter ultimately turns into PRs), "edit suggestion" (under-specifies the system-improvement bar).

**`doc_proposals` queue**:
The Postgres table (migration 037, 2026-04-29) holding pending prose-doc-change proposals between the moment claude.ai writes via `propose_doc_changes` and the moment Claude Code applies via the arbiter procedure. **Status lifecycle**: `pending` → `applied` / `rejected` / `superseded`. One row per proposal; each row carries an array of citations + a summary + a source (`source.kind` + `source.id`, e.g. `brew:uuid`). RLS-scoped to Chris's user_id. The substrate of the propose-then-apply tier — the table IS the queue. Status flips happen at the arbiter (Claude Code runs `UPDATE doc_proposals SET status = ... WHERE id = ...` per resolved proposal during the arbiter procedure). **Multi-day write windows are normal**: Chris may not run "process pending arbitration" for days after proposals land; the queue tolerates batching by design.
_Avoid_: "doc_proposals table" (technically correct but under-specifies its role — it IS a queue, with status semantics), "proposal queue" (under-specified — there are at least two queue types, this is the prose one), "BREWING.md queue" (wrong — covers all 4 target families).

**`taxonomy_overrides_queue`**:
The Postgres table (migration 045, Phase 3, 2026-05-05) holding pending canonical-promotion proposals across **7 taxonomy axes today**: `producer`, `roaster`, `brewer`, `filter`, `grinder`, `terroir`, `cultivar`. The substrate of the canonical-promotion variant of asymmetric write trust — structural parallel to `doc_proposals` (same arbiter procedure walks both). One row per net-new canonical proposal; carries `axis`, `raw_value` (verbatim), `submission_path` (`override_flag` for override-eligible axes or `manual_propose` for strict axes), `source_kind` + `source_id` (the brew / green_bean / roast that triggered it), `evidence` jsonb. **Status lifecycle**: `pending` → `promoted` / `aliased` / `rejected` / `duplicate`. **EXCLUDE constraint on `(user_id, axis, lower(raw_value)) WHERE status='pending'`** auto-collapses duplicate pushes — a second push of the same string returns the existing pending row id; the arbiter sees the value once even if 5 brews triggered it. RLS-scoped. **Axis set is expected to grow** — signature_method is a queued architectural addition (Chris, 2026-05-15) so net-new proprietary processes (currently constrained to 3 canonical entries against Chris's 14-entry working list) flow through the same arbiter pipeline rather than failing fast on a missing canonical.
_Avoid_: "the canonical queue" (under-specified), "override queue" (close but only one of two submission paths uses override flags), "taxonomy queue" (informal short-form — fine in prose; the canonical noun is `taxonomy_overrides_queue`).

**`propose_canonical_addition`**:
The Tool claude.ai calls when introducing a canonical value on a **strict axis** where direct-write-with-override-flag isn't available. Strict axes (cultivar / terroir today) fail fast on canonical resolution failure — the entity write is rejected outright at validation — so claude.ai's recovery path is to call `propose_canonical_addition` explicitly with `axis` + `raw_value` + `evidence`, landing the row in `taxonomy_overrides_queue` with `submission_path='manual_propose'`. claude.ai then re-attempts the entity write once Chris-as-arbiter has promoted the canonical via the arbiter procedure (or supplies an alias resolution). Distinct from the override-flag path (`*_override:true`) which lands the entity AND queues the canonical in one call — strict axes don't get the one-call shortcut because Chris deliberately wants the entity write blocked until the canonical is resolved.
_Avoid_: "propose new canonical" (close but the literal Tool name with backticks is canonical), "canonical proposal" (ambiguous — could mean a queue row), "strict-axis fallback" (informal).

**Override flag**:
The `<axis>_override: true` parameter (`producer_override`, `roaster_override`, `brewer_override`, `filter_override`, `grinder_override`) that lets claude.ai bypass canonical-resolution validation on an **override-eligible axis** when it encounters a net-new value. The entity write succeeds with the raw_value persisted verbatim; the net-new value is auto-queued to `taxonomy_overrides_queue` via `submission_path='override_flag'`. **Override is broad, not narrow** (Chris, 2026-05-15) — every override-flag axis can produce a queued canonical promotion; the flag is the **escape hatch** that keeps direct-write strict for normal writes but tractable for the long tail of new-coffee discovery. The flag exists precisely **because cascading-failure was the failure mode it prevents** — pre-override, claude.ai could not push an entity with a net-new value at all, breaking the workflow mid-write.

**Canonical-strictness spectrum** (Chris, 2026-05-15) — the per-axis policy choice is calibrated to the expected net-new rate for that axis:
- **Strict / static** (cultivar, terroir): Chris built comprehensive registries; net-new is rare (genuinely new cross-bred cultivar; region missing from the macro list). Strict-axis path only.
- **Self-owned** (brewer, filter, grinder): net-new only when Chris buys new equipment. Override-flag path (purchases happen mid-workflow).
- **Closed-objective** (roast level): Standardized canonical set; mostly about alignment, not extension. Not in the queue today.
- **Open / loose** (producer, roaster, signature_method, flavor, process modifiers): High net-new probability — producers and farms are too numerous to fully canonicalize; roasters are too numerous; signatures emerge constantly; flavor vocabulary is subjective. Producers + roasters are override-flag axes; signature_method is a queued addition; flavor + process modifiers remain outside the queue today.

_Avoid_: "override parameter" (under-specified), "bypass flag" (could mean any validation bypass), "escape hatch" (descriptive but not canonical), bare "override" (ambiguous against general programming meaning).

**find-or-create**:
The canonical-resolution write-path pattern every `push_*` and `patch_*` Tool runs for each canonical-axis field on the entity. Ordered steps for a single field: **(1) alias lookup first** — the `CanonicalLookup` bundle (built by `makeCanonicalLookup(names, aliases?)` in `lib/canonical-registry.ts`) is checked for an alias match (e.g. `"Geisha"` → `"Gesha"`); aliases resolve to a canonical without any further work; **(2) canonical check** — if no alias match, the input is checked against the canonical names directly (case-correcting via `canonicalize`); **(3) FK materialization** — if found canonically AND the axis is FK-resolved (cultivar / terroir), look for the canonical row, create it lazily with full metadata from the registry entry (`getCultivarEntry()` / `getTerroirEntry()`) if absent, FK-link the entity. Records provenance on the entity (`*_provenance` column flips `canonical` vs `auto_created`); `green_beans.canonicals_updated_at` timestamps the moment; **(4) NET-NEW branch** — if neither alias nor canonical resolves AND the axis is override-eligible, persist the raw_value verbatim + queue to `taxonomy_overrides_queue` for arbiter review; **(5) fail-fast** — if no override path is available (strict axes), the entity write is rejected and claude.ai recovers via `propose_canonical_addition`.

**Two flavors**:
- **FK-creating** (cultivar / terroir): canonical rows are materialized lazily on first reference, full metadata flows from the registry entry, FK-linked + provenance-tracked.
- **Text-only** (roaster / producer / brewer / filter / grinder): canonicalize-then-persist; no FK, no auto-created row. Override-eligible.

The pattern is **lazy by design** — canonical FK rows are only materialized when a write references them, never pre-populated speculatively. Shared cores (`validateCanonicalText` for text-only, `findOrCreate*` for FK) live in `lib/brew-import.ts` + `lib/canonical-registry.ts` to avoid re-implementing across axes.
_Avoid_: "upsert" (close but specifically write-with-update-on-conflict; find-or-create is read-then-conditional-create), "lookup-or-insert" (under-specified), "lazy canonical creation" (only covers the FK-creating half), "validate" (too generic — validation is a separate, broader concept).

**Canonical validation**:
The umbrella process every MCP write runs at push time — each canonical-axis field is resolved against its `CanonicalLookup` bundle and the outcome routes to one of three buckets: **resolves canonically** (write proceeds with canonical value), **resolves via alias** (write proceeds with the canonical target — raw input was just an alias for an already-known canonical), or **fails to resolve** (write either fails fast on strict axes or persists verbatim + queues to `taxonomy_overrides_queue` on override-eligible axes — the NET-NEW branch). Validation surface includes `isCanonical(v)`, `findClosest(v)`, `canonicalize(v)` (title-case canonical), `isResolvable(v)`, and `isOverridableValid(v)` used by UI save-gates. **Same validation logic runs in three places**: MCP push handlers (the canonical write surface), `/add` + `/edit` save paths (the legacy UI inputs, partially deprecated per the MCP-only input principle), and the patch route's per-field canonicalize blocks — single source of truth via the `makeCanonicalLookup(names, aliases?)` factory in `lib/canonical-registry.ts`.
_Avoid_: bare "validation" (too generic — canonical validation is specifically about canonical-registry alignment), "registry lookup" (lookup is one step within validation, not the whole), "canonical resolution" (close but resolution is the success path; validation includes the failure-routing decision).

**NET-NEW**:
Chris's verbal + memory-file shorthand for **the net-new-canonical branch of find-or-create** — the case where a write references a value that resolves neither canonically nor via alias. Triggers the override-flag path on override-eligible axes (entity persists verbatim with the canonical-promotion row landing in `taxonomy_overrides_queue` for arbiter review) or `propose_canonical_addition` on strict axes (entity write fails, queue row lands, claude.ai re-attempts the entity write after arbitration). NET-NEW writes are the **canonical-promotion entry point** — every queue row was a NET-NEW write at some moment. The arbiter's job on a queued NET-NEW entry is the **promote vs alias decision**: **promote** if the value is truly a new canonical taxonomy record (and at promotion time the full registry entry — 27 producer fields, 29 roaster fields, etc. — is populated, never left partial); **alias** if the value should resolve to an existing canonical (the alias map gets extended); **reject** if the value was drift / typo / not truly net-new; **duplicate** if it collapses to another pending queue row. Memory + followup files use the all-caps form `NET-NEW`; the glossary lemma matches.
_Avoid_: "new canonical" (under-specified — could mean a promoted-to-canonical), "missing canonical" (implies the registry SHOULD have it; NET-NEW is neutral about that), "unknown value" (too generic), bare "new" (overloaded).

**Bearer token auth**:
The long-lived `Authorization: Bearer <KEY>` auth path on the Latent MCP server — used by **MCP clients that don't speak OAuth**, principally Claude.ai's desktop app and Claude Code's local config. Keys are generated once via Supabase admin SQL into the `api_keys` table (`SUPABASE_SERVICE_ROLE_KEY` for the lookup), stored in the client's MCP config, and validated server-side on every call. Single Chris-owned api_keys row covers all bearer-auth clients; rotation = invalidate row, generate new, paste into client config. **Not a trust-tier distinction** — bearer-token and OAuth resolve to the same `user_id`, the same RLS scope, the same Tool surface; the split is purely about client protocol requirements. Bearer was the only auth path in **V1 sync** (the original MCP server ship in Sprint 2.3) because the desktop MCP client family only supported it; OAuth came in Sprint 3.0 specifically to unlock the web + mobile clients.
_Avoid_: "API key auth" (close — the literal mechanism is a bearer-token-API-key — but bearer token is the canonical MCP-spec phrasing), "static key", "long-lived key" (descriptive but not canonical), "service auth", "auth tier" (no tier distinction — bearer + OAuth carry equal trust).

**OAuth 2.1 + PKCE auth**:
The claude.ai web + mobile auth path on the Latent MCP server — added Sprint 3.0 (2026-05-03) specifically because the claude.ai web / mobile clients require OAuth; the desktop client's bearer-token path didn't extend to them. Uses MCP-spec discovery endpoints (`/.well-known/oauth-protected-resource` + `/.well-known/oauth-authorization-server`), a 5-minute auth code at `/api/mcp/authorize` (stored in `oauth_authorization_codes`, migration 043), an access token exchange at `/api/mcp/token`, and a `WWW-Authenticate` 401 trigger for re-auth. Static client credentials live in Vercel env (`MCP_OAUTH_CLIENT_ID` + `MCP_OAUTH_CLIENT_SECRET`). The single-tenant + single-user shape **collapses OAuth's heaviest costs to ~nothing** — no consent UI, no scopes, no identity surface — leaving only the spec-required discovery + token-exchange machinery. **The dominant auth path in active use** — Chris's primary input surface today is the claude.ai web + mobile app (mobile especially), so OAuth carries most production write traffic. Resolves to the same api_keys row as the bearer-token path, same trust, same Tool surface.
_Avoid_: bare "OAuth" (under-specified — version + PKCE matter), "web auth", "claude.ai auth" (descriptive but not canonical), "auth tier" (same reason as bearer-token entry).

**Roest API integration**:
The **external-device I/O surface** on the Latent MCP server — bidirectional integration with the Roest L200 Ultra ([roestcoffee.com/products/sample-roasters/l200-ultra](https://www.roestcoffee.com/products/sample-roasters/l200-ultra)), Chris's physical counterflow roaster. Six Tools span the surface: `push_roast_profile` (push a profile spec to the machine), `push_inventory` + `patch_inventory` (push and update inventory entries), `pull_roest_log` (pull one roast graph by ID — temperature / fan / power curves), `list_roest_logs` + `list_roest_inventory` (list available logs + inventory).

**The killer capability is the roast recipe push** — pre-integration, claude.ai handed Chris recipes as prose and Chris typed each V-set into the machine batch-by-batch; with V1a / V1b / V1c carrying minute differences between slots, the manual typing was error-prone and tedious enough that the integration was one of the highest-value MCP additions. With `push_roast_profile` live, claude.ai sets up the V-set AND writes the per-batch recipes into the machine in one step. **Reverse-engineered write surface**: Roest's published API documentation only covers read access; the write capability was discovered via a third-party GitHub project. Latent acts as a client to Roest's cloud API, brokering through credentials in Vercel env. Shipped 2026-05-06 (Roest API write Phase 1+2). One of the **only output exceptions** to the MCP server's primary-input direction (the other being `propose_doc_changes`); both write OUT of Latent rather than IN.

**Two distinctions to keep crisp**:
- **Roest L200 Ultra** is the **physical machine** (the device Chris roasts on, exclusively in counterflow mode); **Roest API** is the cloud surface the integration talks to. The MCP server is a client of the cloud, never directly connected to the machine.
- The integration is **not part of asymmetric write trust** — Roest writes are direct (no propose-then-apply), but the round-trip is mechanical (push a profile spec, the machine accepts it), not interpretive — there's no drift surface for an arbiter to manage.

**Adjacent equipment that is NOT integrated today**: the xBloom (manual one-recipe operation, no API need); kettle / brewer / scale / grinder (no API on any of them — all manual). Roest is the only piece of physical equipment in Chris's setup that exposes an API surface, and Latent uses all of it.
_Avoid_: bare "Roest integration" (under-specified — could mean machine, cloud, or MCP surface), "Roest sync" (close but integration is canonical), "external API integration" (too generic), conflating "L200 Ultra" with "Roest API".

## Relationships

- A **V-set** belongs to exactly one green-bean lot.
- A **V-set** contains 1–4 **batch slots** (typically 3, occasionally 4, rarely 1–2 for one-shot lots).
- A green-bean lot progresses through one or more **V-sets** until a reference roast emerges.
- Each **batch slot** corresponds to exactly one roast execution and exactly one Roest machine profile (same name everywhere).
- A later **V-set** (e.g. V2) is informed by the outcomes of the earlier V-set on the same lot.
- Each **V-set** has exactly one **experiment frame**, authored before any roasting happens.
- An **experiment frame** predicts outcomes at both the roast layer (what the curve will look like) and the cup layer (what the brew will taste like); these predictions get compared against actuals to produce two distinct deltas per batch slot.
- A **variable** becomes a **lever** post-hoc once cross-batch-slot or cross-V-set evidence shows it meaningfully affects the cup; if the same evidence shows no effect, it becomes a **non-factor** instead.
- An experiment frame's `levels_tested` enumerates the values a **variable** takes across batch slots (e.g. fast / medium / slow, or 240°C / 250°C / 260°C peak inlet).
- A **roast→cup trace** runs per batch slot — each slot has its own predicted-roast, actual-roast, roast-delta, predicted-cup-initial (on the recipe), updated-cup-prediction (post-roast), taste-for, actual-cup, and cup-delta.
- A V-set's **taste-for** for slot `n` is informed by what was tasted in the corresponding slot of prior V-sets on the same lot, plus the producer tasting notes, plus the adjustment being tested this round.
- All taste-based terms in this glossary are **single-palate** (Chris's). Producer tasting notes are an external ballpark anchor, not a target.
- Each V-set produces a **leading slot** (the V-set-scoped winner; lives in `experiments.winner`). The leading slot of the final V-set is typically (but not always) promoted to the lot-level **reference roast** at close-out.
- A **reference roast** emerges from a V-set when the cup matches expectations + diminishing returns set in, and is typically locked via one final **control experiment** (which replicates the prior V-set's leading slot with two slight adjustments to confirm).
- A **reference cup** is the xBloom day-7 pour-over of the reference roast — one cup, one method, one row.
- An **optimized brew** is dialed in *after* the reference cup, by feeding the reference roast into the brewing-side workflow (same as a purchased roasted bean).
- The end target of the full roast→cup trace is the **optimized brew**, not the reference cup. Post-hoc attribution flows backward: optimized brew → reference cup → reference roast → variables / levers / non-factors.
- Green-bean inventory is finite: each lot supports a bounded number of V-sets (typically ~10 100g batches per pound), making the trace time-constrained. Faster lessons-learned compounding = fewer V-sets needed to reach a reference roast.
- A **reference roast** has a **roasted bean characteristic** that the brewing-side workflow reads when dialing in the optimized brew.
- The "Roast Character" mini-card on the resolved page surfaces three attributes side-by-side: **primary lever**, **acceptable roast window** (roast-deviation latitude), and **brewing tolerance** (brew-deviation latitude). They're three different facets, not three names for the same thing.
- Chris's roasting/brewing style **deprioritizes brewing tolerance** in favor of expressiveness — he intentionally pushes extremes (very light + Full Expression on one side, very dark + Suppression on the other), so a low-tolerance lot is acceptable provided it doesn't fully collapse.
- A green-bean lot moves through lifecycle states in this order: **In inventory** → **Waiting for next roast** → **Waiting for next cupping** → (loop back to Waiting for next roast for V_{n+1}, or progress to) **Resolved**.
- The loop between Waiting for next roast and Waiting for next cupping repeats N times (one per V-set) until a reference roast is declared.
- The transition from a winning cupping to Resolved compresses two sub-events: (1) reference roast designation immediately after a winning xBloom cupping, (2) optimized-brew dial-in by handing the reference roast into the brewing-side workflow (same flow as a purchased roasted bean). Both must complete before the lot is marked Resolved.
- Concurrent multi-lot mode is the normal operating state: Chris typically has several lots in different lifecycle states simultaneously, visible side-by-side on the /green index.
- **Division of labor across the workflow**: Chris does all physical work (roast, cup, taste, judge); **claude.ai** designs experiment frames, writes hypotheses, and inserts/updates app rows via the MCP server; the **Latent app** archives the resulting state. claude.ai is the canonical author for every roasting-side entity.
- A **lot** is uniquely identified by `green_lot_id`; same producer + same cultivar across two purchases = two distinct lots that behave and taste differently (agricultural variability).
- A lot is **typically single-cultivar single-origin**, but green-bean SKUs are occasionally sold as **incoming blends** (e.g. Libertad: Bourbon + Caturra from the same processing station). The current schema (one cultivar / terroir per `green_beans` row) is lossy for these — known limitation.
- **Intentional roasted-blending** (WBC-style: same bean roasted three ways and blended, or two varieties blended) is NOT in current scope but is on the long-term roadmap as Chris's interests move toward competition-style work.
- A resolved lot produces both **lot-specific learnings** (backward-looking audit-trail) and **carry-forward learnings** (forward-looking compounding-knowledge primitive). Two cards, two roles, two audiences.
- **Carry-forward learnings are the compounding-knowledge primitive** — claude.ai reads them when designing V1 on a new lot with overlapping cultivar/terroir/process. Faster compounding = fewer V-sets needed to reach a reference roast on future lots. This is the *whole point* of Latent as a journal.
- **Non-intervention principle**: Chris does not hand-author or edit any roasting-side records. claude.ai writes via MCP based on the cupping/roast trace; Chris's role is physical execution + judgment. Manual intervention is reserved for "glaringly wrong" cases (and even then he asks claude.ai to fix it rather than editing directly). The goal is a self-sustaining knowledge system.
- **Underdev signal** and **overdev signal** key off **development** (whole-curve completeness, including WB→Gnd Agtron uniformity), not classical post-FC time alone.
- The xBloom gate exists precisely **because cup-side signals must be isolated from brewing variance** — bowl cupping's variance smuggled too much brewing-tolerance noise into what looked like cup-side observations.
- **Diagnostic signals** (underdev / overdev) describe *failure modes* — what goes wrong when development is pushed off-target. **Character descriptors** (aromatic / structural / rest behavior) describe *what the cup is* when roasted well. Two different jobs, two different homes.
- Every brew names exactly one **extraction strategy**, drawn from a strict 6-value canonical enum. The strategy is declared at the Coffee Brief Step 1d *before* equipment, grind, temp, or pour structure are decided — those parameters fall out of the strategy choice.
- **Extraction strategy is intent-defined, not mechanics-defined.** Two brews with identical recipes can name different strategies if the intent differs (Suppression vs Clarity-First on the same coarse-grind / low-temp / low-agitation recipe). Strategy reclassification across schema versions (e.g. the v8.4 Hybrid promotion that moved Sebastian Ramirez White Honey + Janson 1010 from `Balanced Intensity + Immersion modifier` to `Hybrid (Sequential)` without changing the recipe) is therefore a frame change, not a recipe change.
- The 6 strategies split 5+1: five **intensity** strategies (one logic running throughout the brew) + one **structure** strategy (`Hybrid`, where phase boundaries change the brew's mode mid-stream). When the brewer changes mode (immersion ↔ percolation, or pours have explicitly different sensory jobs) the strategy is Hybrid; otherwise pick from the five intensity strategies.
- The **two-axis framework** is a deliberate **learning-stage compression** of the WBC five-axis taxonomy, chosen because (a) many WBC subtypes are not in Chris's active practice yet, (b) the compression keeps the canonical taxonomy + picker surface tractable, and (c) the modifier system gives extensibility without committing to first-class status. The shape is expected to evolve, not to remain at 2 axes forever.
- A brew may carry zero or more **modifiers**; modifiers are stackable and operate on mechanical axes orthogonal to the chosen extraction strategy. Default empty — a "no modifiers" answer at Step 1d is a positive signal that modifiers were considered.
- An Axis 2 **modifier** can be **promoted** to a first-class Axis 1 **extraction strategy** when sustained usage + cross-coffee pattern + a coherent distinct intent justify the schema cost. Promotion churns existing brew records (reclassification at promotion time); the bar is deliberately high.
- The v8.4 Hybrid promotion (Immersion modifier → Hybrid strategy) is the canonical promotion precedent — driven by SWORKS Bottomless Dripper office usage reaching threshold plus selective Hario Switch home use. The three pre-promotion brews (Sebastian Ramirez White Honey, Janson 1010, Finca La Reserva) reclassified to Hybrid (Sequential) at promotion without recipe changes.
- Cross-cutting WBC moves not promoted to canonical modifiers (water strength / agitation taper / filter behavior / pre-brew conditioning / time-distribution templates beyond Role-Based Pulse) persist via free-text `strategy_notes` on the brew row and live as doc-layer playbook patterns in [docs/brewing/wbc-reference.md](../../docs/brewing/wbc-reference.md).
- WBC's **Physical System** axis (Structural Systems family) is explicitly out of scope — blend-engineering, layered beds, dual-chamber extraction don't apply to Latent's single-origin home brewing context.
- "Promotion" is overloaded across roasting and brewing — the roasting-side **variable → lever** promotion is post-hoc per-V-set evidence-driven; the brewing-side **modifier → strategy** promotion is sustained-usage / schema-evolution-driven. Distinct mechanisms, distinct domains, same word.
- **Hybrid is the only extraction strategy with a mandatory second-level pick** (`hybrid_subform`, 5-value enum). The other 5 strategies are self-contained — their name carries the recipe intent. Hybrid is a pointer that becomes meaningful only once the sub-form names what kind of structural compositing the brew is doing.
- Hybrid sits on a different axis from the other 5 strategies: **structural composition** (phase boundaries change brew mode mid-stream) rather than **extraction intensity** (one logic running throughout). This is why Hybrid's labels are about *kinds of compositing* rather than *amounts of extraction*.
- The Phase-Mapped sub-form ties each pour to a cup-side attribute it primarily extracts; the canonical mapping is bloom → aromatics, Pour 1 → acidity, Pour 2 → sweetness, last pour → finish. The brewer's per-phase mechanics (valve / agitation / flow) are then tuned to emphasize the targeted attribute.
- Selective Bloom Hybrid and the Output Selection modifier both involve treating a fraction of the extraction separately, but the architectural split is intentional: **Selective Bloom = role assignment** (the bloom participates differently in the cup design), **Output Selection = fraction discarding** (kept some, threw some away). The wbc-reference.md doc explicitly preserves this split.
- Every **phase** in a brew carries **two parallel labels** (formal split, 2026-05-15): a **mechanical role** (what the brewer is doing — which WBC axis is engaged) plus a **cup-side target** (which sensory attribute the phase is meant to deliver). The two are orthogonal and both load-bearing during Phase-Mapped Hybrid + Role-Based Pulse design.
- The mechanical role per phase **names which WBC foundational control axis is being engaged** — extraction-intensity (grind / temp / ratio), time-distribution (pulse / hold / drain), physical-system (valve / immersion / percolation), or external-control (Melodrip / chilling ball / kettle off-base). This is the through-line that ties Latent's per-phase recipe vocabulary back to the WBC 5-axis lineage.
- BREWING.md's prior compressed phrasing `saturation / body / clarity / finish` (used in both Phase-Mapped and Role-Based Pulse definitions) **collapsed mechanical role and cup-side target into one mixed vocabulary** — it's been formally retired in favor of the two-label split. BREWING.md will need a doc-edit pass to update both definitions; flagged for sprint.
- **Cup-side targets use the fixed 5-attribute set** (`aroma / attack / mid-palate / body / finish`) — same vocabulary across recipe design, Step 3 iteration, and Step 4 archive output. Locked 2026-05-15.
- **Phase → cup-side-target pairing is soft.** Typical defaults exist (bloom → aroma, last pour → finish) but recipes vary in pour count (single-pour through 5+) and a phase often touches multiple attributes; design-time pairings are aspirations, not guarantees. Number of pours is itself a lever — more pours = more extraction into the cup, fewer pours = less.
- Bloom and double-bloom variants are explicit **future-scope experiments**, not currently in routine practice. They're queued in `wbc-reference.md` Tier 1 experiments but not yet shaping the canonical phase vocabulary.
- The **Coffee Brief** is the brewing-side analog of the roasting-side **experiment frame** — both are pre-execution, claude.ai-authored design artifacts that lock intent before physical work happens. Differences are real and load-bearing: Coffee Brief covers one brew (cheap to re-do — re-brew from the same bag), experiment frame covers one V-set of typically 3 batch slots (expensive — burns ~10% of green-bean inventory per V-set). Coffee Brief has an explicit confirmation gate ("yep go for it"); experiment frame is typically implicit (claude.ai writes, Chris doesn't push back). Both are claude.ai-authored on Chris's behalf — neither is hand-edited at runtime.
- The **brewing iteration loop** is the brewing-side analog of the roasting V-set cycle, but **simpler and sequential**: one recipe per turn, ~3 brews average, no parallel batch slots, cup-side feedback drives recipe adjustment. **Brewing only touches the last segment of the cup pipeline** — the bean is fixed input from the roaster, only the brewing recipe varies; roasting touches the full chain (green selection → roast recipe → cupping → optimized brew).
- The **end-state** of the brewing iteration loop is the **reference brew** (covered in next round). Like the roasting reference roast, it's not designated via a control experiment — it's recognized when iteration deltas flatten and the cup matches the targeted profile. One-shot successes get a single confirmatory brew before locking.
- **Diminishing returns** is the canonical iteration-stop signal on both sides — same word, same shape. On the roasting side it triggers reference-roast designation; on the brewing side it terminates the iteration loop and triggers reference-brew commit.
- **claude.ai is the canonical input author across the entire Latent workflow** — explicitly stated by Chris: "All of the things that end up inside the Latent app are not pre-written by me, not edited by me, not drafted by me." This applies to brewing-side workflow now (Coffee Brief authoring, Step 4 resolved-brew sync) the same way it already applies to roasting (per [feedback_mcp_only_input.md](~/.claude/projects/-Users-chrismccann-latent-coffee/memory/feedback_mcp_only_input.md)).
- **CONTEXT.md is registered as a Resource in the Latent MCP server** ([lib/mcp/docs.ts](../../lib/mcp/docs.ts) at `docs://context.md`) precisely because the claude.ai input side needs to be familiar with the same vocabulary the codebase uses — Chris stated this directly during 2026-05-15 grilling. Glossary integrity is workflow-load-bearing, not just developer-facing documentation.
- **Brewing iteration budget is bound by roasted-bean inventory** — typical purchase is 125-500g of roasted coffee, allowing ~8-30 physical brews per bag. The ~3-brew average iteration loop leaves headroom but not infinite — narrower than the roasting side's ~10-V-set budget per green lot. Drives the brewing workflow's bias toward one-shot success and tight iteration.
- Brewing is **subjective like roasting** — there's no objective "best" brew, only the recipe Chris commits to when iteration deltas flatten. Same single-palate framing as the roasting side.
- The brewing iteration loop **converges within a strategy zone, not across strategies**. A wrong-zone strategy choice at Coffee Brief Step 1d traps the loop in suboptimal territory regardless of how many turns it runs — iteration tightens within a strategy, it does not transit between strategies. **Canonical example: the Jeferson Motta anaerobic-washed Gesha** — pre-framework, Chris ran clarity-first iteration to no avail (sour / flat / grassy) before realizing Full Expression was the right zone. Tasting the coffee at Moonwake (where it was roasted) surfaced the candied strawberry / hibiscus profile that Full Expression unlocked. This is why the Coffee Brief confirmation gate matters: it's the only opportunity to catch a wrong-zone strategy before burning iteration budget.
- **Reference brew (purchased context) and optimized brew (self-roasted context) are the same artifact-shape** — same brewing iteration loop, same Step 4 resolved-brew row, same canonical-validation pass. The naming distinction reflects which side of the pipeline you're looking from: reference brew is the terminal output for a coffee that started as a purchased roasted bean; optimized brew is the terminal output for a coffee that started as a green lot, was roasted to a reference roast, and then handed into the brewing workflow.
- **The xBloom reference cup and the optimized brew are intentionally separate cup-tasting moments on the self-roasted pipeline**. Reference cup is **mechanically constrained** (same xBloom recipe every time — designed to eliminate brewing-side variance so cup-side signals can be isolated for picking the reference roast). Optimized brew is **mechanically unconstrained** (full lever range — real dripper, real filter, full strategy + modifier toolbox — designed to make the bean shine). They do different jobs: reference cup is calibration, optimized brew is consumption-condition.
- A **resolved** state applies to entities on both sides of the workflow: a green-bean lot is **resolved** when reference roast + optimized brew are both locked (roasting lifecycle terminal state); a brew row is **resolved** when it has been written via Step 4 and synced to the app. Same word, different scope — lot-level on the roasting side, per-row on the brewing side.
- **Latent records only the resolved brew, not the iteration trace.** Intermediate brews + tasting deltas + adjustment reasoning live in the claude.ai conversation thread. This is asymmetric with the roasting side (which records every batch slot within a V-set) and is a flagged ambiguity Chris is open to reconsidering.
- **The geography mental model: strategy = country, sub-form / modifier = state, iteration target = city.** Strategy choice picks the country; Hybrid sub-form (when applicable) and modifier stack pick the state inside that country; the iteration loop dials within state/city granularity. Pre-Coffee-Brief, Chris frequently ended up in the wrong country (Clarity-First when the coffee needed Full Expression) and could only iterate within that wrong country. Post-Coffee-Brief, the country is usually right by Step 1d and iteration is purely state/city dialing.
- **The Coffee Brief subsumed Extraction Confirmed's original purpose**. Pre-Latent, Chris added the `extraction_confirmed` field to catch wrong-zone strategy divergence after the fact (Jeferson Motta lesson). Today, prior-archive lookup + WBC corpus check + roaster brew guide cross-check + the BREWING.md doc-based reasoning catch wrong-zone calls at Step 1d, *before* iteration starts. Extraction Confirmed remains useful when it does fire — that signal means the upstream-prevention layer missed something specific worth carrying forward — but it's expected to fire rarely.
- **Extraction Confirmed and Modifiers Confirmed are asymmetric for historical-vs-experimental reasons**, not binary-vs-interpretive reasons. Extraction Confirmed is a **legacy field** whose original wrong-zone-gut-check role has shifted upstream into the Coffee Brief; Modifiers Confirmed is **active experimental territory** Chris is leaning into more, since modifiers are slight-variation add-ons whose effect needs per-brew validation. Different histories drive different populate-rules.
- **Three signal sources feed the Coffee Brief**: **variety signal** (from cultivar genetics, read at Step 1c), **process signal** (from processing intervention depth, read at Step 1b via the Process/Variety Signal table), and **roaster signal** / **house style** (from the roaster's persistent extraction direction + published brew guide). All three converge at Step 1d when the extraction strategy is proposed.
- **Default = variety-driven**; **signal override** is judgment-based at Step 1c, not strict precedence. Three reliable override patterns to remember: (1) intervention-heavy process overrides variety (anoxic naturals, heavy co-ferments, Moonwake Lord Voldemort); (2) strong roaster house style overrides variety + process (DAK, Sey, Picolot Garrido naturals); (3) transparency-driven variety overrides mild process (Sydra TyOxidator, washed Geshas).
- **Strong house styles name specific strategy zones**, not generic preference strength. Sey is Extraction-Push-leaning (light + high extraction + low agitation = clean but pushed); DAK is Full Expression (push to get the cup out); Picolot raised-bed Garrido naturals are Full Expression with fast/fast/slow valve discipline. The override target is encoded in the roaster's strategy tag in [docs/brewing/roasters.md](../../docs/brewing/roasters.md).
- **The heaviest-process-wins-everything pattern is canonical**: Moonwake Lord Voldemort (yeast-inoculated Ombligon, Colombia) is the reference example where the process is intervention-heavy enough that it overrides cultivar + terroir + the roaster's own published brew guide. When a coffee is "its own beast," all other signals defer to the process-driven extraction strategy choice.
- **The WBC Thermal Systems family maps to four distinct surfaces in Latent**: the **Inverted Temperature Staging** modifier (low→high temp across phases), the **Aroma Capture** modifier (chilling-ball early-phase volatile lock-in), the **Cooling-Curve Design** Named Consideration (evaluate / serve at a non-default peak temp), and the **Hybrid (Temperature-Staged)** sub-form (phase boundary coincides with temp change). All four engage temperature as a primary lever but at different architectural layers (modifier / consideration / sub-form). When a brief lands in Thermal Systems territory, the right surface depends on whether the temperature move is layered on top of a strategy (modifier), informs evaluation/service timing (consideration), or defines phase structure (sub-form).
- **Cooling-Curve Target operates at three moments** (brewing-time / evaluation-time / service-time), only two of which intersect the Latent data model. Service-time temp management (PC-fan rapid-cool to ~55°C before pouring for guests) is a real Chris-side discipline but is not logged on the brew row today.
- **Aroma Capture's observed cooling-arc artifact shifts peak warmer** (~48-50°C from a non-modifier ~45°C baseline) — the *inverse* direction from cool-window-dependent coffees. Aroma Capture is therefore the wrong tool for coffees with established cool-window dependencies (El Paraíso thermal-shock, Garrido naturals, etc.). The cooling-arc artifact was first observed on Pepe Jijón Sidra Wave Hybrid (2026-05-07).
- **`process_dominant` is an aggregation trust signal**, not a Step 1d signal-arbitration reflection. It marks brews whose learnings should NOT aggregate to cultivar / terroir / roaster pages because the process is too dominant for any non-process generalization to apply. Tightly correlated in practice with process-signal-overrode-at-Step-1d but conceptually a different surface.
- **The compounding-knowledge motivation predates the Latent app**: the precursor claude.ai-local-app that became Latent pulled three context blocks per brew (roaster / terroir / cultivar) specifically so cup-side learnings could aggregate into per-axis lesson sheets — the proto-form of today's `/cultivars`, `/terroirs`, and `/roasters` aggregation surfaces. `process_dominant` was invented to keep heavy-process brews from skewing those aggregations.
- **Roasting has no equivalent process-dominant flag** today, even though Lord-Voldemort-tier green-bean lots could in principle skew the carry-forward learnings the same way. Open follow-up: does roasting need a `process_dominant` analog on `green_beans` or `roast_learnings`? Or is the per-cultivar / per-terroir aggregation on the roasting side robust enough at current corpus sizes that it's not load-bearing?
- **Role separation has four parties**, all treated as first-class actors with shared language: **claude.ai** (input — the `claude.ai/projects` surface, authors every entity + every doc proposal), **Claude Code** (builder + arbiter — same model, two operational modes), **Latent** (the system itself — Next.js + Postgres + the MCP server, source of truth for state), **Chris** (physical execution — roast / brew / cup / taste / judge, no record authoring). The MCP-only input principle enforces the boundary between input and physical execution; the builder/arbiter boundary is internal to Claude Code.
- **The Latent MCP server is the substrate of role separation** — without it, the input surface would be the deprecated `/add` forms and the role boundary would erode. It exposes Tools (writes + Tool-shaped reads) + Resources (URI-addressable read-only catalog) under one HTTP transport on the same Vercel deployment as the app.
- **Single-tenant + single-user is permanent** (Chris, 2026-05-15): "even in the long, long term, there's nothing more than just me." No multi-tenancy plan, ever. Multi-actor framing (claude.ai + Claude Code + Latent + Chris as four parties) is the only "multi-user" sense the system has.
- **claude.ai is the dominant active client of the Latent MCP server** via the web + mobile apps (OAuth 2.1 + PKCE auth path); Claude Code is the secondary client (bearer-token auth path). The auth path split is **client-protocol-driven, not trust-tier-driven** — both paths resolve to the same `user_id`, same RLS scope, same Tool surface.
- **Tool and Resource have an asymmetric discoverability surface** in claude.ai's MCP client — Tools are model-callable, Resources are catalog-only. The **dual-surface pattern** (same payload as both a Tool and a Resource) exists because critical reads need to be model-callable; `read_canonical` + `canonicals://{axis}` + `read_doc` + `docs://*` are the canonical instances.
- **Asymmetric write trust has two tiers + one variant**: direct write (canonical-registry field-picking) trusts claude.ai fully; propose-then-apply (free-form prose mutations) routes through `doc_proposals` for Claude Code arbitration; canonical-promotion writes (net-new taxonomy values) route through `taxonomy_overrides_queue` for the same arbiter. The asymmetry is the load-bearing call of SYNC_V2 Decision #5.
- **The arbiter procedure walks both queue types in one session** — there's one trigger phrase ("process pending arbitration") and one playbook (ARBITER.md), with axis grouping per queue type. Future curation surfaces (null-field reviews, missing-entity flags) can attach to the same trigger without changing the procedure shape.
- **Drift (slow-motion canonical naming inconsistency) and category bloat (over-promotion of canonical entries)** are paired failure modes that asymmetric write trust + the arbiter procedure prevent. State bloat is the doc-layer analog (un-curated edits accumulating in living docs). All three failure modes were experienced firsthand in Chris's pre-Latent spreadsheet workflow, compounded once his EA Belle joined the loop, and motivated the MCP-only input principle.
- **The MCP-only input principle has paired motivations**: (1) opportunity — every claude.ai input flows through `propose_doc_changes` and canonical-validation, contributing to the System self-improvement loop; (2) constraint — the schema complexity has outgrown human-tractable input. Going back to manual input is functionally not viable.
- **find-or-create starts with alias lookup, not canonical lookup** — the first step is `CanonicalLookup`'s alias-resolution map (e.g. "Geisha" → "Gesha"). Only after alias resolution fails does the lookup check canonical names directly. NET-NEW is the branch hit when both resolutions fail.
- **Promotion at arbiter-time always carries a full canonical entry** — when Chris-as-arbiter promotes a NET-NEW queue row to canonical, the full rich registry entry (27 producer fields, 29 roaster fields, etc.) gets populated at that moment, not partial-now-fill-in-later. The discipline applies across all rich-registry axes; the promotion moment is the only one where the entry's context is fresh.
- **Cross-system consistency is the meta-rule that makes everything else work** — claude.ai, Claude Code, and Latent must share vocabulary + canonical taxonomy + operational rules. Sync is maintained by live Resource reads + validation-on-push + `propose_doc_changes` + the arbiter procedure. CONTEXT.md exists, is a Resource, and grows via `/grill-with-docs` sessions specifically because the principle needs a shared-language artifact to anchor on.
- **The Roest API integration is the only external-device surface** on the Latent MCP server today. Six Tools span read (logs / inventory) + write (recipes / inventory). The roast recipe push capability is the killer feature — pre-integration, claude.ai handed Chris recipes as prose and Chris manual-typed per-batch (V1a / V1b / V1c with minute differences); post-integration, claude.ai writes them straight into the machine. Roest's published API only covers read; write was reverse-engineered via a third-party GitHub project. xBloom + kettle + brewer + grinder are all manual / no-API.
- **No real-time push from app → claude.ai is planned, ever.** Roest profiles support drop-on-bean-temperature with drop rules (`drop_rule_if_fast` + `drop_rule_if_slow`); the machine handles mid-roast decision logic locally, claude.ai doesn't need real-time data pulling.

## Flagged ambiguities

- "Experiment set" was Chris's prior term for the same concept (used interchangeably during the transition to V notation). Resolved: **V-set** is canonical; the V prefix is already encoded in the spreadsheet labels, Roest profile names, and DB identifiers.
- Initial grilling attempt conflated **variable** and **lever** into one term. Resolved: they're distinct — variable is the design-time noun (pre-judgment about whether it matters), lever is the post-hoc promotion (variable that turned out to affect the cup), and **non-factor** names a variable that was tested but didn't matter.
- "Reference cup" was initially treated as the umbrella for "everything tasted from the reference roast". Resolved: it's specifically the xBloom day-7 pour-over cupping; the optimized pour-over recipe is the **optimized brew** (separate entry). Three distinct things: reference roast (the bean state) → reference cup (the xBloom evaluation) → optimized brew (the consumption-condition cup).
- "Best roast" / "winning roast" naming was rejected in favor of **reference roast** because tasting is subjective and there is no objective best — "reference" reflects the calibration role this roast plays for the lot.
- "Elasticity" was previously a single concept conflating roast-side latitude (range of acceptable lever values) and brew-side latitude (how the cup holds up under brewing push/pull). Resolved: split into **acceptable roast window** (roast deviation) and **brewing tolerance** (brew deviation). The `roast_learnings.elasticity` column should be renamed to `brewing_tolerance` and the `roast_window` column relabeled in UI as `acceptable_roast_window` — both are open follow-ups.
- "Lot" cleanly handles single-cultivar single-origin purchases but is lossy for **incoming blends** (a single green-bean SKU sold as a 2-cultivar mix from the same processing station — e.g. Libertad Bourbon + Caturra). **Roasted blends** (multiple lots roasted separately then combined) aren't modeled at all yet — future schema concern if WBC-style work enters scope.
- **Carry-forward learnings is missing a terroir-takeaway axis.** Chris articulated four axes in his mental model (cultivar / terroir / general / starting-hypothesis); the schema today only has three (cultivar / general / starting-hypothesis).
